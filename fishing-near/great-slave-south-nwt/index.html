Fishing in Great Slave Lake & Southern NWT

Great Slave Lake, Hay River, Fort Smith, Slave River, Taltson River, and the productive waters of southern NWT.

1. Great Slave Lake (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Hay River / Fort Providence area

Great Slave Lake is the deepest lake in North America. The west basin near Hay River offers excellent lake trout, northern pike, and Arctic grayling fishing. Lake trout in the 10-20 pound range are common. Shallow bays hold good pike populations.

2. Slave River (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Fort Smith area

The Slave River offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing flowing from Lake Athabasca into Great Slave Lake. Walleye in the 2-5 pound range are common, with larger fish possible. The river is also famous for world-class whitewater kayaking.

3. Hay River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike, Walleye)

Hay River area

The Hay River offers walleye, northern pike, and Arctic grayling fishing flowing into Great Slave Lake. The river is accessible from the town of Hay River and provides good shore and boat fishing.

4. Taltson River (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Taltson River area, east of Great Slave Lake

The Taltson River offers excellent northern pike and walleye fishing in a remote wilderness setting. Pike in the 5-15 pound range are common. Accessible by boat from Great Slave Lake or float plane.

5. Little Buffalo River (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Little Buffalo River area, near Fort Resolution

The Little Buffalo River offers northern pike and walleye fishing flowing into Great Slave Lake near Fort Resolution. A productive river in a scenic setting.

6. Buffalo Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Buffalo Lake area, near Fort Smith

Buffalo Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing in southern NWT near Fort Smith. A productive lake with good fish populations in the Slave River watershed.

7. Caribou River (NWT)

Caribou River area, near Great Slave Lake

The Caribou River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing flowing into Great Slave Lake. A productive river with good fish populations.

8. Jean Marie River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Jean Marie River area, Mackenzie Valley

The Jean Marie River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing near the Mackenzie River in southern NWT.

9. Trout River (NWT)

Trout River area, south of Great Slave Lake

The Trout River offers Arctic grayling and walleye fishing in southern NWT near the Alberta border. A productive river with good fish populations.

10. Kakisa River (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Kakisa River area, south of Great Slave Lake

The Kakisa River offers walleye and northern pike fishing flowing into the Mackenzie River near the community of Kakisa. The river features the spectacular Lady Evelyn Falls.

11. Fort Resolution Area (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Fort Resolution area, Great Slave Lake

The Fort Resolution area on Great Slave Lake offers lake trout, northern pike, and Arctic grayling fishing from this historic community on the lake's south shore.

12. Pine Point Area (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Pine Point area, Great Slave Lake south shore

The Pine Point area on Great Slave Lake's south shore offers excellent lake trout and northern pike fishing near the former mining community.

13. West Channel (Great Slave Lake)

West Channel area, near Hay River

The West Channel of the Mackenzie River outflow from Great Slave Lake offers northern pike, walleye, and Arctic grayling fishing near Hay River.

14. Great Slave Lake (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

North Arm area, near Yellowknife

The North Arm of Great Slave Lake offers lake trout, northern pike, and Arctic grayling fishing near the Yellowknife River delta.

15. Fort Smith Area Lakes (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Fort Smith area

The many small lakes around Fort Smith offer northern pike and walleye fishing with easy access from the community. Ideal for family fishing outings.

16. Salt River (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Salt River area, near Fort Smith

The Salt River offers northern pike and walleye fishing near Fort Smith in southern NWT. The river is known for its unique salt plains along its banks.

17. Wood Buffalo National Park (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Wood Buffalo National Park, southern NWT

The NWT portion of Wood Buffalo National Park offers remote northern pike and walleye fishing in pristine wilderness. The park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

18. Peace River (NWT reach)

Peace River area, near the Alberta border

The Peace River forms part of the southern NWT border and offers northern pike and walleye fishing near its confluence with the Slave River.

19. Tsu Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Tsu Lake area, east of Great Slave Lake

Tsu Lake offers lake trout and northern pike fishing east of Great Slave Lake in the Taltson River watershed.

20. Deskenatlata Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Deskenatlata Lake area, near Taltson River

Deskenatlata Lake offers lake trout and northern pike fishing in the Taltson River system east of Great Slave Lake.

21. Tathlina Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Tathlina Lake area, south of Great Slave Lake

Tathlina Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing south of Great Slave Lake in the Hay River watershed.

22. Alexandra Falls (Hay River)

Hay River area, near Enterprise

The Hay River near Alexandra Falls offers walleye and northern pike fishing in a scenic river setting near the spectacular 33-metre waterfall.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at nwtfishing.ca. Non-residents require a separate NWT fishing licence.
  • Fish Great Slave Lake -- Great Slave Lake offers world-class lake trout fishing accessible from Yellowknife, Hay River, and Fort Resolution.
  • Try the Slave River -- The Slave River near Fort Smith offers excellent walleye fishing with easy road access.
  • Book a lodge -- Several lodges on Great Slave Lake offer guided fishing packages with boats and equipment.
  • Watch for bison -- The Fort Smith area has a free-roaming wood bison population. Give them plenty of space.
