Fishing on Manitoba's Hudson Bay Coast

The Churchill River and Hudson Bay coast offer some of the most remote fishing in Manitoba — Arctic char, brook trout, and the unique experience of fishing in polar bear country.

The Hudson Bay coast of Manitoba is a land apart. The town of Churchill, known worldwide for its polar bears and beluga whales, also offers unique fishing opportunities. The Churchill River and the dozens of streams flowing into Hudson Bay host runs of Arctic char and sea-run brook trout. This is not a destination for casual anglers — travel is expensive and logistics are complex — but for those who make the journey, the fishing is unforgettable.

1. Churchill River (Arctic Char, Brook Trout & Northern Pike)

Churchill area, 1,000 km north of Winnipeg

The Churchill River is the largest river system flowing into Hudson Bay in Manitoba. The river hosts a significant run of Arctic char, with fish averaging 4-8 pounds. The river also holds brook trout and northern pike. The Churchill River near the town of Churchill offers the most accessible fishing in the region. Guided trips are available through local outfitters. The char run occurs in July and August. The river's tidal estuary also offers excellent beluga whale viewing — thousands of belugas gather here each summer.

2. Seal River (Arctic Char & Brook Trout)

150 km north of Churchill, fly-in only

The Seal River, flowing into Hudson Bay north of Churchill, offers exceptional Arctic char and brook trout fishing in a pristine subarctic setting. The river's clear, cold water and classic pool-riffle structure provide ideal habitat. The char run is strong, with fish averaging 4-8 pounds. The Seal River is fly-in only, accessed through lodges that operate on the river. The fishing is strictly catch-and-release. This is a destination for serious Arctic char anglers.

3. Broad River (Brook Trout & Arctic Char)

Near Churchill

The Broad River, south of Churchill, offers fishing for brook trout and Arctic char. The river's lower reaches near Hudson Bay hold sea-run brook trout that average 2-4 pounds. The char run is smaller than in the Churchill or Seal rivers but still provides excellent fishing. The Broad River is accessible by boat from Churchill or by all-terrain vehicle.

4. Little Churchill River (Brook Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Churchill

The Little Churchill River offers good fishing for brook trout and northern pike. The river is smaller and more intimate than the main Churchill River. The brook trout are primarily sea-run, averaging 1-3 pounds. Northern pike are present throughout the river system. The Little Churchill is a good option for anglers based in Churchill looking for a day trip.

5. Hudson Bay Coast Streams (Sea-Run Brook Trout)

Various locations along the coast

The numerous small streams flowing into Hudson Bay along Manitoba's coast offer excellent fishing for sea-run brook trout. These fish migrate into the bay to feed and return to freshwater larger and more powerful than their landlocked cousins. Sea-run brook trout in the 2-5 pound range are common. These streams are remote and require boat or ATV access from Churchill.

6. Kasmere Lake & River (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

The Kasmere Lake and River system, located in northwestern Manitoba near the Nunavut border, offers fishing for lake trout and northern pike in a remote wilderness setting. The lake holds lake trout that average 5-12 pounds. Northern pike in the 5-15 pound range are common. The area is fly-in only and is accessed through lodges and outfitters.

7. Southern Indian Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike & Walleye)

North-central Manitoba, fly-in or remote road access

Southern Indian Lake is one of the largest lakes in Manitoba and offers excellent fishing for lake trout, northern pike, walleye, and Arctic grayling. The lake's vast size and diverse habitat provide outstanding fishing opportunities. Lake trout in the 5-15 pound range are common. The lake has several lodges and is accessible by float plane or by a rough road from Thompson.

8. Nueltin Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

Nueltin Lake, straddling the Manitoba-Nunavut border, offers trophy lake trout and northern pike fishing. The lake is large, deep, and incredibly clear. Lake trout exceeding 30 pounds are caught here. Northern pike in the 10-20 pound range are common. The lake is fly-in only and has several remote lodges. This is one of the premier trophy fishing destinations in Manitoba.

9. Caribou River (Arctic Char)

Near Churchill

The Caribou River, flowing into Hudson Bay east of Churchill, offers Arctic char fishing in a remote setting. The char run in the Caribou River is smaller than in the main Churchill River but offers quality fish in a less-pressured environment. The river is accessible by boat from Churchill.

10. Churchill Town Shoreline (Beluga Whale Watching & Shore Fishing)

Churchill townsite

The shoreline along the Churchill townsite offers shore fishing for Arctic char and brook trout, as well as world-class beluga whale viewing. Thousands of beluga whales gather in the Churchill River estuary each summer, providing an unforgettable wildlife experience. Shore fishing is possible from several points along the town's coastline. Local guides offer boat tours that combine fishing and whale watching.

11. Owl River (Brook Trout, Arctic Char)

South of Churchill

The Owl River, flowing into Hudson Bay south of Churchill, offers brook trout and Arctic char fishing. The river's cold, clear waters hold sea-run brook trout that average 2-4 pounds. The char run is smaller than in the Churchill River but provides quality fishing. The Owl River is accessible by boat from Churchill or ATV.

12. North Knife River (Arctic Char, Brook Trout)

South of Churchill

The North Knife River offers Arctic char and brook trout fishing in a remote subarctic setting. The river's clear, cold waters hold healthy populations of both species. The North Knife River is less accessible than the Churchill River, requiring boat or ATV transport from Churchill. The fishing is excellent for those willing to make the trip.

13. South Knife River (Arctic Char)

South of Churchill

The South Knife River, located south of Churchill, offers Arctic char fishing in a pristine setting. The char run occurs in July and August. The river is remote and requires boat access from Churchill. The South Knife River offers excellent fishing with minimal angling pressure.

14. Thlewiaza River (Arctic Char, Grayling, Lake Trout)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

The Thlewiaza River flows through the far northwestern corner of Manitoba into Nunavut. The river system holds Arctic char, Arctic grayling, and lake trout. The river is fly-in only and is one of the most remote fishing destinations in Canada. Lodges on the Thlewiaza offer week-long packages for serious wilderness anglers.

15. Cochrane River (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

The Cochrane River system in northwestern Manitoba offers fishing for Arctic grayling, lake trout, and northern pike. The river's clear, cold waters provide ideal habitat for grayling. Lake trout are found in the deeper sections. The Cochrane River is fly-in only and is accessed through lodges in the area.

16. Caribou Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Churchill

Caribou Lake, located inland from the Hudson Bay coast, offers lake trout and northern pike fishing. The lake is less known than the region's rivers but holds healthy fish populations. Caribou Lake is accessible by ATV or boat from Churchill. The lake offers a change of pace from the river fishing that dominates the region.

17. Nejaniilini Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

Nejaniilini Lake, located in northwestern Manitoba near the Nunavut border, offers trophy lake trout and northern pike fishing. The lake is large, deep, and incredibly clear. Lake trout in the 10-20 pound range are common. The lake is fly-in only and has a small lodge operation. This is a destination for serious trophy anglers.

18. Shethanei Lake (Lake Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

Shethanei Lake, located in the far northwestern corner of Manitoba, offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in a pristine wilderness setting. The lake's clear, cold waters produce healthy fish. Shethanei Lake is fly-in only and receives very few anglers each season. This is one of Manitoba's most remote and least-pressured fisheries.

19. Munroe Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Near Churchill

Munroe Lake, located south of Churchill, offers northern pike and walleye fishing. The lake is shallower and warmer than the region's deep trout lakes. Munroe Lake provides a different fishing experience from the Arctic char and trout rivers that dominate the Hudson Bay coast region.

20. Button Bay & Cape Merry (Shore Fishing)

Churchill area

Button Bay and Cape Merry, located at the mouth of the Churchill River near the historic Fort Prince of Wales, offer shore fishing for Arctic char and brook trout. These historic sites offer excellent shore fishing access combined with a rich history. Fort Prince of Wales, built in the 1700s, is a National Historic Site and a must-visit for anyone travelling to Churchill.

21. Kasmere Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

Kasmere Lake, located in northwestern Manitoba near the Nunavut border, offers lake trout and northern pike fishing in a remote wilderness setting. The lake is fly-in only and receives minimal fishing pressure.

22. Nejaniilini Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northwest Manitoba, fly-in only

Nejaniilini Lake offers trophy lake trout and northern pike fishing. The lake is large, deep, and incredibly clear. Lake trout in the 10-20 pound range are common. Fly-in access only.

Fishing Tips for the Hudson Bay Coast

  • Plan extensively — Churchill is accessible by train (VIA Rail from Winnipeg) or by air. Both options are expensive and weather-dependent. Plan your trip months in advance.
  • Book guides early — Local guides and outfitters in Churchill have limited capacity and book well in advance, especially for the summer season.
  • Prepare for the weather — The Hudson Bay coast can experience near-freezing temperatures, fog, and strong winds even in July and August. Bring warm, waterproof clothing.
  • Respect the polar bears — Churchill is known as the polar bear capital of the world. Polar bears can be present year-round. Always travel with a local guide who carries deterrents and knows the area.
  • Bring insect repellent — The Hudson Bay coast has intense mosquito and black fly populations in summer. A head net and high-DEET repellent are essential.
