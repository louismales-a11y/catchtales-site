Fishing in Southwest Missouri & the Ozarks

Table Rock Lake, Bull Shoals Lake, Stockton Lake, Springfield lakes, and the bass, trout, crappie, and walleye fishing of the southwest Missouri Ozarks.

1. Table Rock Lake (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Walleye, Rainbow Trout)

Table Rock Lake, Stone/Taney counties

Table Rock Lake is a 43,000-acre reservoir in the Ozarks offering world-class bass fishing with excellent spotted bass, largemouth bass, and crappie populations.

2. Bull Shoals Lake (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Walleye, Channel Catfish, Rainbow Trout)

Bull Shoals Lake, Ozark/Taney counties

Bull Shoals Lake is a 45,000-acre reservoir on the Missouri-Arkansas border offering excellent bass and walleye fishing with deep, clear water.

3. Stockton Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Walleye, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Stockton Lake, Cedar/Dade counties

Stockton Lake is a 25,000-acre reservoir offering excellent walleye fishing, considered one of the best walleye lakes in Missouri.

4. James River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Rainbow Trout)

James River, Webster/Greene/Christian counties

The James River flows through the Springfield area and offers excellent smallmouth bass fishing with good float access.

5. Lake Springfield (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Lake Springfield, Greene County

Lake Springfield is a 200-acre lake in the heart of Springfield offering excellent urban fishing with a fishing dock.

6. Fellows Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Fellows Lake, Greene County

Fellows Lake offers excellent fishing with good shore access north of Springfield.

7. McDaniel Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

McDaniel Lake, Greene County

McDaniel Lake offers good fishing north of Springfield.

8. Lake Taneycomo (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Lake Taneycomo, Taney County

Lake Taneycomo is a 2,200-acre tailwater lake below Table Rock Dam offering world-class rainbow and brown trout fishing.

9. White River (Missouri) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

White River, Taney/Stone counties

The White River below Table Rock Lake offers excellent trout fishing in a scenic Ozark setting.

10. Roaring River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Roaring River, Barry County

Roaring River in Roaring River State Park is a premier trout fishing destination with a hatchery and excellent shore access.

11. Bennett Spring (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Bennett Spring, Laclede/Dallas counties

Bennett Spring State Park is one of Missouri's top trout parks with a large spring-fed creek and hatchery.

12. Buffalo National River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Ozark Bass, Channel Catfish)

Buffalo National River, Newton County

The Buffalo National River offers excellent float fishing for smallmouth bass in a pristine Ozark setting.

13. Elk River (MO) (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie)

Elk River, McDonald County

The Elk River offers excellent smallmouth bass fishing in southwest Missouri.

14. Big Sugar Creek (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Ozark Bass)

Big Sugar Creek, McDonald County

Big Sugar Creek is a pristine Ozark stream offering excellent smallmouth bass fishing.

15. Indian Creek (Barry) (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish)

Indian Creek, Barry County

Indian Creek offers good smallmouth bass fishing.

16. Shoal Creek (SW MO) (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish)

Shoal Creek, Newton County

Shoal Creek offers good smallmouth bass fishing in southwest Missouri.

17. Center Creek (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish)

Center Creek, Jasper County

Center Creek offers good fishing near Joplin.

18. Spring River (MO) (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish)

Spring River, Jasper County

The Spring River offers good smallmouth bass fishing.

19. Sac River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie)

Sac River, Dade/Polk counties

The Sac River offers good fishing in southwest Missouri.

20. Little Sac River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish)

Little Sac River, Greene/Polk counties

The Little Sac River offers good smallmouth bass fishing.

21. Turnback Creek (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish)

Turnback Creek offers good fishing.

Cedar Creek offers good fishing.

Lost Creek, Stone County

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at mdc.mo.gov. Trout stamp required for trout fishing.
  • Fish Table Rock Lake -- One of the best bass fishing lakes in the country with excellent spotted bass and largemouth bass.
  • Try Lake Taneycomo -- Lake Taneycomo offers world-class rainbow and brown trout fishing below Table Rock Dam.
  • Visit the trout parks -- Roaring River, Bennett Spring, and Montauk offer excellent family trout fishing.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-May and September-October offer the best fishing throughout the Ozarks.
