Fishing on Molokai & Offshore Hawaii

Molokai, deep sea, island channels, and the remote fishing grounds of Hawaii.

1. Molokai South Shore (Bonefish, Papio, Ulua, Trevally)

Molokai south coast

The south shore of Molokai offers excellent bonefish and papio fishing along the longest continuous fringing reef in Hawaii.

2. Molokai North Shore (Ulua, Giant Trevally, Papio)

Molokai north coast

The remote north shore of Molokai offers spectacular ulua fishing from the rugged coastline, accessible only by boat or trail.

3. Kalaupapa Coast (Ulua, Trevally, Papio)

Kalaupapa, north Molokai

The Kalaupapa coastline offers remote fishing below the historic Kalaupapa Peninsula.

4. Pala'au State Park (Ulua, Papio, Trevally)

Pala'au, west Molokai

The west end of Molokai offers good shore fishing near Pala'au State Park.

5. Molokai Reefs (Bonefish, Papio, Goatfish, Reef Fish)

Molokai fringing reef, south shore

Molokai's extensive fringing reef offers world-class bonefish and reef fishing.

6. Pailolo Channel (Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Ono)

Pailolo Channel, between Molokai and Maui

The Pailolo Channel is a productive offshore fishing area between Molokai and Maui.

7. Kaiwi Channel (Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Ono)

Kaiwi Channel, between Molokai and Oahu

The Kaiwi Channel offers excellent offshore fishing for marlin and tuna.

8. Penguin Banks (Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Bottom Fish)

Penguin Banks, off Molokai

The Penguin Banks are a productive offshore fishing ground known for bottom fish and pelagics.

9. Deep Sea (Blue Marlin, Black Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Wahoo)

Offshore Hawaii, various locations

Hawaii's deep offshore waters offer world-class fishing for blue marlin, black marlin, yellowfin tuna, mahi-mahi, and wahoo (ono).

10. Hawaiian Islands Humpback Whale National Marine Sanctuary (Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi)

Humpback Sanctuary, various channels

The sanctuary waters offer excellent fishing for pelagic species while protecting humpback whales during winter.

11. Cross-Seamount (Tuna, Mahi-Mahi, Marlin)

Cross-Seamount, south of Hawaii

The Cross-Seamount is a productive offshore fishing ground south of the Big Island.

12. Kahoolawe (Offshore) (Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Ono)

Kahoolawe, off Maui

The waters around Kahoolawe offer excellent offshore fishing with restricted access to the island itself.

13. French Frigate Shoals (Tuna, Mahi-Mahi, Marlin, Reef Fish)

Northwestern Hawaiian Islands

The remote Northwestern Hawaiian Islands offer pristine fishing in a protected marine national monument.

14. Midway Atoll (Tuna, Mahi-Mahi, Marlin, Reef Fish)

Midway Atoll, Northwestern Hawaiian Islands

Midway Atoll offers world-class fishing in one of the most remote locations in the Pacific, within the Papahānaumokuākea Marine National Monument.

15. Offshore Bottom Fishing (Snapper, Grouper, Jacks, Amberjack)

Offshore Hawaii, various locations

Hawaii's offshore bottom fishing targets onaga, ehu, opakapaka, and other deep-water snappers found only in Hawaii.

16. Molokai South Reef (Bonefish, Papio, Goatfish)

Molokai south shore

Molokai's south reef offers excellent bonefish fishing.

17. Kawela Bay (Papio, Bonefish)

Kawela, Molokai

Kawela Bay offers good fishing.

18. Kamalo Harbor (Papio, Bonefish, Goatfish)

Kamalo, Molokai

Kamalo Harbor offers good fishing.

19. Ilio Point (Ulua, Papio, Trevally)

Ilio Point, Molokai

Ilio Point offers good shore fishing.

20. Kapukahehu Bay (Papio, Bonefish)

Kapukahehu, Molokai

Kapukahehu Bay offers good fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- No recreational saltwater licence required in Hawaii.
  • Fish Molokai's reef -- Molokai's south shore fringing reef offers some of the best bonefish fishing in Hawaii.
  • Try offshore charters -- Hawaii's deep-sea fishing for marlin and tuna is world-class. Book through any major island harbor.
  • Best months -- May through September offers the best offshore conditions across the Hawaiian Islands.
  • Endangered species -- The Northwestern Hawaiian Islands are strictly protected. Fishing permits are required and limited.
