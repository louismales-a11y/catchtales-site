Fishing in Far North Saskatchewan

Lake Athabasca, Cree Lake, Wollaston Lake, and Reindeer Lake — trophy lake trout and northern pike in some of Canada's most remote waters.

1. Lake Athabasca (Lake Trout, Northern Pike, Arctic Grayling)

Far northern Saskatchewan, accessible via Fort McMurray or float plane

Lake Athabasca is one of the largest lakes in North America and offers world-class lake trout fishing. The Saskatchewan side of the lake produces trophy lake trout that regularly exceed 30 pounds. Northern pike are abundant in the shallow bays. Arctic grayling are found in the tributaries. The Saskatchewan side of the lake is less developed than the Alberta side and offers a true wilderness experience. Access is by float plane or boat from Uranium City.

2. Cree Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Central northern Saskatchewan, fly-in only

Cree Lake is one of the premier trophy lake trout destinations in North America. The lake's deep, clear, cold waters produce lake trout that regularly exceed 40 pounds. Northern pike in Cree Lake also reach trophy sizes, with fish over 30 pounds possible. Cree Lake is fly-in only, accessed through lodges that have operated here for decades. This is a must-fish destination for serious trophy anglers.

3. Wollaston Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike, Arctic Grayling)

Far northeastern Saskatchewan, fly-in or road access

Wollaston Lake is one of the largest lakes in Saskatchewan and offers excellent fishing for lake trout, northern pike, and Arctic grayling. The lake is unique because it drains to both the Arctic Ocean and Hudson Bay. Wollaston Lake has road access from the community of Wollaston Lake and several lodges. Lake trout in the 5-15 pound range are common.

4. Reindeer Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye, Lake Trout)

Northeastern Saskatchewan, straddles Manitoba border

The Saskatchewan side of Reindeer Lake offers excellent fishing for northern pike, walleye, and lake trout. The lake is massive and diverse, with shallow weedy bays and deep clear basins. Reindeer Lake has several lodges on the Saskatchewan side and is accessible by road or float plane.

5. Black Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike, Arctic Grayling)

Far northern Saskatchewan, fly-in only

Black Lake, located north of Lake Athabasca, offers lake trout, northern pike, and Arctic grayling fishing in a pristine wilderness setting. The lake is fly-in only and receives very few anglers. This is a destination for serious wilderness anglers.

6. Hasbala Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Northern Saskatchewan, fly-in only

Hasbala Lake offers trophy lake trout and northern pike fishing in a remote shield setting. The lake is fly-in only and has a lodge that offers all-inclusive packages.

7. Waterfound Lake (Lake Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Northern Saskatchewan, fly-in only

Waterfound Lake offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in a remote wilderness setting. The lake is known for its clear waters and healthy fish populations.

8. Geikie River (Arctic Grayling, Brook Trout)

Northern Saskatchewan

The Geikie River system, flowing through the shield country of northern Saskatchewan, offers Arctic grayling and brook trout fishing. The cold, clear waters provide ideal habitat for both species.

9. Foster Lake (Lake Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Northern Saskatchewan, fly-in only

Foster Lake offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in a remote shield setting. The lake is less known than the region's premier destinations but provides excellent fishing.

10. Tazin Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Far northern Saskatchewan, fly-in only

Tazin Lake, located near the Northwest Territories border, offers lake trout and northern pike fishing in a remote wilderness setting. The lake is fly-in only and receives minimal fishing pressure.

11. Garson Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Near La Loche

Garson Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the far northwestern region.

12. Lac La Loche (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Near La Loche

Lac La Loche is a large lake in northwestern Saskatchewan offering walleye and northern pike.

13. Turnor Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Near Turnor Lake

Turnor Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the far north.

14. Cree Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike, Walleye)

Central northern SK

The south basin of Cree Lake offers excellent lake trout and northern pike fishing with fly-in access.

15. Hearne Lake (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout)

Near Cree Lake

Hearne Lake offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in the Cree Lake region.

16. Waddy Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Black Lake

Waddy Lake offers lake trout and northern pike fishing in far northern Saskatchewan.

17. Cole Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Black Lake

Cole Lake offers northern pike and lake trout fishing in the Athabasca region.

18. Mud Lake (Athabasca)

Near Lake Athabasca

Mud Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the Lake Athabasca region.

19. Ennuyeuse Lake (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout)

Far north SK

Ennuyeuse Lake offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in the remote north.

20. Fontas River (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Near NWT border

The Fontas River offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the far north.

21. Twei Lake (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout)

Far north SK

Twei Lake offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in the remote shield country.

22. Neck Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Black Lake

Neck Lake offers northern pike and lake trout fishing in the Black Lake region.

23. Knight Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Lake Athabasca

Knight Lake offers lake trout and northern pike fishing in the Athabasca region.

24. Scimitar Lake (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout)

Far north SK

Scimitar Lake offers lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in the remote far north.

25. Patterson Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Near Black Lake

Patterson Lake offers northern pike and lake trout fishing in northern Saskatchewan.

Fishing Tips for Far North Saskatchewan

  • Get a licence — Buy online at nirb.saskatchewan.ca.
  • Plan extensively — Far northern Saskatchewan lodges book years in advance. Plan your trip well ahead.
  • Bring warm clothing — Even in July, northern Saskatchewan can experience cold temperatures and strong winds.
  • Book fly-ins early — Fly-in lodges on Cree Lake, Black Lake, and Lake Athabasca fill up quickly.
  • Respect the resource — These northern fisheries are fragile. Most trophy lake trout fisheries are catch-and-release.
