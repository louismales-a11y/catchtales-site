Fishing in Southwestern NM & Gila

Gila River, Bill Evans Lake, Lake Roberts, Gila Wilderness, and the Gila trout, rainbow trout, largemouth bass, and channel catfish fishing of southwestern New Mexico.

1. Gila River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Gila Trout, Channel Catfish, Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass)

Gila River, Grant/Catron counties

The Gila River in the Gila National Forest is home to the rare native Gila trout and offers excellent wilderness trout fishing in the rugged Gila Wilderness.

2. Gila Trout Recovery Waters (Gila Trout)

Various streams, Grant/Catron counties

Several remote streams in the Gila Wilderness are managed for the recovery of the endangered Gila trout, New Mexico's state fish.

3. Bill Evans Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Bluegill, Rainbow Trout)

Bill Evans Lake, Grant County

Bill Evans Lake is a 120-acre lake near Silver City offering excellent warmwater fishing with a county park.

4. Lake Roberts (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Lake Roberts, Grant County

Lake Roberts is a 250-acre lake in the Gila National Forest offering excellent trout and bass fishing with a Forest Service campground.

5. Mountain View Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie, Bluegill)

Mountain View Lake, Grant County

Mountain View Lake near Silver City offers good fishing in a mountain setting.

6. Bear Mountain Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Bear Mountain Lake, Grant County

Bear Mountain Lake offers good high-elevation trout fishing in the Pinos Altos Mountains.

7. Lake Bataan (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Lake Bataan, Grant County

Lake Bataan offers good trout fishing in the Gila National Forest near Silver City.

8. Ft. Bayard Pond (Rainbow Trout, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Ft. Bayard, Grant County

Ft. Bayard Pond offers good community fishing near Silver City.

9. Wall Lake (Catron Co.) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Wall Lake, Catron County

Wall Lake in the Gila National Forest offers excellent trout fishing in a scenic mountain setting.

10. Snow Lake (Catron Co.) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Snow Lake, Catron County

Snow Lake offers good trout fishing in the Gila Wilderness area.

11. Mogollon Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Gila Trout)

12. Whitewater Creek (Catron) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Gila Trout)

Fishing Tips for the Southwestern NM & Gila

  • Check local regulations before fishing — each water body may have specific rules on limits, seasons, and bait.
  • Spring and fall typically offer the best fishing in New Mexico for most species.
  • Consider hiring a local guide for the best experience on unfamiliar waters.
  • Always carry a valid New Mexico fishing license — available online through the state wildlife agency.
  • Practice catch and release for sensitive species to help maintain healthy fish populations.
