Fishing in Southeastern Montana & Yellowstone Region

Yellowstone River, Bighorn River, Tongue River, Powder River, and the rainbow trout, brown trout, smallmouth bass, walleye, and catfish fishing of southeastern Montana.

1. Yellowstone River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Sauger, Channel Catfish)

Yellowstone River, Park/Stillwater/Yellowstone counties

The Yellowstone River is the longest free-flowing river in the lower 48 states, offering world-class trout fishing in Paradise Valley and excellent warmwater fishing downstream.

2. Bighorn River - Afterbay Tailwaters (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Mountain Whitefish)

Bighorn River below Yellowtail Dam, Big Horn County

The Bighorn River below Yellowtail Dam is one of the most famous tailwater trout fisheries in the world with exceptional rainbow and brown trout fishing.

3. Bighorn Lake (Yellowtail Reservoir) (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Bighorn Lake, Big Horn/Carbon counties

Bighorn Lake is a 17,000-acre reservoir created by Yellowtail Dam offering excellent walleye, pike, and smallmouth bass fishing.

4. Tongue River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Walleye)

Tongue River, Big Horn/Custer counties

The Tongue River Reservoir and downstream river offer excellent trout and warmwater fishing.

5. Tongue River Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish)

Tongue River Reservoir, Big Horn County

Tongue River Reservoir is a 2,000-acre impoundment offering excellent walleye and pike fishing.

6. Powder River (Channel Catfish, Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Sauger)

Powder River, Powder River/Custer counties

The Powder River offers excellent channel catfish and walleye fishing with good access.

7. Little Bighorn River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Little Bighorn River, Big Horn County

The Little Bighorn River offers good trout fishing in the Wolf Mountains.

8. Rosebud Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Rosebud Creek, Treasure/Rosebud counties

Rosebud Creek offers good trout and smallmouth bass fishing.

9. Stillwater River (MT) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Stillwater River, Stillwater County

The Stillwater River is a major tributary of the Yellowstone offering excellent trout fishing in the Beartooth foothills.

10. Clarks Fork Yellowstone River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Clarks Fork Yellowstone, Carbon County

The Clarks Fork of the Yellowstone offers excellent trout fishing with spectacular mountain scenery.

11. Rock Creek (Carbon) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Rock Creek, Carbon County

Rock Creek near Red Lodge offers excellent trout fishing in the Beartooth Mountains.

12. Beartooth Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Golden Trout)

Beartooth Lake, Carbon County

Beartooth Lake in the Beartooth Mountains offers spectacular alpine fishing for cutthroat and golden trout.

13. Island Lake (MT) (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Island Lake, Carbon County

Island Lake in the Beartooths offers excellent high alpine trout fishing.

14. Cooney Reservoir (Walleye, Rainbow Trout, Yellow Perch, Northern Pike)

Cooney Reservoir, Carbon County

Cooney Reservoir offers good walleye and trout fishing south of Billings.

15. Lake Elmo (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Walleye, Yellow Perch)

Lake Elmo, Yellowstone County

Lake Elmo in Billings offers excellent urban fishing with a fishing dock.

16. Pompeys Pillar (Channel Catfish, Walleye, Smallmouth Bass, Sauger)

Pompeys Pillar area, Yellowstone County

The Yellowstone River near Pompeys Pillar offers excellent catfish and walleye fishing.

17. Big Horn Canyon (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Lake Trout)

Big Horn Canyon, Big Horn County

Big Horn Canyon on Bighorn Lake offers spectacular scenery and excellent fishing.

18. Afterbay Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Afterbay Lake, Big Horn County

The Bighorn Afterbay below Yellowtail Dam offers good fishing for stocked trout.

19. Horseshoe Bend (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass)

Horseshoe Bend, Big Horn County

Horseshoe Bend on Bighorn Lake offers good fishing access.

20. Ok-A-Beh Marina (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Lake Trout)

Ok-A-Beh Marina, Big Horn County

Ok-A-Beh Marina on Bighorn Lake offers good fishing access.

21. Fort Smith Area (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Walleye)

Fort Smith area, Big Horn County

The Fort Smith area near the Bighorn River offers excellent trout and walleye fishing.

22. Lake Mason (Walleye, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch)

Lake Mason, Musselshell County

Lake Mason offers good walleye and pike fishing.

23. Deadmans Basin Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch, Rainbow Trout)

Deadmans Basin, Wheatland County

Deadmans Basin Reservoir offers good walleye and perch fishing.

24. Musselshell River (Channel Catfish, Walleye, Northern Pike, Sauger, Smallmouth Bass)

Musselshell River, Musselshell/Golden Valley counties

The Musselshell River offers good warmwater fishing for catfish, walleye, and pike.

25. Yellowtail Dam Tailwaters (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Yellowtail Dam, Big Horn County

The tailwaters below Yellowtail Dam offer consistent cold water and excellent trout fishing.

26. Sarpy Creek (Channel Catfish, Walleye, Northern Pike)

Sarpy Creek, Treasure/Big Horn counties

Sarpy Creek offers good warmwater fishing.

27. Armells Creek (Walleye, Northern Pike, Channel Catfish)

Armells Creek, Treasure County

Armells Creek offers good fishing.

28. Pumpkin Creek (Channel Catfish, Walleye, Sauger)

Pumpkin Creek, Custer County

Pumpkin Creek offers good fishing near Miles City.

29. O'Fallon Creek (Walleye, Northern Pike, Channel Catfish)

O'Fallon Creek, Custer County

O'Fallon Creek offers good fishing.

30. Mizpah Creek (Channel Catfish, Walleye, Northern Pike)

Mizpah Creek, Custer/Powder River counties

Mizpah Creek offers good warmwater fishing in southeastern Montana.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fwp.mt.gov. Conservation license required.
  • Fish the Bighorn River -- The Bighorn below Yellowtail Dam is one of the world's most famous tailwater trout fisheries.
  • Try the Yellowstone River -- The Yellowstone offers diverse fishing from trout in Paradise Valley to catfish near Billings.
  • Explore the Beartooths -- The Beartooth Mountains offer spectacular alpine lake fishing for cutthroat and golden trout.
  • Late summer and fall are best -- August through October offer the best fishing throughout the region.
