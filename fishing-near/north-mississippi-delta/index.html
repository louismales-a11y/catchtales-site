Fishing in North Mississippi & Delta

Sardis Lake, Arkabutla Lake, Enid Lake, Grenada Lake, Mississippi River oxbows, and the big reservoirs and bass lakes of the Mississippi Delta.

1. Sardis Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill, Redear Sunfish)

Sardis Lake, Panola/Lafayette counties

Sardis Lake is a 98,000-acre flood control reservoir on the Little Tallahatchie River, famous for outstanding crappie and largemouth bass fishing.

2. Arkabutla Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill, Striped Bass)

Arkabutla Lake, Tate/DeSoto counties

Arkabutla Lake is a 13,000-acre reservoir offering excellent crappie, bass, and white bass fishing.

3. Enid Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill, Striped Bass)

Enid Lake, Yalobusha/Panola counties

Enid Lake is known for producing trophy crappie and excellent bass fishing.

4. Grenada Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Striped Bass)

Grenada Lake, Grenada/Yalobusha counties

Grenada Lake is a 64,000-acre reservoir famous for producing world-record crappie and excellent bass fishing.

5. Mississippi River Oxbows (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Bluegill, Redear Sunfish)

MS River oxbows, Washington/Bolivar counties

The oxbow lakes along the Mississippi River — including Lake Lee, Lake Washington, and Lake Jackson — offer excellent bass, crappie, and blue catfish fishing in unique natural settings.

6. Lake Washington (MS) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Lake Washington, Washington County

Lake Washington is a 5,000-acre oxbow lake near Greenville, famous for its blue catfish and bass fishing.

7. Lake Lee (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Lake Lee, Bolivar County

Lake Lee is an oxbow lake offering good fishing near Rosedale.

8. Lake Jackson (MS Delta) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Bluegill)

Lake Jackson, Washington County

Lake Jackson offers good oxbow fishing.

9. Lake Beulah (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish)

Lake Beulah, Bolivar County

Lake Beulah offers good oxbow fishing.

10. Choctaw Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Choctaw Lake, Montgomery County

Choctaw Lake is a state park lake offering good family fishing.

11. John W. Kyle Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

John W. Kyle Lake, Panola County

John Kyle Lake is a 154-acre lake at Sardis Lake State Park offering good fishing.

12. Lake Lamar Bruce (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Lamar Bruce, Itawamba County

Lake Lamar Bruce is a state lake offering good fishing.

13. Lake Mike Conner (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Redear Sunfish, Channel Catfish)

Lake Mike Conner, Copiah County

Lake Mike Conner offers good fishing in central Mississippi.

14. Lake Mary Crawford (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Mary Crawford, Tishomingo County

Lake Mary Crawford offers good fishing.

15. Tishomingo State Park Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Tishomingo SP, Tishomingo County

Tishomingo State Park Lake offers good fishing in northeast Mississippi.

16. Lake Binder (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Binder, Lafayette County

Lake Binder offers good fishing near Oxford.

17. Sardis Lake (Upper) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Striped Bass)

Upper Sardis Lake, Lafayette County

The upper portion of Sardis Lake offers excellent fishing.

18. Little Tallahatchie River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Blue Catfish)

Little Tallahatchie River, Panola/Lafayette counties

The Little Tallahatchie River flows through the Delta and offers good river fishing.

19. Yocona River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Blue Catfish)

Yocona River, Lafayette/Yalobusha counties

The Yocona River offers good fishing.

20. Tallahatchie River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish)

Tallahatchie River, Panola/Quitman counties

The Tallahatchie River offers excellent catfish and bass fishing in the Delta.

21. Yazoo River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish)

Yazoo River, Yazoo/Humphreys counties

The Yazoo River offers excellent catfish fishing in the Delta.

22. Sunflower River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Bowfin, Chain Pickerel)

Sunflower River, Sunflower/Sharkey counties

The Sunflower River offers unique fishing in the Delta with bowfin and chain pickerel.

23. Big Sunflower River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish)

Big Sunflower River, Washington County

The Big Sunflower River offers good fishing.

24. Steele Bayou (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Bowfin)

Steele Bayou, Washington County

Steele Bayou offers good fishing near the Mississippi River.

25. Black Bayou (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Blue Catfish, Bowfin)

Black Bayou, Bolivar County

Black Bayou offers good fishing.

26. Coldwater River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Blue Catfish)

Coldwater River, Tate/DeSoto counties

The Coldwater River offers good fishing.

27. Wolf River (MS) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish)

Wolf River, DeSoto/Tate counties

The Wolf River offers good fishing near Memphis.

28. Lake Cormorant (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Cormorant, DeSoto County

Lake Cormorant offers good fishing near the Tennessee border.

29. Arkabutla Creek (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish)

Arkabutla Creek, Tate County

Arkabutla Creek offers good fishing below the dam.

30. Hurricane Creek (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish)

Hurricane Creek, Panola County

Hurricane Creek offers good fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at mdwfp.com. Freshwater and saltwater licences available.
  • Fish Sardis Lake -- Sardis is one of Mississippi's premier crappie and bass lakes.
  • Try the oxbows -- Mississippi River oxbow lakes offer unique fishing for blue catfish and bass.
  • Grenada Lake crappie -- Grenada Lake has produced world-record crappie.
  • Spring and fall are best -- March-May and September-November offer the best fishing.
