Fishing in North Missouri

Mark Twain Lake, Thousand Hills Lake, Hunnewell Lake, and the bass, crappie, bluegill, and catfish fishing of north Missouri.

1. Mark Twain Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Blue Catfish, Walleye, Bluegill)

Mark Twain Lake, Monroe/Ralls counties

Mark Twain Lake is an 18,000-acre reservoir offering excellent crappie, bass, and catfish fishing with multiple fishing docks and shore fishing areas.

2. Thousand Hills Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Thousand Hills Lake, Adair County

Thousand Hills Lake is a 640-acre lake in Thousand Hills State Park offering excellent fishing with a fishing dock.

3. Hunnewell Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Hunnewell Lake, Shelby County

Hunnewell Lake is a 165-acre managed fishing lake offering excellent shore fishing access.

4. Long Branch Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Walleye, Bluegill)

Long Branch Lake, Macon County

Long Branch Lake is a 5,400-acre reservoir offering excellent crappie and walleye fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at mdc.mo.gov.
  • Fish Mark Twain Lake -- Mark Twain Lake offers excellent crappie and catfish fishing with great shore access.
  • Try Thousand Hills Lake -- Thousand Hills State Park offers good fishing with a fishing dock.
  • Managed fishing lakes -- Many north Missouri lakes are managed by MDC with fishing docks and shore access.
  • Spring and fall are best -- March-May and September-November offer the best fishing.
