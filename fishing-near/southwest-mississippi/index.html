Fishing in Southwest Mississippi

Mississippi River, Homochitto River, Okhissa Lake, Clear Springs Lake, and the bass, catfish, crappie, and stocked trout fishing of southwest Mississippi.

1. Mississippi River (Lower) (Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, Largemouth Bass, White Bass, Crappie)

Mississippi River, Claiborne/Warren counties

The lower Mississippi River along Mississippi's western border offers world-class blue catfish and flathead catfish fishing, with trophy fish exceeding 100 pounds.

2. Homochitto River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, Rainbow Trout, Redear Sunfish)

Homochitto River, Lincoln/Franklin/Amite counties

The Homochitto River offers excellent catfish and bass fishing through southwest Mississippi, with winter trout stocking near Okhissa Lake.

3. Okhissa Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish, Rainbow Trout)

Okhissa Lake, Franklin County

Okhissa Lake is a 1,000-acre lake in the Homochitto National Forest offering excellent bass, crappie, and winter trout fishing.

4. Clear Springs Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Clear Springs Lake, Franklin County

Clear Springs Lake is a 30-acre lake stocked with rainbow trout in winter, offering a unique coldwater fishery in Mississippi.

5. Lake Mary (Lincoln) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Mary, Lincoln County

Lake Mary offers good fishing near Brookhaven.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at mdwfp.com.
  • Fish the Mississippi River -- The big river offers world-class blue catfish fishing with fish over 100 pounds.
  • Try Okhissa Lake -- Okhissa Lake offers excellent bass fishing and winter trout stocking.
  • Winter trout fishing -- Clear Springs and Okhissa Lake are stocked with rainbow trout in November-February.
  • Spring and fall are best -- March-May and September-November offer the best fishing.
