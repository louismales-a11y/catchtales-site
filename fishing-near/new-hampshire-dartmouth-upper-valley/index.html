Fishing in the Dartmouth & Upper Valley Region

Connecticut River, Mascoma Lake, Lake Morey, Lake Fairlee, and the landlocked salmon, lake trout, rainbow trout, brown trout, and smallmouth bass fishing of the Upper Connecticut River Valley.

1. Connecticut River (Upper Valley) (Landlocked Salmon, Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye, Northern Pike)

Connecticut River, Grafton County

The Connecticut River through the Upper Valley from Hanover to Haverhill offers exceptional fishing for landlocked salmon, lake trout, and smallmouth bass in the deep, wide reaches of the river with numerous public access points and boat launches.

2. Mascoma Lake (Landlocked Salmon, Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, White Perch)

Mascoma Lake, Grafton County

Mascoma Lake is a 1,320-acre lake in Enfield offering excellent landlocked salmon and lake trout fishing with a state-owned boat launch and the Mascoma Lake State Forest.

3. Lake Morey (Lake Trout, Landlocked Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Yellow Perch)

Lake Morey, Grafton County

Lake Morey is a 435-acre lake in Fairlee offering excellent lake trout and landlocked salmon fishing with a resort and the longest ice skating trail in the US.

4. Lake Fairlee (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Lake Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, White Perch)

Lake Fairlee, Grafton County

Lake Fairlee is a 537-acre lake straddling the Vermont-New Hampshire border near Orford offering excellent rainbow and lake trout fishing in a scenic Upper Valley setting.

5. Sugar River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Fallfish)

Sugar River, Sullivan County

The Sugar River flows 27 miles through Newport, Claremont, and to the Connecticut River, offering excellent trout fishing with the Sugar River Rail Trail providing access.

6. White River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye)

White River, Grafton County

The White River is a major Connecticut River tributary flowing from Vermont into New Hampshire near White River Junction, offering excellent trout and smallmouth bass fishing in its lower reaches.

7. Ompompanoosuc River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Ompompanoosuc River, Grafton County

The Ompompanoosuc River — known as "The Omp" — flows from Vermont to the Connecticut River in Hanover, offering excellent trout fishing with walk-in access.

8. Waits River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Waits River, Grafton County

The Waits River flows from Vermont to the Connecticut River near Bradford, offering good trout fishing in its lower reaches in New Hampshire.

9. Oliverian Brook (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Brown Trout)

Oliverian Brook offers good trout fishing in the Upper Valley near Haverhill and Piermont.

10. Mink Brook (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Brown Trout)

Fishing Tips for the Dartmouth & Upper Valley Region

  • Check local regulations before fishing — each water body may have specific rules on limits, seasons, and bait.
  • Spring and fall typically offer the best fishing in New Hampshire for most species.
  • Consider hiring a local guide for the best experience on unfamiliar waters.
  • Always carry a valid New Hampshire fishing license — available online through the state wildlife agency.
  • Practice catch and release for sensitive species to help maintain healthy fish populations.
