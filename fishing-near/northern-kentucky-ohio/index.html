Fishing in Northern Kentucky & Ohio River

Ohio River, Licking River, Markland Dam, Meldahl Dam, and the border waters of northern Kentucky.

1. Ohio River (Sauger, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Ohio River, northern Kentucky border

The Ohio River forms Kentucky's northern border and offers world-class sauger and catfish fishing from numerous access points.

2. Licking River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie, Sauger)

Licking River, Kenton/Campbell counties

The Licking River offers excellent smallmouth bass fishing flowing through northern Kentucky to the Ohio River.

3. Markland Dam Pool (Sauger, Walleye, Catfish, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass)

Markland Dam, Gallatin/Boone counties

The Markland Dam pool on the Ohio River offers excellent sauger and walleye fishing.

4. Meldahl Dam Pool (Sauger, Walleye, Catfish, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass)

Meldahl Dam, Bracken/Mason counties

The Meldahl Dam pool offers excellent sauger fishing on the Ohio River.

5. McAlpine Dam Pool (Sauger, Catfish, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass)

McAlpine Dam, Jefferson County

The McAlpine Dam pool near Louisville offers excellent sauger and catfish fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fw.ky.gov.
  • Fish the Ohio River -- The Ohio River offers excellent sauger and catfish fishing. Spring sauger runs are legendary.
  • Try Markland Dam -- The tailwaters below Markland Dam offer excellent fishing for sauger and walleye.
  • Licking River smallmouth -- The Licking River offers good smallmouth bass fishing in northern Kentucky.
  • Spring and fall are best -- March-May and September-November offer the best fishing.
