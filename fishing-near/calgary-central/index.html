Fishing in Calgary & Central Alberta

Bow River through Calgary, Kananaskis Country, Ghost Reservoir, and the central Alberta parklands.

1. Bow River (Calgary)

Calgary

The Bow River through Calgary offers world-class rainbow and brown trout fishing within the city. The river's nutrient-rich waters produce large trout.

2. Ghost Reservoir

West of Calgary

Ghost Reservoir offers rainbow trout, brown trout, and northern pike fishing in a scenic setting west of Calgary.

3. Kananaskis River

Kananaskis Country

The Kananaskis River offers rainbow and cutthroat trout fishing in the beautiful Kananaskis Valley.

4. Spray Lakes Reservoir

Kananaskis

Spray Lakes Reservoir offers rainbow trout and lake trout fishing in a high-elevation reservoir.

5. Upper Kananaskis Lake

Kananaskis

Upper Kananaskis Lake offers rainbow trout, cutthroat trout, and bull trout fishing.

6. Lower Kananaskis Lake

Kananaskis

Lower Kananaskis Lake offers rainbow trout and cutthroat trout fishing in Kananaskis Country.

7. Barrier Lake

Kananaskis

Barrier Lake offers rainbow trout and lake trout fishing in the Kananaskis Valley.

8. Elbow River

Calgary/SW Alberta

The Elbow River offers rainbow and brown trout fishing flowing from the Elbow-Sheep Wildland into Calgary.

9. Sheep River

Turner Valley area

The Sheep River offers rainbow and cutthroat trout fishing in the foothills southwest of Calgary.

10. Highwood River

Longview area

The Highwood River offers excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing in the foothills.

11. Jumpingpound Creek

Cochrane area

Jumpingpound Creek offers rainbow and brown trout fishing near Cochrane.

12. Cochrane Lake

Cochrane

Cochrane Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing near Cochrane.

13. Sibbald Lake

Kananaskis

Sibbald Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in Sibbald Lake Provincial Recreation Area.

14. Chain Lakes Reservoir

Nanton area

Chain Lakes Reservoir offers rainbow trout and northern pike fishing on the eastern slopes.

15. Pine Coulee Reservoir

Near Claresholm

Pine Coulee Reservoir offers rainbow trout, walleye, and northern pike fishing.

16. Travers Reservoir

Near Carmangay

Travers Reservoir offers walleye, northern pike, and perch fishing in south-central Alberta.

17. Little Bow Reservoir

Near Carmangay

Little Bow Reservoir offers walleye, northern pike, and perch fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at albertarelm.com.
  • Fish the Bow in Calgary -- The Bow River through Calgary offers world-class trout fishing within the city limits.
  • Try Kananaskis -- Kananaskis Country offers excellent trout fishing in a beautiful mountain setting.
  • Watch for closures -- Some Kananaskis lakes have seasonal closures for fish spawning. Check regulations.
  • Bring bear spray -- Kananaskis Country has significant bear populations. Carry bear spray when hiking to remote fishing spots.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
