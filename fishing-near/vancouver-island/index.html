Fishing in Vancouver Island

Campbell River salmon, Port Alberni steelhead, Sooke halibut, trout lakes -- world-class fishing around every corner.

1. Campbell River

Campbell River

Campbell River is known as the "Salmon Capital of the World." The river offers excellent Chinook, coho, and pink salmon fishing. The Tyee Club has been based here since 1924.

2. Port Alberni (Stamp River)

Port Alberni

The Stamp River and Somass River offer world-class Chinook and steelhead fishing. The rivers drain into Barkley Sound, one of BC's premier salmon fishing areas.

3. Sooke (Salmon & Halibut)

Sooke

Sooke offers excellent saltwater fishing for Chinook salmon, coho, and halibut. The Sooke Harbour and offshore waters are productive year-round.

4. Victoria Harbour & Area

Victoria

Victoria offers saltwater salmon and halibut fishing in the Strait of Juan de Fuca. The Oak Bay and Haro Strait areas are productive.

5. Cowichan River (Steelhead)

Cowichan Valley

The Cowichan River is one of Vancouver Island's premier steelhead streams. The river also hosts excellent salmon runs and cutthroat trout fishing.

6. Gold River (Muchalat)

Gold River

The Gold River and Muchalat River offer excellent Chinook and coho salmon fishing on the island's west coast.

7. Nimpkish River

North Vancouver Island

The Nimpkish River on northern Vancouver Island offers excellent salmon and steelhead fishing in a remote wilderness setting.

8. Englishman River

Parksville area

The Englishman River offers steelhead, salmon, and cutthroat trout fishing near Parksville and the island's east coast.

9. Qualicum River

Qualicum Beach

The Qualicum River offers good salmon and steelhead fishing on Vancouver Island's east coast.

10. Butchart Gardens Area

Brentwood Bay

The waters near Brentwood Bay offer salmon and bottom fish fishing in a scenic setting near Butchart Gardens.

11. Port Renfrew (San Juan River)

Port Renfrew

The San Juan River near Port Renfrew offers excellent steelhead and salmon fishing on the island's southwest coast.

12. Nitinaht River

Nitinaht Lake area

The Nitinaht River system offers salmon and steelhead fishing on the island's west coast. Nitinaht Lake also offers excellent fishing.

13. Great Central Lake

Port Alberni

Great Central Lake offers rainbow trout and Dolly Varden fishing in a large lake setting. The lake is the source of the Stamp River system.

14. Sproat Lake

Port Alberni

Sproat Lake offers rainbow trout and kokanee fishing near Port Alberni. The lake's warm waters also support bass.

15. Cowichan Lake

Cowichan Valley

Cowichan Lake offers rainbow trout, cutthroat trout, and kokanee fishing in the Cowichan Valley.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fishing.gov.bc.ca. A salmon conservation stamp is required for salmon fishing.
  • Try Campbell River -- Campbell River is the salmon fishing capital of Canada. July through September offers the best Chinook fishing.
  • Watch the tides -- Vancouver Island saltwater fishing is tide-dependent. Incoming tides produce the best fishing.
  • Book charters -- For Vancouver Island saltwater fishing, chartered trips are the most productive.
  • Fish the rivers in fall -- September through November offers the best salmon and steelhead fishing on Vancouver Island rivers.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
