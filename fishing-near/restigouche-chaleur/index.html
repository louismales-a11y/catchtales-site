Fishing in Restigouche-Chaleur & Northern New Brunswick

Restigouche River Atlantic salmon, Nepisiguit River brook trout, Chaleur Bay sea-run trout, and northern NB wilderness fishing.

1. Restigouche River (Atlantic Salmon)

Restigouche area, Campbellton region

The Restigouche River is one of the world's premier Atlantic salmon rivers. This legendary river forms the border with Quebec and offers world-class salmon fishing with fish in the 10-25 pound range. The river flows into Chaleur Bay at Dalhousie.

2. Upsalquitch River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Upsalquitch area

The Upsalquitch is a major tributary of the Restigouche, offering excellent Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing. Known for its productive pools and scenic wilderness setting, it's a favorite among serious salmon anglers.

3. Nepisiguit River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Nepisiguit area, Bathurst region

The Nepisiguit River is one of New Brunswick's finest brook trout and Atlantic salmon rivers. Flowing through the Acadian Peninsula near Bathurst, it offers excellent fishing for both species with good public access.

4. Patapedia River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Patapedia area

The Patapedia River is a remote tributary of the Restigouche offering outstanding Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in a wilderness setting. Access is primarily by ATV or helicopter.

5. Kedgwick River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Kedgwick area

The Kedgwick River is another major Restigouche tributary offering excellent Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing. This scenic river flows through the Kedgwick region of northern New Brunswick.

6. Little Main Restigouche River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Kedgwick area

The Little Main Restigouche is a major headwater branch of the Restigouche system, offering Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in a remote wilderness area.

7. Matapedia River (NB reach)

Matapedia area, Quebec border

The Matapedia River joins the Restigouche at the Quebec border, offering Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing. The NB reach provides excellent wading opportunities.

8. Jacquet River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Jacquet River area

The Jacquet River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing along the Chaleur Bay coast between Bathurst and Campbellton. This productive river has good public access.

9. Charlo River (Arctic Char, Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at gnb.ca/fishing. A separate Atlantic salmon conservation licence is required for the Restigouche system.
  • Fish the Restigouche in summer -- Late June through August offers the best Atlantic salmon fishing on the Restigouche River.
  • Book a camp -- The Restigouche system has many private salmon camps and lodges. Book well in advance for prime summer weeks.
  • Try the Nepisiguit for trout -- The Nepisiguit River is one of the best brook trout rivers in the Maritimes, with both resident and sea-run populations.
  • Check access rules -- Some waters on the Restigouche system are privately leased. Check access rules before planning a trip.
