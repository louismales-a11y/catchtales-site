Fishing in Western Kansas

Cedar Bluff Reservoir, Webster Reservoir, Kirwin Reservoir, Norton Lake, and the prairie reservoirs of western Kansas.

1. Cedar Bluff Reservoir (Wiper, Walleye, Crappie, Channel Catfish, White Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Cedar Bluff, Trego County

Cedar Bluff Reservoir is a 6,800-acre lake offering excellent wiper and walleye fishing in west-central Kansas.

2. Webster Reservoir (Walleye, Wiper, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish)

Webster Reservoir, Rooks County

Webster Reservoir is a 3,700-acre lake offering good walleye and wiper fishing.

3. Kirwin Reservoir (Wiper, Walleye, Crappie, Channel Catfish, White Bass)

Kirwin Reservoir, Phillips County

Kirwin Reservoir is a 5,100-acre lake offering good wiper and walleye fishing.

4. Norton Lake (Walleye, Wiper, Crappie, Channel Catfish, White Bass)

Norton Lake, Norton County

Norton Lake offers good fishing in northwest Kansas.

5. Lovewell Reservoir (Wiper, Walleye, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish)

Lovewell Reservoir, Jewell County

Lovewell Reservoir is a 3,000-acre lake offering good wiper and walleye fishing.

6. Webster State Park (Walleye, Wiper, Crappie, Catfish)

Webster SP, Rooks County

Webster State Park offers good fishing on Webster Reservoir.

7. Cedar Bluff State Park (Wiper, Walleye, Crappie)

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at ksoutdoors.com.
  • Fish Cedar Bluff -- Cedar Bluff Reservoir offers excellent wiper fishing in western Kansas.
  • Try Kirwin Reservoir -- Kirwin offers good walleye and wiper fishing in north-central Kansas.
  • County lakes -- Western Kansas has numerous county lakes offering good family fishing.
  • Watch for wind -- Western Kansas is very windy. Fish early morning for calmer conditions.
