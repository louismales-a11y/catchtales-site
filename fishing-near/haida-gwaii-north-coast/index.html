Fishing in Haida Gwaii & the North Coast

Remote wilderness salmon and halibut fishing in one of BC's last frontiers. Langara Island, Masset, Prince Rupert.

1. Langara Island

Haida Gwaii

Langara Island, off the northern tip of Haida Gwaii, is world-famous for its huge Chinook salmon averaging 25-40 pounds. The island's lodges offer all-inclusive salmon fishing packages.

2. Masset & Graham Island

Haida Gwaii

The waters around Masset offer excellent Chinook, coho, and halibut fishing. The Yakoun River and other streams offer salmon and steelhead.

3. Prince Rupert Area

Prince Rupert

Prince Rupert offers excellent saltwater fishing for salmon and halibut. The Skeena River mouth and Chatham Sound are productive.

4. Skeena River Mouth

Near Prince Rupert

The Skeena River mouth offers world-class Chinook and coho salmon fishing. The river's massive flow creates nutrient-rich waters.

5. Kitimat Area

Kitimat

The Kitimat area offers excellent salmon and halibut fishing in Douglas Channel and the Kitimat River.

6. Stewart (Portland Canal)

Stewart

The Stewart area offers excellent salmon and halibut fishing in the Portland Canal fjord. The Bear Glacier provides a stunning backdrop.

7. Queen Charlotte City

Haida Gwaii

Queen Charlotte City offers salmon and halibut fishing in Skidegate Inlet. The surrounding waters are productive.

8. Sandspit (Moresby Island)

Haida Gwaii

Sandspit offers excellent fishing in the southern Haida Gwaii waters. Coho salmon and halibut are abundant.

9. Hecate Strait

Between Haida Gwaii and mainland

Hecate Strait offers world-class halibut and salmon fishing. These open waters are known for large fish but require careful weather planning.

10. Dixon Entrance

North of Haida Gwaii

The Dixon Entrance offers excellent salmon and halibut fishing where the Pacific meets the Hecate Strait.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fishing.gov.bc.ca. A salmon conservation stamp is required.
  • Book months in advance -- Langara Island lodges book years in advance for the summer season.
  • Watch the weather -- Haida Gwaii and the north coast are exposed to Pacific storms. Check marine forecasts carefully.
  • Bring rain gear -- The north coast is one of the wettest regions in Canada. Quality rain gear is essential.
  • Respect the communities -- Haida Gwaii is the ancestral home of the Haida Nation. Respect local protocols and cultural sites.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
