Fishing on the Big Island (Hawaii)

Kona, Hilo, Kohala Coast, and the Big Island's world-class marlin and offshore fishing.

1. Kona Coast (Blue Marlin, Black Marlin, Ahi, Ono, Mahi-Mahi)

Kona, west Big Island

The Kona Coast is world-famous for blue marlin fishing. Kona hosts the International Billfish Tournament and offers year-round offshore fishing at its best.

2. Hilo Area (Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Skipjack, Papio, Bonefish)

Hilo, east Big Island

The Hilo area offers good offshore and inshore fishing on the rainy east side of the Big Island.

3. Kohala Coast (Blue Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Ono)

Kohala Coast, northwest Big Island

The Kohala Coast offers excellent offshore fishing from the resorts of the Big Island's luxury coast.

4. Puako Reef (Bonefish, Papio, Trevally, Reef Fish)

Puako, Kohala Coast

Puako offers excellent bonefish and reef fishing in shallow flats.

5. South Point (Ulua, Papio, Bonefish, Giant Trevally)

South Point (Ka Lae), southernmost Big Island

South Point is the southernmost point in the US and offers excellent shore fishing for ulua and papio from the rugged coastline.

6. Kealakekua Bay (Bonefish, Papio, Goatfish, Reef Fish)

Kealakekua Bay, Kona Coast

Kealakekua Bay offers good inshore fishing in a historic bay and marine sanctuary.

7. Honokohau Harbor (Blue Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi, Ono)

Honokohau Harbor, Kona

Honokohau Harbor is the main charter fishing port on the Big Island, offering access to world-class offshore fishing.

8. Kailua Pier (Papio, Bonefish, Mullet, Goatfish)

Kailua-Kona, Big Island

The Kailua Pier offers convenient shore fishing in the heart of Kailua-Kona.

9. Punalu'u Black Sand Beach (Papio, Bonefish, Trevally)

Punalu'u, south Big Island

Punalu'u offers good shore fishing at a famous black sand beach.

10. Waipi'o Valley Coast (Ulua, Papio, Trevally)

Waipi'o Valley, Hamakua Coast

The Waipi'o Valley coastline offers remote shore fishing below the iconic valley.

11. Volcano Area Freshwater (O'opu, Native Fish)

Volcano area, Big Island

Hawaii's only native freshwater fish, the o'opu, can be found in some streams near Volcano National Park.

12. Kawaihae Harbor (Yellowfin Tuna, Mahi-Mahi, Ono)

Kawaihae, Kohala Coast

Kawaihae Harbor offers charter access to the Kohala Coast offshore fishing grounds.

13. Halape (Ulua, Trevally, Bonefish)

Halape, south Big Island

Halape offers remote shore fishing accessible only by hiking in Hawaii Volcanoes National Park.

14. Miloli'i (Papio, Ulua, Bonefish)

Miloli'i, south Kona Coast

Miloli'i is a traditional Hawaiian fishing village offering excellent shore fishing.

15. Mahukona (Papio, Ulua, Trevally)

Mahukona, Kohala Coast

Mahukona offers good shore fishing near an old sugar plantation landing.

16. Keauhou Bay (Blue Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi)

Keauhou, Kona Coast

Keauhou Bay offers charter fishing access.

17. Honokohau Marina (Blue Marlin, Ahi, Mahi-Mahi)

Honokohau, Kona

Honokohau Marina is a major charter port.

18. Kailua Pier (Papio, Bonefish, Goatfish)

Kailua-Kona, Big Island

Kailua Pier offers convenient shore fishing.

19. Laupahoehoe Point (Ulua, Papio, Trevally)

Laupahoehoe, Hamakua Coast

Laupahoehoe offers good shore fishing.

20. Kapoho Tidepools (Reef Fish, Tropical Fish)

Kapoho, Puna District

The Kapoho tidepools offer unique fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- No recreational saltwater licence required in Hawaii.
  • Fish Kona for marlin -- Kona is one of the best places in the world to catch blue marlin. Book a charter in advance.
  • Try shore casting -- The Big Island's rocky coastline offers excellent ulua fishing for shore anglers.
  • Best season -- Summer (May-September) is peak marlin season, but fishing is good year-round.
  • Book a Kona charter -- Kona has one of the largest sportfishing fleets in the Pacific.
