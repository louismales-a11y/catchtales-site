Fishing in Northeast Ontario & Algoma

Sudbury, Algoma, Chapleau, Wawa -- brook trout, walleye, pike, and lake trout in Ontario's boreal shield.

7. Lake Nipissing (Sturgeon)

North Bay / Sturgeon Falls area

Lake Nipissing is a massive, shallow lake covering over 870 square kilometres, located between Sudbury and North Bay near Sturgeon Falls. The lake is incredibly productive due to its shallow average depth of just 15 feet, which allows sunlight to reach the bottom across much of the lake, creating rich aquatic vegetation and abundant baitfish populations.

The lake is famous for its excellent walleye and northern pike fishing. Walleye in the 2-5 pound range are common, with larger fish available for anglers who work the deeper channels and river mouths. The lake's stained water means walleye are more accessible during daylight hours than in clearer lakes, making it an excellent choice for anglers who prefer fishing during the day.

Northern pike are abundant throughout the lake, with fish in the 5-15 pound range common. The weedy bays and shallow flats provide excellent pike habitat. Yellow perch are also abundant and provide excellent family fishing, particularly in summer when perch school in 15-25 feet of water.

Lake Nipissing is also one of Ontario's premier ice fishing destinations. The lake's ice fishing season typically runs from January through March, with thousands of ice huts set up each winter. Walleye and perch are the primary targets through the ice. The lake has numerous ice hut operators who offer heated huts, equipment rentals, and guiding services.

Key access points include North Bay, Callander, Sturgeon Falls, Cache Bay, and the French River outlet. The lake has dozens of boat launches, marinas, and resorts. The West Arm of Lake Nipissing, stretching toward Sturgeon Falls, offers excellent fishing with more protected waters than the main lake basin. Anglers should note that parts of Lake Nipissing are within the Dokis First Nation territory, where a separate permit may be required for fishing certain areas.

8. Temagami Region (Brook Trout, Lake Trout, Walleye)

Temagami

The Temagami region is one of Ontario's premier wilderness fishing destinations. Lake Temagami itself covers 1,900 square kilometres with over 1,200 islands and countless kilometres of shoreline. The region's clear, deep shield lakes offer exceptional lake trout, brook trout, and walleye fishing.

Lake Temagami is particularly famous for its lake trout, with fish in the 5-12 pound range common in the deep, cold basins. The countless smaller lakes in the region offer excellent brook trout and walleye fishing with minimal fishing pressure.

The region is accessible via Highway 11 and the town of Temagami, with numerous lodges, outfitters, and canoe routes. The Lady Evelyn River and Sturgeon River systems offer excellent river fishing. Temagami's old-growth red pine forests and pristine waters make it one of the most beautiful fishing destinations in Ontario.

17. Lac aux Sables (Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye)

Near Noelville, between Sudbury and North Bay

Lac aux Sables is a large, clear lake near Sturgeon Falls, sitting right between Sudbury and North Bay on Highway 17. The lake offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass. The lake's clear water and rocky structure provide ideal habitat, with walleye holding in 15-25 feet of water during summer. The lake has several cottage developments, campgrounds, and boat launches. The nearby Sturgeon River also offers additional fishing opportunities.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Try brook trout -- Algoma offers some of the best brook trout fishing in Ontario.
  • Explore the Chapleau Preserve -- The Chapleau Crown Game Preserve offers remote, untouched fishing.
  • Bring insect repellent -- Algoma has intense mosquitoes and black flies.
  • Book canoe trips -- The Spanish and Mississagi rivers offer excellent multi-day canoe fishing.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
