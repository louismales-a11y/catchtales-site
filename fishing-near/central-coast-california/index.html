Fishing in Central Coast & Bay Area

San Francisco Bay, Monterey Bay, Half Moon Bay, Santa Cruz, and the productive saltwater of California's central coast.

1. San Francisco Bay (Halibut, Striped Bass, Leopard Shark, Bat Ray)

San Francisco Bay, multiple counties

San Francisco Bay offers excellent saltwater fishing for halibut, striped bass, leopard sharks, and bat rays from piers, shorelines, and boats.

2. Half Moon Bay (Rockfish, Lingcod, Chinook Salmon, Halibut)

Half Moon Bay, San Mateo County

Half Moon Bay is a premier saltwater fishing port offering rockfish, lingcod, salmon, and halibut fishing in the Pacific Ocean.

3. Monterey Bay (Rockfish, Lingcod, Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Squid)

Monterey Bay, Monterey/Santa Cruz counties

Monterey Bay offers excellent saltwater fishing from charter boats and the famous Monterey Municipal Wharf.

4. Santa Cruz (Rockfish, Lingcod, Halibut, Chinook Salmon)

Santa Cruz, Santa Cruz County

Santa Cruz offers excellent saltwater fishing from the Santa Cruz Wharf and charter boats.

5. Bodega Bay (Rockfish, Lingcod, Chinook Salmon, Halibut)

Bodega Bay, Sonoma County

Bodega Bay offers excellent saltwater fishing north of San Francisco.

6. Pacifica Pier (Surf Perch, Rockfish, Kingfish)

Pacifica, San Mateo County

The Pacifica Pier offers excellent pier fishing for surf perch and kingfish.

7. Fisherman's Wharf (San Francisco) (Halibut, Striped Bass, Crabs)

Fisherman's Wharf, San Francisco

Fisherman's Wharf offers charter fishing for halibut and striped bass in San Francisco Bay.

8. Moss Landing (Halibut, Striped Bass, Rockfish)

Moss Landing, Monterey County

Moss Landing is a major fishing port offering excellent saltwater fishing in Monterey Bay.

9. Morro Bay (Rockfish, Lingcod, Halibut, Surf Perch)

Morro Bay, San Luis Obispo County

Morro Bay offers excellent saltwater fishing around the iconic Morro Rock.

10. Avila Beach (Rockfish, Lingcod, Halibut)

Avila Beach, San Luis Obispo County

Avila Beach offers good saltwater fishing from the pier and charter boats.

11. Pismo Beach (Surf Perch, Halibut, Croaker)

Pismo Beach, San Luis Obispo County

Pismo Beach offers excellent surf fishing from its famous beach for surf perch and halibut.

12. Stearns Wharf (Santa Barbara) (Rockfish, Halibut, Mackerel)

Stearns Wharf, Santa Barbara

Stearns Wharf offers excellent pier fishing for mackerel and halibut.

13. Tomales Bay (Halibut, Leopard Shark, Bat Ray, Oysters)

Tomales Bay, Marin County

Tomales Bay offers good halibut and shark fishing in a unique estuary setting.

14. San Pablo Bay (Striped Bass, Sturgeon, Halibut)

San Pablo Bay, Marin/Solano counties

San Pablo Bay offers excellent sturgeon and striped bass fishing in the northern Bay Area.

15. Lake Del Valle (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass)

Lake Del Valle, Alameda County

Lake Del Valle offers trout and bass fishing in the Livermore area of the East Bay.

16. Capitola Wharf (Rockfish, Halibut, Mackerel)

Capitola, Santa Cruz County

The Capitola Wharf offers good pier fishing.

17. Monterey Wharf (Rockfish, Halibut, Squid)

Monterey, Monterey County

Monterey Wharf offers excellent pier fishing.

18. San Simeon Pier (Rockfish, Halibut, Surf Perch)

San Simeon, San Luis Obispo County

San Simeon Pier offers good fishing.

19. Cayucos Pier (Rockfish, Halibut, Surf Perch)

Cayucos, San Luis Obispo County

Cayucos Pier offers good fishing.

20. Malibu Pier (Mackerel, Halibut, Surf Perch)

Malibu, Los Angeles County

Malibu Pier offers good coastal fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at wildlife.ca.gov. Saltwater fishing requires an additional Ocean Enhancement Validation.
  • Book a Bay Area charter -- Half Moon Bay, San Francisco, and Monterey have numerous charter operators.
  • Pier fishing -- California's coastal piers offer excellent family fishing without a boat. Most piers don't require a fishing licence.
  • Check rockfish seasons -- Rockfish seasons are regulated and can change annually. Check current regulations.
  • Watch for fog -- The central coast is famous for summer fog. Morning fishing is often best before the fog burns off.
