Fishing in the Monadnock & Southwest Region

Spofford Lake, Thorndike Pond, Ashuelot River, Contoocook River, and the largemouth bass, chain pickerel, rainbow trout, and brown trout fishing of southwestern New Hampshire.

1. Spofford Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Crappie, Yellow Perch)

Spofford Lake, Cheshire County

Spofford Lake is a 464-acre lake near Chesterfield at the base of Mount Monadnock, offering excellent warmwater fishing for bass and panfish with a public boat launch and state park.

2. Thorndike Pond (Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Crappie, Bluegill)

Thorndike Pond, Cheshire County

Thorndike Pond is a 250-acre lake in Jaffrey offering excellent largemouth bass and chain pickerel fishing at the foot of Mount Monadnock.

3. Ashuelot River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Fallfish)

Ashuelot River, Cheshire County

The Ashuelot River flows 60 miles through southwestern New Hampshire to the Connecticut River, offering excellent trout and smallmouth bass fishing with numerous public access points through Keene.

4. Contoocook River (northern) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Fallfish)

Contoocook River, Hillsborough/Merrimack counties

The upper Contoocook River flows through southwest New Hampshire offering excellent trout fishing in a scenic valley with state fish hatchery releases.

5. Nubanusit Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, White Perch)

Nubanusit Lake, Cheshire County

Nubanusit Lake is a 590-acre lake in Nelson offering excellent rainbow and brook trout fishing in a remote setting at 1,400 feet elevation.

6. Dublin Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel)

Dublin Lake, Cheshire County

Dublin Lake is a 172-acre lake in the town of Dublin offering good trout and bass fishing in a scenic Monadnock region setting.

7. Highland Lake (Stoddard) (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, White Perch)

Highland Lake, Cheshire County

Highland Lake is a 420-acre lake in Stoddard offering excellent trout fishing in the Monadnock highlands with a public boat launch.

8. Granite Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, White Perch)

Granite Lake, Cheshire County

9. Wilson Lake (Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Rainbow Trout, Crappie, Bluegill)

Wilson Lake, Cheshire County

10. Glen Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel)

11. Minnewawa Lake (Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Rainbow Trout, Crappie, Bluegill)

Minnewawa Lake is a 75-acre lake in Marlborough offering good warmwater fishing in the Ashuelot River watershed.

12. Halfmoon Pond (Hancock) (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Chain Pickerel)

Fishing Tips for the Monadnock & Southwest Region

  • Check local regulations before fishing — each water body may have specific rules on limits, seasons, and bait.
  • Spring and fall typically offer the best fishing in New Hampshire for most species.
  • Consider hiring a local guide for the best experience on unfamiliar waters.
  • Always carry a valid New Hampshire fishing license — available online through the state wildlife agency.
  • Practice catch and release for sensitive species to help maintain healthy fish populations.
