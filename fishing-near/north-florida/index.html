Fishing in North Florida

St. Johns River, Jacksonville, Gainesville, Suwannee River, and the rich waters of northern Florida.

1. St. Johns River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Striped Bass, Catfish)

St. Johns River, multiple counties

The St. Johns River is one of Florida's most famous bass fisheries, flowing 310 miles north to Jacksonville. The river offers world-class largemouth bass and crappie fishing.

2. Lake George (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Lake George, Volusia/Putnam counties

Lake George is the second-largest lake in Florida at 46,000 acres, offering excellent bass and crappie fishing on the St. Johns River.

3. Rodman Reservoir (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Rodman Reservoir, Putnam County

Rodman Reservoir is a 9,500-acre impoundment offering excellent bass fishing on the Ocklawaha River.

4. Lake Woodruff (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Lake Woodruff, Volusia County

Lake Woodruff offers good bass and panfish fishing in a National Wildlife Refuge.

5. Suwannee River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish, Striped Bass)

Suwannee River, multiple counties

The Suwannee River offers excellent blackwater river fishing for bass, bream, and catfish.

6. Santa Fe River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish)

Santa Fe River, Alachua/Gilchrist counties

The Santa Fe River offers good fishing with numerous springs.

7. Orange Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Orange Lake, Alachua County

Orange Lake offers excellent bass fishing near Gainesville.

8. Lochloosa Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Lochloosa Lake, Alachua County

Lochloosa Lake offers good bass and crappie fishing near Orange Lake.

9. Newnans Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Newnans Lake, Alachua County

Newnans Lake offers good fishing near Gainesville.

10. Lake Santa Fe (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Shellcracker)

Lake Santa Fe, Alachua County

Lake Santa Fe offers excellent panfish and bass fishing.

11. Jacksonville Beach (Whiting, Pompano, Redfish, Flounder)

Jacksonville Beach, Duval County

Jacksonville Beach offers excellent surf fishing for whiting, pompano, and redfish.

12. Mayport (Flounder, Redfish, Speckled Trout, Tarpon)

Mayport, Duval County

Mayport at the mouth of the St. Johns River offers excellent inshore and nearshore fishing.

13. St. Augustine Inlet (Redfish, Speckled Trout, Flounder, Tarpon)

St. Augustine, St. Johns County

St. Augustine offers excellent inshore fishing around the oldest city in the US.

14. Matanzas Inlet (Redfish, Speckled Trout, Flounder, Tarpon)

Matanzas Inlet, St. Johns County

Matanzas Inlet offers good fishing near St. Augustine.

15. Lake City Area Lakes (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Lake City, Columbia County

The Lake City area has numerous small lakes offering good freshwater fishing.

16. Lake Jackson (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Lake Jackson, Leon County

Lake Jackson offers good fishing near Tallahassee.

17. Lake Iamonia (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Lake Iamonia, Leon County

Lake Iamonia offers good fishing.

18. Lake Miccosukee (Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Lake Miccosukee, Jefferson County

Lake Miccosukee offers good fishing.

19. Lake Talquin (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Striped Bass)

Lake Talquin, Leon/Gadsden counties

Lake Talquin offers excellent fishing.

20. Ochlockonee River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish)

Ochlockonee River, Wakulla County

The Ochlockonee River offers good fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at myfwc.com.
  • Fish the St. Johns River -- The St. Johns River is legendary for largemouth bass fishing. Spring and fall are peak.
  • Try Rodman Reservoir -- Rodman Reservoir is consistently ranked among Florida's top bass lakes.
  • Spring crappie -- The St. Johns River chain offers excellent crappie fishing in late winter and spring.
  • Inshore fishing -- The Jacksonville and St. Augustine areas offer excellent redfish and speckled trout fishing.
