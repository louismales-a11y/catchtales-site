Fishing in Central Missouri & Lake of the Ozarks

Lake of the Ozarks, Osage River, Pomme de Terre Lake, Niangua River, and the bass, crappie, catfish, and walleye fishing of central Missouri.

1. Lake of the Ozarks (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, Walleye, Bluegill)

Lake of the Ozarks, Camden/Benton/Morgan/Miller counties

Lake of the Ozarks is a 54,000-acre reservoir with 1,150 miles of shoreline offering world-class bass, crappie, and catfish fishing with numerous fishing docks and resorts.

2. Pomme de Terre Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Walleye, Bluegill)

Pomme de Terre Lake, Hickory/Polk counties

Pomme de Terre Lake is a 7,800-acre reservoir offering excellent bass, crappie, and walleye fishing with good shore access.

3. Niangua River (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Rainbow Trout)

Niangua River, Dallas/Laclede counties

The Niangua River offers excellent float fishing for smallmouth bass and is stocked with rainbow trout below Bennett Spring State Park.

4. Osage River (Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, Blue Catfish, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass)

Osage River, Benton/Miller counties

The Osage River below Bagnell Dam offers excellent catfish fishing with good access points.

5. Truman Lake (East) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Blue Catfish, Walleye)

Truman Lake (east), Benton/Hickory counties

The eastern portion of Truman Lake near the Osage River arm offers excellent crappie and catfish fishing.

6. Bagnell Dam Tailwaters (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Walleye, White Bass, Channel Catfish)

Bagnell Dam tailwaters, Miller County

The cold water released from Bagnell Dam creates a put-and-take trout fishery below Lake of the Ozarks.

7. Little Niangua River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish)

Little Niangua River, Camden/Dallas counties

The Little Niangua River offers good float fishing for smallmouth bass.

8. Gravois Arm (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Gravois Arm, Morgan County

The Gravois Arm of Lake of the Ozarks offers excellent fishing with less boat traffic.

9. Grand Glaize Arm (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Grand Glaize Arm, Camden County

The Grand Glaize Arm offers excellent fishing near Osage Beach with good public access.

10. Niangua Arm (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Niangua Arm, Camden County

The Niangua Arm of Lake of the Ozarks offers excellent fishing with many coves and creeks.

11. Big Bend of the Osage (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, White Bass)

Big Bend, St. Clair County

The Big Bend area of the Osage River offers good fishing.

12. Lake of the Ozarks State Park (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake of the Ozarks SP, Camden County

Lake of the Ozarks State Park offers excellent shore fishing access with multiple fishing docks.

13. Ha Ha Tonka Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Ha Ha Tonka Lake offers good fishing in a unique park setting.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at mdc.mo.gov. Trout stamp required for trout fishing.
  • Fish Lake of the Ozarks -- Lake of the Ozarks offers world-class bass and crappie fishing with 1,150 miles of shoreline to explore.
  • Try Pomme de Terre -- Pomme de Terre Lake offers excellent walleye fishing.
  • Float the Niangua -- The Niangua River is one of Missouri's best float fishing streams for smallmouth bass.
  • Spring prespawn is best -- April-May offers the best crappie and bass fishing of the year.
