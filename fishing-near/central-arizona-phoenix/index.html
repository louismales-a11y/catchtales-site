Fishing in Central Arizona & Phoenix Area

Roosevelt Lake, Apache Lake, Canyon Lake, Saguaro Lake, Bartlett Lake, and the Salt River chain.

1. Roosevelt Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Flathead Catfish, Tilapia)

Roosevelt area, Tonto National Forest

Roosevelt Lake is Arizona's largest lake at over 21,000 acres. Known for excellent largemouth bass fishing, the lake also holds crappie, flathead catfish, and tilapia. The underwater brush and standing timber provide excellent fish habitat.

2. Apache Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Sunfish)

Apache Lake, Salt River chain

Apache Lake is a narrow, 2,600-acre reservoir on the Salt River offering excellent largemouth bass and crappie fishing. The lake's steep rocky shoreline provides unique structure.

3. Canyon Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, Sunfish)

Canyon Lake, Salt River chain

Canyon Lake is a scenic 950-acre reservoir surrounded by steep canyon walls. The lake offers good largemouth and smallmouth bass fishing in a stunning desert setting.

4. Saguaro Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Bass, Sunfish)

Saguaro Lake, Salt River chain

Saguaro Lake is a 1,200-acre reservoir near Mesa offering excellent bass and crappie fishing. The lake's proximity to Phoenix makes it one of the most popular fishing spots in Arizona.

5. Bartlett Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Sunfish)

Bartlett Lake, Verde River

Bartlett Lake is a 2,800-acre reservoir on the Verde River offering excellent largemouth bass and crappie fishing. The lake's numerous coves and brushy shoreline provide prime habitat.

6. Lake Pleasant (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Lake Pleasant, north of Phoenix

Lake Pleasant is a 10,000-acre reservoir on the Agua Fria River offering excellent striped bass and largemouth bass fishing. The lake is one of the most popular boating and fishing destinations in Arizona.

7. Horseshoe Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Sunfish)

Horseshoe Lake, Verde River

Horseshoe Lake is a 1,300-acre reservoir on the Verde River offering good largemouth bass and crappie fishing in a quieter setting than Bartlett Lake.

8. Tempe Town Lake (Largemouth Bass, Rainbow Trout, Sunfish)

Tempe, urban lake

Tempe Town Lake is a 2-mile-long urban lake in downtown Tempe offering stocked rainbow trout in winter and largemouth bass year-round. Easily accessible from the Phoenix metro area.

9. Papago Park Ponds (Rainbow Trout, Sunfish, Channel Catfish)

Papago Park, Phoenix

Papago Park's ponds are stocked with rainbow trout in winter and offer channel catfish and sunfish fishing in a scenic urban park setting.

10. Alvord Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Catfish)

Alvord Lake, Cibola National Forest

Alvord Lake offers good rainbow trout and largemouth bass fishing in the Cibola National Forest west of Phoenix.

11. Deadhorse Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass)

Deadhorse Lake, near Flagstaff

Deadhorse Lake offers good rainbow trout fishing in a small lake setting along the Interstate 17 corridor north of Phoenix.

12. Lower Lake Mary (Northern Pike, Rainbow Trout, Crappie)

Lower Lake Mary, near Flagstaff

Lower Lake Mary offers northern pike, rainbow trout, and crappie fishing near Flagstaff. The lake is one of the few pike fisheries in Arizona.

13. Upper Lake Mary (Northern Pike, Rainbow Trout, Yellow Perch)

Upper Lake Mary, near Flagstaff

Upper Lake Mary offers northern pike, rainbow trout, and yellow perch fishing in a high-elevation lake south of Flagstaff.

14. Verde River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Catfish)

Verde River, central Arizona

The Verde River offers excellent smallmouth bass and catfish fishing in a scenic desert river setting. The river flows through Tonto National Forest and the Verde Valley.

15. Salt River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Catfish)

Salt River, Tonto National Forest

The Salt River below the dam chain offers good smallmouth bass and catfish fishing in the lower Salt River canyon.

16. Agua Fria River (Largemouth Bass, Catfish, Sunfish)

Agua Fria River, north of Phoenix

The Agua Fria River offers good bass and catfish fishing in its lower reaches north of Phoenix.

17. New River (Catfish, Sunfish, Largemouth Bass)

New River, north of Phoenix

The New River offers good channel catfish and sunfish fishing in the desert north of Phoenix.

18. Gila River (Catfish, Largemouth Bass, Sunfish)

Gila River, central/southern Arizona

The Gila River offers channel catfish and bass fishing in its lower reaches through central Arizona.

19. Cortez Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Catfish)

Cortez Lake, near Flagstaff

Cortez Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in a small lake north of Flagstaff.

20. Watson Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie)

Watson Lake, Prescott area

Watson Lake offers good largemouth and smallmouth bass fishing in the Prescott area with unique granite rock formations.

21. Goldwater Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Sunfish)

Goldwater Lake, Prescott

Goldwater Lake offers stocked rainbow trout in winter and good bass fishing in a scenic Prescott mountain lake.

22. Lynx Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Lynx Lake, Prescott area

Lynx Lake offers excellent rainbow trout and largemouth bass fishing in the Prescott National Forest.

23. Mingus Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass)

Mingus Lake, Cottonwood area

Mingus Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Mingus Mountain area near Cottonwood.

24. Granite Basin Lake (Rainbow Trout, Sunfish)

Granite Basin Lake, Prescott

Granite Basin Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in a scenic lake surrounded by the Granite Dells near Prescott.

25. Verde Valley Lakes (Multiple Species)

Verde Valley, Cottonwood/Clarkdale area

The various small lakes in the Verde Valley offer good fishing for stocked trout, bass, and catfish in a scenic red rock setting.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at azgfd.gov. Non-resident licences available.
  • Fish Roosevelt in spring -- March through May offers the best bass fishing on Roosevelt Lake before the summer heat.
  • Try the Salt River chain -- The four Salt River lakes (Roosevelt, Apache, Canyon, Saguaro) offer diverse fishing within 2 hours of Phoenix.
  • Winter trout stocking -- Many urban Phoenix lakes are stocked with rainbow trout from December through February.
  • Watch for heat -- Arizona summer temperatures can exceed 110F. Fish early morning or evening during summer months.
