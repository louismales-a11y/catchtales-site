Fishing in Eastern Missouri & St. Louis Area

Mississippi River, Meramec River, Busch Conservation Area, Cuivre River, and the bass, trout, catfish, and crappie fishing of eastern Missouri and the St. Louis area.

1. Mississippi River (St. Louis) (Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, Largemouth Bass, White Bass, Sauger, Crappie)

Mississippi River, St. Louis City/County

The Mississippi River through the St. Louis area offers world-class blue catfish and flathead catfish fishing with access at multiple parks and ramps.

2. Meramec River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie, Rainbow Trout)

Meramec River, Franklin/St. Louis/Jefferson counties

The Meramec River is a premier Ozark float stream offering excellent smallmouth bass fishing with good public access and stocked rainbow trout near Maramec Spring.

3. Missouri River (St. Louis) (Blue Catfish, Channel Catfish, Flathead Catfish, White Bass, Sauger, Largemouth Bass)

Missouri River, St. Charles/St. Louis counties

The Missouri River near its confluence with the Mississippi offers excellent catfish fishing with multiple access points.

4. Busch Conservation Area (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Busch CA, St. Charles County

Busch Conservation Area offers excellent fishing in its 35 managed lakes with well-maintained fishing docks and shore access.

5. Cuivre River State Park (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Cuivre River SP, Lincoln County

Cuivre River State Park offers excellent fishing with a 200-acre lake and good shore access.

6. Creve Coeur Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

Creve Coeur Lake, St. Louis County

Creve Coeur Lake is a 320-acre oxbow lake offering excellent urban fishing in St. Louis County with a fishing dock.

7. Forest Park Lakes (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Rainbow Trout)

Forest Park, St. Louis City

Forest Park's multiple lakes offer excellent urban fishing including stocked rainbow trout in the winter.

8. Horseshoe Lake (St. Louis) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Horseshoe Lake, St. Louis County

Horseshoe Lake offers good urban fishing in the St. Louis area.

9. Carondelet Park Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Carondelet Park, St. Louis City

Carondelet Park Lake offers good urban fishing in south St. Louis.

10. Willmore Park Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Willmore Park, St. Louis City

Willmore Park Lake offers good urban fishing.

11. Tilles Park Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Tilles Park, St. Louis County

Tilles Park Lake offers good urban fishing.

12. Queeny Park Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Queeny Park, St. Louis County

Queeny Park Lake offers good fishing in west St. Louis County.

13. Simpson Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Simpson Lake, St. Louis County

Simpson Lake offers good fishing in Valley Park.

14. Lake 35 (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake 35, St. Charles County

Lake 35 offers good fishing near the Katy Trail.

15. Lake Saint Louis (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Saint Louis, St. Charles County

Lake Saint Louis offers good fishing in a residential community.

16. August A. Busch Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Redear Sunfish)

August A. Busch Lake #33, St. Charles County

Busch Lake #33 is one of the most popular managed lakes in the Busch Conservation Area system.

17. Maramec Spring Park (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Maramec Spring Park, Crawford County

Maramec Spring Park is one of Missouri's premier trout parks with a large spring-fed creek and hatchery.

18. Onondaga Cave State Park (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Onondaga Cave SP, Crawford County

Onondaga Cave State Park offers good fishing on the Meramec River.

19. Big River (St. Louis) (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie)

Big River, Jefferson/St. Francois counties

The Big River offers good smallmouth bass fishing south of St. Louis.

20. Bourbeuse River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish)

Bourbeuse River, Franklin/Gasconade counties

The Bourbeuse River offers good float fishing for smallmouth bass.

Cuivre River, Lincoln/St. Charles counties

The Cuivre River offers good fishing in eastern Missouri.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at mdc.mo.gov. Trout stamp required for trout fishing.
  • Fish the Mississippi River -- The Mississippi River through St. Louis offers excellent catfish and sauger fishing.
  • Try the Meramec River -- The Meramec is one of the best float fishing streams in Missouri for smallmouth bass.
  • Visit Busch Conservation Area -- Offers 35 managed lakes with excellent fishing docks and shore access.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-May and September-October offer the best fishing. Winter trout stocking in urban lakes.
