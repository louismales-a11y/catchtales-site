Fishing in Southern Illinois

Rend Lake, Carlyle Lake, Kinkaid Lake, Crab Orchard Lake, and the best fishing in southern Illinois.

1. Carlyle Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Walleye, White Bass, Bluegill)

Carlyle Lake, Clinton/Bond counties

Carlyle Lake is the largest lake in Illinois at 26,000 acres, offering world-class crappie, bass, and catfish fishing.

2. Rend Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill, Walleye)

Rend Lake, Franklin/Jefferson counties

Rend Lake is an 18,900-acre reservoir offering excellent bass, crappie, and catfish fishing in southern Illinois.

3. Kinkaid Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill, Striped Bass)

Kinkaid Lake, Jackson County

Kinkaid Lake is a 2,750-acre lake offering excellent striped bass and crappie fishing.

4. Crab Orchard Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish, Walleye)

Crab Orchard Lake, Williamson County

Crab Orchard Lake is a 6,965-acre reservoir offering excellent bass and crappie fishing in a wildlife refuge.

5. Little Grassy Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Little Grassy Lake, Jackson County

Little Grassy Lake offers good fishing near Carbondale in the Crab Orchard Refuge.

6. Devils Kitchen Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Devils Kitchen Lake, Williamson County

Devils Kitchen Lake offers good fishing in the Crab Orchard Refuge.

7. Lake Murphysboro (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Lake Murphysboro, Jackson County

Lake Murphysboro offers good fishing near Murphysboro.

8. Baldwin Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill)

Baldwin Lake, Randolph County

Baldwin Lake is a 2,000-acre cooling lake offering excellent fishing.

9. Mississippi River (Pools 24-26) (Walleye, Sauger, Catfish, Crappie, White Bass)

Mississippi River, Randolph/Jackson counties

The Mississippi River along southern Illinois offers excellent walleye and catfish fishing.

10. Ohio River (Largemouth Bass, Sauger, Catfish, Crappie, White Bass)

Ohio River, Hardin/Gallatin counties

The Ohio River along Illinois' southeastern border offers good sauger and catfish fishing.

11. Wabash River (Lower) (Catfish, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Bass)

Lower Wabash River, Edwards/Wabash counties

The lower Wabash River offers good fishing along the Indiana border.

12. Saline River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill)

Saline River, Gallatin/Saline counties

The Saline River offers good fishing in southern Illinois.

13. Big Muddy River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill)

Big Muddy River, Jackson/Perry counties

The Big Muddy River offers good fishing through southern Illinois.

14. Cache River (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill)

Cache River, Johnson/Pulaski counties

The Cache River offers unique swamp fishing in the Cache River Wetlands.

15. Lake Egypt (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bluegill)

Lake Egypt, Johnson County

Lake Egypt is a 2,200-acre reservoir offering good bass and crappie fishing.

16. Lake of Egypt (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Lake of Egypt, Johnson County

Lake of Egypt offers good fishing near Marion.

17. Cedar Lake (IL) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Cedar Lake, Jackson County

Cedar Lake offers good fishing near Carbondale.

18. Lake Du Quoin (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Lake Du Quoin, Perry County

Lake Du Quoin offers good fishing at the Du Quoin State Fairgrounds.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at dnr.illinois.gov.
  • Fish Carlyle Lake for crappie -- Carlyle Lake is one of the best crappie lakes in the Midwest.
  • Try Rend Lake -- Rend Lake offers excellent bass and crappie fishing with full-service marinas.
  • Kinkaid striped bass -- Kinkaid Lake is one of the few places in Illinois with hybrid striped bass.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-May and September-October offer the best fishing in southern Illinois.
