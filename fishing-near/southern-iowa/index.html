Fishing in Southern Iowa

Lake Rathbun, Lake Sugema, Lake Wapello, Chariton River, and the reservoirs and rivers of southern Iowa.

1. Rathbun Reservoir (Walleye, Crappie, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Saugeye, Bluegill)

Rathbun Reservoir, Appanoose/Wayne counties

Rathbun Reservoir is an 11,000-acre lake offering excellent walleye and crappie fishing in southern Iowa.

2. Lake Sugema (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish, Walleye)

Lake Sugema, Van Buren County

Lake Sugema offers good bass and crappie fishing near Keosauqua.

3. Lake Wapello (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Channel Catfish)

Lake Wapello, Davis County

Lake Wapello offers good fishing at Lake Wapello State Park.

4. Chariton River (Channel Catfish, Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at iowadnr.gov/fishing.
  • Fish Rathbun Lake -- Rathbun Reservoir is one of Iowa's best walleye and crappie lakes.
  • Try Lake Sugema -- Lake Sugema offers excellent bass and crappie fishing in a scenic setting.
  • County lakes -- Southern Iowa has numerous county conservation lakes offering good family fishing.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-June and September-October offer the best fishing in southern Iowa.
