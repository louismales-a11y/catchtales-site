Fishing in Central Alabama

Lake Martin, Coosa River, Lay Lake, Mitchell Lake, and the heart of Alabama's bass fishing country.

1. Lake Martin (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Striped Bass, Crappie)

Alexander City / Dadeville area

Lake Martin is a 44,000-acre reservoir on the Tallapoosa River, known for its clear waters and excellent bass fishing. The lake holds largemouth bass, spotted bass, and a healthy striped bass population. The rocky points and creek channels provide prime fishing.

2. Coosa River (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass)

Central Alabama, multiple dams

The Coosa River system is famous for its spotted bass fishery, one of the best in the country. The river's chain of reservoirs from Weiss to Mitchell Lake offers diverse fishing for bass, striped bass, and catfish.

3. Lay Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Birmingham / Columbiana area

Lay Lake is a 12,000-acre Coosa River reservoir known for excellent spotted bass fishing. The lake has hosted multiple bass tournaments and offers healthy populations of largemouth bass and crappie.

4. Mitchell Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Mitchell area, Coosa River

Mitchell Lake is a 5,850-acre Coosa River reservoir offering good spotted bass and largemouth bass fishing. The lake's clear waters and rocky structure provide excellent habitat for bass.

5. Jordan Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Wetumpka area, Coosa River

Jordan Lake is a 6,800-acre reservoir on the Coosa River offering excellent spotted bass and largemouth bass fishing. The lake's diverse habitat includes creek arms, points, and submerged structure.

6. Bouldin Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Coosa River, below Jordan Dam

Bouldin Lake is a small but productive Coosa River reservoir offering spotted bass and largemouth bass fishing in a scenic river setting.

7. Tallapoosa River (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Shoal Bass)

Tallapoosa River, central Alabama

The Tallapoosa River offers excellent spotted bass and shoal bass fishing in its free-flowing sections. The river's rocky shoals and deep pools provide diverse fishing opportunities.

8. Lake Tuscaloosa (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Crappie)

Tuscaloosa area

Lake Tuscaloosa is a 5,885-acre reservoir providing drinking water for Tuscaloosa. The lake offers excellent largemouth bass and crappie fishing with good shoreline access.

9. Holt Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Holt area, Black Warrior River

Holt Lake is a 3,200-acre reservoir on the Black Warrior River offering spotted bass and largemouth bass fishing near Tuscaloosa.

10. Lake Harris (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Lake Harris area, near Wedowee

Lake Harris is a 1,100-acre lake on the Little Tallapoosa River offering good bass and crappie fishing in a rural setting.

11. Wedowee Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Wedowee area, Randolph County

Wedowee Lake is a productive lake offering largemouth bass, crappie, and catfish fishing in east-central Alabama.

12. Yates Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Yates area, Tallapoosa River

Yates Lake is a 2,000-acre reservoir on the Tallapoosa River offering spotted bass and largemouth bass fishing near Lake Martin.

13. Thurlow Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Thurlow area, Tallapoosa River

Thurlow Lake is a 900-acre reservoir on the Tallapoosa River offering good spotted bass fishing below Yates Dam.

14. Lake Shelby (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Pelham / Birmingham area

Lake Shelby is a 90-acre state-managed public fishing lake at Oak Mountain State Park offering excellent family fishing with easy access from Birmingham.

15. Double Oak Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Double Oak area, near Birmingham

Double Oak Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake offering good bass and bream fishing for families and novice anglers.

16. Lake Claire (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Lake Claire, Lee County

Lake Claire offers good fishing near Auburn.

17. Chewacla Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream)

Chewacla Lake, Lee County

Chewacla Lake offers good fishing in Chewacla State Park.

18. Lake Ogletree (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Lake Ogletree, Lee County

Lake Ogletree offers good fishing near Auburn.

19. Yates Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Yates Lake, Tallapoosa County

Yates Lake offers good spotted bass fishing.

20. Lake Jordan (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Lake Jordan, Elmore County

Lake Jordan offers good fishing on the Coosa River.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at outdooralabama.com. Non-resident options available.
  • Fish the Coosa for spots -- The Coosa River chain offers the best spotted bass fishing in Alabama. Focus on rocky banks and points.
  • Try Lake Martin for striped bass -- Lake Martin has a strong striped bass population. Troll deep channels in summer for best results.
  • Spring and fall are prime -- April-May and September-October offer the best bass fishing in central Alabama.
  • Check state fishing lakes -- Alabama's 23 state-managed public fishing lakes are well-stocked and great for family outings.
