Fishing in the Kawarthas & Haliburton

Muskie, bass, trout, and panfish in Ontario cottage country. Kawartha Lakes, Haliburton Highlands, and Trent-Severn Waterway.

1. Kawartha Lakes -- Sturgeon Lake (Muskie, Walleye)

Lindsay area

Sturgeon Lake is one of the premier muskie lakes in the Kawarthas. The lake also holds walleye, bass, and panfish.

2. Kawartha Lakes -- Pigeon Lake (Muskie, Walleye)

Bobcaygeon area

Pigeon Lake offers excellent muskie, walleye, and bass fishing. The lake is part of the Trent-Severn Waterway.

3. Kawartha Lakes -- Chemong Lake (Walleye)

Bridgenorth area

Chemong Lake offers good walleye, bass, and panfish fishing. The lake is shallower than other Kawartha Lakes.

4. Kawartha Lakes -- Rice Lake (Muskie, Walleye)

Peterborough area

Rice Lake offers excellent walleye, bass, and muskie fishing. It is one of the most popular fishing lakes in the Kawarthas.

5. Haliburton -- Haliburton Lake (Brook Trout, Lake Trout)

Haliburton

Haliburton Lake offers lake trout, splake, and brook trout fishing in a beautiful highland setting.

6. Haliburton -- Kushog Lake (Brook Trout, Lake Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Near Minden

Kushog Lake offers smallmouth bass, lake trout, and brook trout fishing in a scenic Haliburton setting.

7. Trent-Severn Waterway (Muskie, Walleye)

Trenton to Port Severn

The Trent-Severn Waterway offers diverse fishing along its entire length, from walleye and bass to muskie and panfish.

8. Muskoka Lakes (Lake Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Bracebridge area

Muskoka lakes -- Lake Muskoka, Lake Rosseau, and Lake Joseph -- offer smallmouth bass, lake trout, and pike.

9. Lake Simcoe (Lake Trout, Northern Pike, Walleye, Whitefish, Yellow Perch)

Barrie area -- 1 hour north of Toronto

Lake Simcoe is one of Ontario's most popular and productive fishing lakes. The lake offers exceptional fishing for lake trout, whitefish, yellow perch, walleye, and northern pike. Lake Simcoe is particularly famous for its ice fishing -- the lake hosts thousands of ice huts each winter, making it one of the premier ice fishing destinations in North America.

In summer, lake trout fishing is excellent in the deeper basins (60-100 feet), particularly around Georgina Island and Snake Island. Yellow perch fishing is outstanding throughout the lake, with fish averaging 8-12 inches. Walleye populations have rebounded strongly in recent years, with the lake now producing good numbers of 2-4 pound fish.

Key access points include Barrie, Keswick, Beaverton, Orillia, and Sibbald Point Provincial Park. The lake has numerous marinas, boat launches, and ice hut operators. Anglers should note that Lake Simcoe has specific regulations including a reduced lake trout limit and seasonal closures on certain species. The lake's size (725 sq km) means anglers should check weather conditions before heading out, as it can get rough in high winds.

Lake Couchiching offers excellent walleye, bass, and perch fishing. The lake connects to Lake Simcoe.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Fish muskie in fall -- The Kawarthas offer world-class muskie fishing in fall.
  • Try ice fishing -- Lake Simcoe is one of Ontario's top ice fishing destinations.
  • Book ahead -- Cottage country accommodation books well in advance.
  • Explore smaller lakes -- The Haliburton Highlands have countless smaller lakes with less pressure.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
