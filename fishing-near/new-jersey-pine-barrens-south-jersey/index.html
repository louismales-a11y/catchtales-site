Fishing in the Pine Barrens & South Jersey

Mullica River, Batsto River, Wading River, Wharton State Forest, and the chain pickerel, largemouth bass, crappie, and yellow perch fishing of the New Jersey Pine Barrens.

1. Mullica River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, Bluegill, White Perch, Sunfish)

Mullica River, Burlington/Atlantic counties

The Mullica River is the heart of the New Jersey Pine Barrens, flowing 50 miles through Wharton State Forest to Great Bay, offering excellent chain pickerel and bass fishing in a unique tea-colored water ecosystem.

2. Batsto River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, Bluegill, Sunfish, White Perch)

Batsto River, Burlington County

The Batsto River is a pristine Pine Barrens river flowing through Wharton State Forest past the historic Batsto Village, offering excellent canoe-access fishing for pickerel and bass.

3. Wading River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Wading River, Burlington County

The Wading River is a scenic Pine Barrens river in Wharton State Forest offering excellent chain pickerel and bass fishing with canoe and kayak access.

4. Oswego River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Oswego River, Burlington County

The Oswego River offers good Pine Barrens fishing in the Bass River State Forest area with canoe access.

5. Bass River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Bass River, Burlington County

Bass River in Bass River State Forest offers good paddling and fishing in the Pine Barrens forest.

6. Atsion Lake (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Yellow Perch, Sunfish)

Atsion Lake, Burlington County

Atsion Lake is a 125-acre man-made lake on the Mullica River in Wharton State Forest offering excellent warmwater fishing with a swimming beach and boat rental.

7. Harrisville Lake (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Yellow Perch, Sunfish)

Harrisville Lake, Burlington County

Harrisville Lake on the Wading River in Wharton State Forest offers good fishing in a historic millpond setting.

8. Lake Absegami (Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Crappie, Bluegill, Yellow Perch, Sunfish)

Lake Absegami, Atlantic County

Lake Absegami is a 60-acre lake in Bass River State Forest offering good warmwater fishing with park amenities.

9. Nummy Lake (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Nummy Lake, Burlington County

Nummy Lake in Wharton State Forest offers good Pine Barrens fishing in a remote lake setting.

10. Hampton Lake (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Hampton Lake, Burlington County

Hampton Lake offers good warmwater fishing in the Bass River watershed of the Pine Barrens.

11. Great Egg Harbor River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Great Egg Harbor River, Camden/Atlantic counties

The Great Egg Harbor River flows 50 miles through the Pine Barrens to the Great Egg Harbor Bay offering excellent pickerel and bass fishing.

12. Tuckahoe River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, White Perch, Sunfish)

Tuckahoe River, Atlantic/Cape May counties

The Tuckahoe River offers excellent Pine Barrens fishing in the southern reaches of the region near Cape May County.

13. Maurice River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, Channel Catfish, White Perch, Sunfish)

Maurice River, Cumberland County

The Maurice River is a 34-mile tidal river in southern New Jersey offering fresh and saltwater species in a scenic Pine Barrens and coastal marsh setting.

14. Cohansey River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Yellow Perch, White Perch, Striped Bass, Sunfish)

Cohansey River, Cumberland County

The Cohansey River offers excellent fishing in the Pine Barrens and tidal marshes of Cumberland County near Bridgeton.

15. Alloway Creek (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, White Perch, Sunfish)

Alloway Creek, Salem County

Alloway Creek offers good fishing in the Salem County Pine Barrens and tidal marsh region.

16. Salem River (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, White Perch, Striped Bass, Channel Catfish)

Salem River, Salem County

The Salem River flows 25 miles through Salem County offering both fresh and tidal fishing for warmwater and saltwater species.

17. Parvin Lake (Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Crappie, Bluegill, Yellow Perch, Channel Catfish)

Parvin Lake in Parvin State Park is a 115-acre lake offering excellent warmwater fishing with a state park, campground, and hiking trails.

18. Thundergust Lake (Largemouth Bass, Chain Pickerel, Crappie, Bluegill, Sunfish)

19. Penny Pot Lake (Chain Pickerel, Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Sunfish)

Fishing Tips for the Pine Barrens & South Jersey

  • Check local regulations before fishing — each water body may have specific rules on limits, seasons, and bait.
  • Spring and fall typically offer the best fishing in New Jersey for most species.
  • Consider hiring a local guide for the best experience on unfamiliar waters.
  • Always carry a valid New Jersey fishing license — available online through the state wildlife agency.
  • Practice catch and release for sensitive species to help maintain healthy fish populations.
