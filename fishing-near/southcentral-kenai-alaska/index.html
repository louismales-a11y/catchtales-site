Fishing in Southcentral & Kenai Alaska

Kenai River world-famous salmon, Anchorage, Homer halibut, Seward, and the Mat-Su Valley.

1. Kenai River (Chinook Salmon, Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Dolly Varden)

Kenai Peninsula

The Kenai River is Alaska's most famous fishing river, world-renowned for king salmon exceeding 80 pounds, massive sockeye runs, and trophy rainbow trout. The river has three distinct sections: upper, middle, and lower, each offering unique fishing opportunities.

2. Cook Inlet (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut)

Cook Inlet, Anchorage to Homer

Cook Inlet offers excellent saltwater fishing for king salmon, silver salmon, and halibut. The inlet's waters are accessible from Anchorage, Kenai, and Homer.

3. Homer Spit (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rockfish)

Homer, "Halibut Capital of the World"

Homer is famous for world-class halibut fishing. The Homer Spit has numerous charter operators offering halibut trips in Cook Inlet and Kachemak Bay.

4. Ship Creek (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon)

Anchorage (downtown)

Ship Creek flows through downtown Anchorage and offers urban salmon fishing within walking distance of hotels. King and silver salmon run right through the city.

5. Russian River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Russian River, Kenai Peninsula

The Russian River is famous for its incredibly dense sockeye salmon runs in June and July. The river also holds rainbow trout and Dolly Varden.

6. Seward / Resurrection Bay (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish, Lingcod)

Seward, Kenai Fjords

Seward offers excellent saltwater fishing in Resurrection Bay and the Kenai Fjords. Charter boats target halibut, salmon, rockfish, and lingcod.

7. Willow Creek (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Willow, Mat-Su Valley

Willow Creek is a popular Mat-Su Valley salmon stream offering good king and silver salmon fishing, plus rainbow trout in its upper reaches.

8. Talkeetna River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Grayling)

Talkeetna, Mat-Su Valley

The Talkeetna River offers excellent salmon fishing plus rainbow trout and Arctic grayling in the shadow of Denali. Talkeetna is also a base for Denali climbers.

9. Kasilof River (Sockeye Salmon, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon)

Kasilof, Kenai Peninsula

The Kasilof River offers excellent sockeye and king salmon fishing on the Kenai Peninsula. It's less crowded than the Kenai River but still very productive.

10. Ninilchik River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon)

Ninilchik, Kenai Peninsula

The Ninilchik River offers good salmon fishing on the lower Kenai Peninsula, with access to Cook Inlet saltwater fishing nearby.

11. Wasilla Area Lakes (Rainbow Trout, Landlocked Salmon, Arctic Char)

Wasilla, Mat-Su Valley

The numerous lakes in the Wasilla area offer excellent stocked rainbow trout, landlocked salmon, and Arctic char fishing, easily accessible from Anchorage.

12. Kachemak Bay (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rockfish)

Kachemak Bay, Homer

Kachemak Bay offers world-class saltwater fishing with stunning mountain and glacier scenery. The bay is rich in marine life and productive fishing grounds.

13. Kenai Lake (Rainbow Trout, Sockeye Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Kenai Lake, Cooper Landing

Kenai Lake at the headwaters of the Kenai River offers excellent rainbow trout and sockeye salmon fishing in a stunning mountain lake setting.

14. Skilak Lake (Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Sockeye Salmon)

Skilak Lake, Kenai National Wildlife Refuge

Skilak Lake offers rainbow trout, lake trout, and sockeye salmon fishing within the Kenai National Wildlife Refuge.

15. Knik River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Knik River, Mat-Su Valley

The Knik River offers good salmon and trout fishing near Anchorage with stunning views of the Knik Glacier.

16. Kasilof River (Sockeye Salmon, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon)

Kasilof, Kenai Peninsula

The Kasilof River offers excellent sockeye and king salmon fishing with less crowding than the Kenai River.

17. Deep Creek (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon)

Deep Creek, Kenai Peninsula

Deep Creek offers good salmon fishing on the lower Kenai Peninsula and provides access to Cook Inlet saltwater fishing.

18. Anchor River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Steelhead, Dolly Varden)

Anchor River, Anchor Point

The Anchor River is the northernmost steelhead stream in North America, offering excellent salmon and steelhead fishing.

19. Moose River (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Moose River, Sterling area

The Moose River is a tributary of the Kenai River offering good sockeye salmon and rainbow trout fishing.

20. Kenai River Upper Reach (Rainbow Trout, Arctic Grayling, Dolly Varden)

Cooper Landing area

The upper Kenai River near Cooper Landing offers world-class rainbow trout and Arctic grayling fishing in breathtaking mountain scenery.

21. Ptarmigan Creek (Rainbow Trout, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling)

Ptarmigan Creek, Cooper Landing

Ptarmigan Creek offers excellent rainbow trout and Dolly Varden fishing near the Kenai River headwaters.

22. Quartz Creek (Rainbow Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Quartz Creek, Cooper Landing

Quartz Creek offers good rainbow trout and Arctic grayling fishing in the Kenai River watershed.

23. Trail Lakes (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Trail Lakes, Seward Highway

The Trail Lakes offer good sockeye salmon and rainbow trout fishing along the Seward Highway between Anchorage and Seward.

24. Portage Lake / River (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Portage, Seward Highway

Portage offers good salmon and Dolly Varden fishing in the shadow of Portage Glacier, easily accessible from Anchorage.

25. Twentymile River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon)

Twentymile River, Seward Highway

The Twentymile River offers good king and silver salmon fishing near the southern end of the Seward Highway.

26. Bird Creek (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Bird Creek, Seward Highway

Bird Creek is a popular roadside fishing spot along the Seward Highway, known for its silver salmon runs in August.

27. Eagle River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Eagle River, Anchorage area

Eagle River offers good salmon fishing in the Eagle River Nature Center area, a popular Anchorage-area fishing destination.

28. Eklutna River (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Eklutna River, near Anchorage

The Eklutna River offers good silver and pink salmon fishing near Anchorage with stunning views of the Chugach Mountains.

29. Campbell Creek (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Campbell Creek, Anchorage

Campbell Creek flows through south Anchorage offering urban salmon fishing with easy access from the city's trail system.

30. Chester Creek (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Chester Creek, Anchorage

Chester Creek offers urban salmon fishing through east Anchorage, with several parks providing access along its length.

31. Ship Creek Upper Reach (Rainbow Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Ship Creek, Anchorage

The upper Ship Creek above the dam offers excellent rainbow trout and Arctic grayling fishing within Anchorage.

32. Little Susitna River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Little Susitna River, Mat-Su

The Little Susitna River offers excellent king and silver salmon fishing plus rainbow trout in the Mat-Su Valley.

33. Montana Creek (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Montana Creek, Mat-Su Valley

Montana Creek offers good silver salmon and rainbow trout fishing in the Willow area of the Mat-Su Valley.

34. Caswell Creek (Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Caswell Creek, Mat-Su Valley

Caswell Creek offers good salmon and trout fishing along the Parks Highway north of Wasilla.

35. Alexander Creek (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Northern Pike)

Alexander Creek, Susitna River

Alexander Creek offers good king salmon and northern pike fishing in the Susitna River drainage.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at adfg.alaska.gov. King salmon stamp required.
  • Fish the Kenai in July -- July offers the best combination of king salmon, sockeye, and rainbow trout fishing on the Kenai River.
  • Book a Kenai guide -- The Kenai River has many experienced guides. Book early for prime summer dates.
  • Try Seward for halibut -- Seward offers excellent halibut fishing combined with Kenai Fjords wildlife cruises.
  • Watch for bears -- Southcentral Alaska has healthy bear populations. Carry bear spray and practice safe food storage.
