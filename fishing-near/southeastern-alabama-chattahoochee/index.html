Fishing in Southeastern Alabama & Chattahoochee

Lake Eufaula, Walter F. George Lake, Chattahoochee River, Dothan area, and the fishing waters of southeastern Alabama.

1. Lake Eufaula (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Eufaula area, Chattahoochee River

Lake Eufaula, also known as Walter F. George Lake, is a 45,000-acre reservoir on the Chattahoochee River. Known as the "Bass Capital of the World," it offers exceptional largemouth bass, striped bass, crappie, and catfish fishing.

2. Walter F. George Lake (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Crappie)

Walter F. George area, Chattahoochee River

Walter F. George Lake is a 45,000-acre impoundment on the Chattahoochee River forming the border between Alabama and Georgia. It is famous for trophy largemouth bass and striped bass fishing.

3. Chattahoochee River (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Catfish)

Chattahoochee River, southeast Alabama

The Chattahoochee River forms the southeastern border of Alabama and offers excellent fishing for largemouth bass, spotted bass, and catfish. The river's diverse habitat of deep holes and shoals provides good fishing throughout the year.

4. Dothan Area Lakes (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Dothan / Houston County area

The Dothan area has several productive lakes offering good largemouth bass, bream, and crappie fishing. These smaller lakes provide excellent family fishing opportunities.

5. Big Creek Lake (Southeast) (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Big Creek area, southeast Alabama

Big Creek Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake in southeastern Alabama offering good bass and bream fishing.

6. Choctawhatchee River (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Catfish)

Choctawhatchee River, southeast Alabama

The Choctawhatchee River flows through southeastern Alabama into Florida, offering excellent largemouth bass and catfish fishing in a scenic river setting.

7. Pea River (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Catfish)

Pea River, southeast Alabama

The Pea River is a tributary of the Choctawhatchee offering good bass and catfish fishing in southeastern Alabama.

8. Dale County Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Dale County, southeast Alabama

Dale County Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake offering good bass and bream fishing for families in southeast Alabama.

9. Geneva County Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Geneva County, southeast Alabama

Geneva County Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake offering productive family fishing in the Wiregrass region.

10. Barbour County Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Barbour County, southeast Alabama

Barbour County Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake offering good bass and bream fishing near Eufaula.

11. Houston County Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Houston County, Dothan area

Houston County Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake offering good family fishing near Dothan.

12. Henry County Lake (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Henry County, southeast Alabama

Henry County Lake is a state-managed public fishing lake offering productive bream and crappie fishing.

13. Jackson Lake (Southeast) (Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Jackson Lake area, southeast Alabama

Jackson Lake offers good largemouth bass and crappie fishing in southeastern Alabama near the Florida line.

14. Cowarts Creek (Largemouth Bass, Bream)

Cowarts area, near Dothan

Cowarts Creek offers good largemouth bass and bream fishing in the Dothan area of southeastern Alabama.

15. Lake Frank Jackson (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Lake Frank Jackson, near Opp

Lake Frank Jackson is a 700-acre lake offering good largemouth bass and crappie fishing in Covington County, southeast Alabama.

16. Lake Eufaula Below Dam (Striped Bass, Shad)

Lake Eufaula tailwaters, Barbour County

The tailwaters below Walter F. George Dam offer excellent striped bass fishing.

17. Cowikee Creek (Largemouth Bass, Bream)

Cowikee Creek, Barbour County

Cowikee Creek offers good fishing on Lake Eufaula.

18. Barbour Creek (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Barbour Creek, Barbour County

Barbour Creek offers good fishing.

19. Cheneyhatchee Creek (Largemouth Bass, Bream)

Cheneyhatchee Creek, Barbour County

Cheneyhatchee Creek offers good fishing.

20. Abbie Creek (Largemouth Bass, Bream)

Abbie Creek, Barbour County

Abbie Creek offers good fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at outdooralabama.com. Fishing on the Chattahoochee may require a Georgia licence on the Georgia side.
  • Fish Lake Eufaula for bass -- Lake Eufaula is one of the top bass lakes in the country. Spring and fall offer the best action.
  • Try striped bass below the dam -- The tailwaters below Walter F. George Dam offer excellent striped bass fishing.
  • Explore the state lakes -- Southeastern Alabama has numerous state-managed public fishing lakes perfect for family outings.
  • Watch for alligators -- Southeastern Alabama's lakes and rivers have alligator populations. Keep your distance and never feed them.
