Fishing in Western Newfoundland

Humber River Atlantic salmon, Grand Codroy River, Gros Morne brook trout, Corner Brook, and the Bay of Islands.

1. Humber River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Deer Lake / Corner Brook area

The Humber River is one of Newfoundland's premier Atlantic salmon rivers. Flowing into the Bay of Islands, it produces large salmon in the 8-15 pound range. The river also offers excellent brook trout fishing in its upper reaches and tributaries. Fly fishing with classic salmon flies is the standard.

2. Grand Codroy River (Atlantic Salmon, Cod)

Codroy Valley, southwest coast

The Grand Codroy River offers excellent Atlantic salmon fishing in a stunning coastal setting on Newfoundland's southwest coast. The river flows through a broad valley flanked by the Long Range Mountains. Salmon in the 6-12 pound range are common.

3. Little Codroy River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout, Cod)

Codroy Valley

The Little Codroy River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in the Codroy Valley. A smaller, more intimate stream than its neighbour, it provides excellent wading and close-quarters fly fishing.

4. Crabbes River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Crabbes area, southwest coast

The Crabbes River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on Newfoundland's southwest coast near Stephenville. A productive river with good runs of salmon and sea-run brook trout.

5. Robinsons River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Robinsons area

The Robinsons River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on the southwest coast near Stephenville. The river provides good wading access and consistent salmon runs.

6. Fischells River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout, Cod)

Fischells area

The Fischells River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on the west coast near the Codroy Valley. A smaller river with productive pools and good early season fishing.

7. Harrys River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Harrys area, west coast

The Harrys River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in western Newfoundland near the Port au Port Peninsula. A productive river with good public access.

8. Flat Bay River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Flat Bay area

The Flat Bay River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on the west coast near the Bay St. George area. A productive river with good runs of salmon.

9. Romaines River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Romaines area

The Romaines River offers brook trout and Atlantic salmon fishing on the Port au Port Peninsula. A smaller coastal stream with good sea-run brook trout fishing.

10. Port au Port River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Port au Port Peninsula

The Port au Port River offers brook trout and Atlantic salmon fishing on the Port au Port Peninsula. The river also provides excellent sea-run brook trout fishing in the tidal reaches.

11. Upper Humber River (Brook Trout)

Deer Lake area

The Upper Humber River offers excellent brook trout fishing in the headwaters above Deer Lake. This section holds healthy populations of wild brook trout and provides good wading.

12. Deer Lake (Brook Trout, Landlocked Salmon)

Deer Lake

Deer Lake offers brook trout and landlocked salmon fishing in a large lake setting near Corner Brook. The lake is accessible from the Trans-Canada Highway and provides good boat fishing.

13. Grand Lake (Newfoundland)

Grand Lake area, west coast

Grand Lake offers brook trout fishing in a large lake west of Corner Brook. The lake holds healthy populations of wild brook trout and provides good canoe and boat fishing.

14. Serpentine River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Serpentine area, Gros Morne region

The Serpentine River offers brook trout and Atlantic salmon fishing in the Gros Morne National Park region. The river flows through spectacular mountain scenery.

15. Lomond River (Brook Trout)

Lomond area, Gros Morne

The Lomond River offers brook trout fishing in Gros Morne National Park. The river flows through the park's dramatic landscape and holds healthy brook trout populations.

16. Bonne Bay (Brook Trout, Cod, Mackerel)

Bonne Bay, Gros Morne

Bonne Bay offers mackerel, cod, and sea-run brook trout fishing in a stunning fjord setting within Gros Morne National Park. Shore and boat fishing opportunities are available.

17. Trout River (Gros Morne)

Trout River, Gros Morne

The Trout River in Gros Morne National Park offers brook trout fishing in a scenic coastal river. The tidal estuary provides sea-run brook trout in spring and fall.

18. Wallace Brook (Brook Trout)

Wallace Brook, Gros Morne

Wallace Brook offers brook trout fishing in Gros Morne National Park. A small stream with good wild brook trout populations in a protected park setting.

19. Corner Brook Stream (Brook Trout)

Corner Brook

Corner Brook Stream offers urban brook trout fishing through the city of Corner Brook. The stream provides accessible fishing for residents and visitors with brook trout in the 8-12 inch range.

20. Pinchgut Lake (Brook Trout)

Pinchgut area, near Corner Brook

Pinchgut Lake offers brook trout fishing near Corner Brook. A popular local spot with good access and healthy brook trout populations.

21. Birchy Lake (Brook Trout)

Birchy Lake area, near Deer Lake

Birchy Lake offers brook trout fishing near Deer Lake on the Trans-Canada Highway. A scenic lake with good brook trout fishing accessible from the highway.

22. Sandy Lake (Newfoundland)

Sandy Lake area, west coast

Sandy Lake offers brook trout fishing in western Newfoundland near Cormack. A productive lake with healthy brook trout populations.

23. St. George's Bay (Cod, Flounder, Mackerel)

St. George's area

St. George's Bay offers mackerel, cod, and flounder fishing along the west coast near Stephenville. The bay provides good shore fishing from beaches and wharves.

24. Stephenville Crossing (Cod, Mackerel)

Stephenville area

Stephenville Crossing offers mackerel and cod fishing from the wharves and beaches near Stephenville on the west coast. A popular family fishing destination.

25. Bay St. George (Brook Trout, Cod, Mackerel)

Bay St. George area

Bay St. George offers mackerel, cod, and sea-run brook trout fishing along the west coast. The bay's sheltered waters provide good boating and shore fishing.

26. Port Saunders (Cod, Mackerel)

Port Saunders area, west coast

Port Saunders offers mackerel and cod fishing from the wharves and harbour on the Great Northern Peninsula's west coast. A productive saltwater fishing spot.

27. River of Ponds (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

River of Ponds area, west coast

The River of Ponds offers brook trout and Atlantic salmon fishing on the west coast of the Great Northern Peninsula. The river is known for its productive salmon pools.

28. Castors River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Castors River area, northern peninsula

The Castors River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on the west coast of the Great Northern Peninsula. A remote river with excellent salmon runs.

29. Torrent River (West Coast)

Torrent River area, west coast

The Torrent River offers Atlantic salmon fishing near St. Anthony on the Great Northern Peninsula. Known for large salmon in the 8-15 pound range, this small river is ideal for wading.

30. Pinware River (Atlantic Salmon, Brook Trout)

Pinware area, southern Labrador

The Pinware River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in southern Labrador near the Strait of Belle Isle. A productive river with good runs of salmon.

31. Forteau River (Arctic Char, Atlantic Salmon)

Forteau area, southern Labrador

The Forteau River offers Atlantic salmon and Arctic char fishing in southern Labrador. The river flows through the coastal region near the Labrador Straits.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at gov.nl.ca/fishing. A separate Atlantic salmon licence is required.
  • Fish the Humber in summer -- Late June through August offers the best Atlantic salmon fishing on the Humber River system.
  • Explore Gros Morne -- Gros Morne National Park offers excellent brook trout fishing in a spectacular UNESCO World Heritage setting.
  • Try sea-run brook trout -- Western Newfoundland's coastal rivers offer exceptional sea-run brook trout fishing in spring and fall.
  • Watch the weather -- Newfoundland weather is famously unpredictable. Always carry rain gear and extra layers.
