Fishing in Western Montana & Flathead Region

Flathead Lake, Clark Fork River, Kootenai River, Hungry Horse Reservoir, and the rainbow trout, bull trout, kokanee, and cutthroat fishing of western Montana.

1. Flathead Lake (Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Bull Trout, Whitefish, Northern Pike, Largemouth Bass)

Flathead Lake, Lake County

Flathead Lake is the largest natural freshwater lake in the western US outside Alaska at 197,500 acres, offering world-class lake trout and kokanee fishing with multiple public access points.

2. Clark Fork River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout, Mountain Whitefish)

Clark Fork River, Sanders/Mineral/Missoula counties

The Clark Fork River is one of Montana's largest rivers offering excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing with good public access.

3. Hungry Horse Reservoir (Kokanee, Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Bull Trout, Whitefish)

Hungry Horse Reservoir, Flathead County

Hungry Horse Reservoir is a 23,000-acre reservoir in the Flathead National Forest offering excellent kokanee and lake trout fishing.

4. Kootenai River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Bull Trout, White Sturgeon, Kokanee)

Kootenai River, Lincoln County

The Kootenai River in northwest Montana offers excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing below Libby Dam.

5. Lake Koocanusa (Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Kokanee, Bull Trout, Walleye)

Lake Koocanusa, Lincoln County

Lake Koocanusa is a 46,000-acre reservoir on the Kootenai River offering excellent kokanee and lake trout fishing.

6. Swan River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Bull Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Swan River, Lake County

The Swan River flows from the Swan Valley into Flathead Lake offering excellent trout fishing.

7. Swan Lake (Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Kokanee, Bull Trout, Northern Pike)

Swan Lake, Lake County

Swan Lake is a 2,000-acre natural lake offering excellent fishing with good public access.

8. Whitefish Lake (Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Northern Pike, Whitefish)

Whitefish Lake, Flathead County

Whitefish Lake is a popular 3,400-acre lake offering excellent lake trout and kokanee fishing near Whitefish.

9. Ashley Lake (Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Kokanee, Northern Pike)

Ashley Lake, Flathead County

Ashley Lake offers excellent fishing in a scenic mountain setting near Kalispell.

10. Bitterroot River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Mountain Whitefish)

Bitterroot River, Ravalli County

The Bitterroot River is a classic blue-ribbon trout stream offering excellent dry fly fishing for rainbow and brown trout.

11. Seeley Lake (Northern Pike, Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Largemouth Bass)

Seeley Lake, Missoula County

Seeley Lake offers excellent northern pike fishing in the Seeley-Swan valley.

12. Salmon Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Northern Pike, Largemouth Bass)

Salmon Lake, Missoula County

Salmon Lake offers good fishing near Seeley Lake.

13. Placid Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Northern Pike)

Placid Lake, Missoula County

Placid Lake offers good fishing near Seeley Lake.

14. Lake Alva (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Northern Pike)

Lake Alva, Missoula County

Lake Alva offers good fishing in the Seeley-Swan valley.

15. Holland Lake (Rainbow Trout, Bull Trout, Kokanee)

Holland Lake, Missoula County

Holland Lake in the Swan Valley offers excellent scenic fishing.

16. Lindbergh Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Bull Trout)

Lindbergh Lake, Missoula County

Lindbergh Lake offers good fishing in the Swan Valley.

17. Noxon Rapids Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Rainbow Trout)

Noxon Rapids Reservoir, Sanders County

Noxon Rapids Reservoir on the Clark Fork offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing.

18. Thompson River (MT) (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout)

Thompson River, Sanders County

The Thompson River offers excellent trout fishing in a scenic setting.

19. Fisher River (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout)

Fisher River, Lincoln County

The Fisher River offers good trout fishing near Libby.

20. Yaak River (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout)

Yaak River, Lincoln County

The Yaak River in northwest Montana offers excellent remote trout fishing.

21. Tobacco River (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Brown Trout)

Tobacco River, Lincoln County

The Tobacco River offers good trout fishing near Eureka.

22. Lake Mary Ronan (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Lake Mary Ronan, Lake County

Lake Mary Ronan offers good fishing near Flathead Lake.

23. Flathead River (North Fork) (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout)

North Fork Flathead River, Flathead County

The North Fork Flathead River along Glacier National Park offers excellent wild trout fishing in a pristine setting.

24. Flathead River (Middle Fork) (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout)

Middle Fork Flathead River, Flathead County

The Middle Fork Flathead River offers excellent trout fishing with spectacular scenery.

25. Flathead River (South Fork) (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Bull Trout)

South Fork Flathead River, Flathead County

The South Fork Flathead River below Hungry Horse Dam offers excellent trout fishing.

26. Stillwater River (Flathead) (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Brown Trout)

Stillwater River, Flathead County

The Stillwater River offers good trout fishing near Whitefish.

27. Echo Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Largemouth Bass, Northern Pike)

Echo Lake, Flathead County

Echo Lake offers good fishing near Kalispell.

28. Foys Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Largemouth Bass, Northern Pike)

Foys Lake, Flathead County

Foys Lake offers good fishing near Kalispell.

29. Bull Lake (Northern Pike, Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Yellow Perch)

Bull Lake, Lincoln County

Bull Lake offers good northern pike fishing near Troy.

30. McGregor Lake (Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Kokanee, Northern Pike)

McGregor Lake, Lincoln/Flathead counties

McGregor Lake offers good fishing northwest of Kalispell.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fwp.mt.gov. Conservation license required.
  • Fish Flathead Lake -- Flathead Lake offers world-class lake trout and kokanee fishing with excellent public access.
  • Try the Clark Fork -- The Clark Fork River offers excellent fly fishing for rainbow and brown trout.
  • Explore Glacier National Park -- Glacier NP contains over 700 lakes including Lake McDonald, St. Mary Lake, Bowman Lake, and Two Medicine Lake. A Montana fishing license is valid inside the park.
  • Bull trout must be released -- Bull trout are a threatened species in Montana. All bull trout caught must be immediately released unharmed.
  • Summer and early fall are best -- July through September offer the best fishing throughout the region. Glacier park roads open late May through October.
