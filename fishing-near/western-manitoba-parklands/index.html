Fishing in Western Manitoba: Parklands & Duck Mountain

Western Manitoba offers a mix of prairie rivers, boreal forest lakes, and the high-country trout fishing of Duck Mountain and Riding Mountain — a hidden gem for anglers willing to explore.

Western Manitoba is often overlooked by anglers who head east to the Whiteshell or north to Lake Winnipeg. But the Parklands region — Duck Mountain, Riding Mountain, and the Souris River — offers exceptional fishing with far fewer crowds. The region's clear lakes, cold trout streams, and prairie rivers provide diverse fishing opportunities for trout, walleye, pike, and smallmouth bass.

1. Duck Mountain Provincial Park (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout & Brown Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Duck Mountain Provincial Park is the premier trout fishing destination in Manitoba. The park's clear, cold lakes are stocked with rainbow trout, brook trout, and brown trout. East Blue Lake, West Blue Lake, and Wellman Lake are the most popular destinations, with rainbow trout averaging 1-3 pounds and larger fish available. The park's streams also hold brook trout. Duck Mountain has excellent campgrounds, hiking trails, and a resort at Wellman Lake. The park is at a higher elevation than surrounding areas, offering cooler temperatures and stunning fall colours. Fly fishing and light spinning tackle are the most productive approaches.

2. Riding Mountain National Park (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout & Northern Pike)

3 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Riding Mountain National Park offers excellent fishing in a stunning natural setting. Clear Lake, the park's most popular lake, holds rainbow trout, brook trout, and northern pike. The lake is deep, clear, and supports a healthy fish population. The park's backcountry lakes and streams offer more remote fishing opportunities. A national park permit is required in addition to a Manitoba fishing licence. The town of Wasagaming offers accommodations, restaurants, and outfitters. The park is also known for its bison herd and excellent hiking trails.

3. Souris River (Smallmouth Bass, Walleye & Northern Pike)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg

The Souris River flows through southwestern Manitoba and offers excellent fishing for smallmouth bass, walleye, and northern pike. The river's current, deep pools, and rocky structure provide diverse habitat. Smallmouth bass fishing is particularly good, with fish in the 1-3 pound range common. The river is accessible from several points near the town of Souris and through the Souris River Bend Wildlife Management Area. The river is also popular for canoeing and kayaking.

4. Lake Metigoshe & Turtle Mountain (Walleye, Northern Pike & Perch)

3.5 hours southwest of Winnipeg

Lake Metigoshe, in Turtle Mountain Provincial Park, offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake straddles the Canada-US border, with the southern portion in North Dakota. The Canadian side offers good access and several campgrounds. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold healthy fish populations. Turtle Mountain Provincial Park has excellent hiking and biking trails in addition to fishing.

5. Assiniboine River (Westman) (Walleye, Channel Catfish & Goldeye)

Through Brandon area, 2 hours west of Winnipeg

The Assiniboine River flows through Brandon and offers good fishing for walleye, channel catfish, and goldeye. The river through Brandon has several access points in parks and along the river trail. Walleye fishing is best in spring and fall. Channel catfish provide excellent sport in the summer months. The river is also popular for paddling.

6. Oak Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike & Perch)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg

Oak Lake, located near Virden, offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is one of the larger lakes in southwestern Manitoba and holds healthy fish populations. Oak Lake has a popular beach and campground, making it a great family destination. The lake's stained water provides good fishing during daylight hours.

7. Rock Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike & Perch)

3 hours west of Winnipeg

Rock Lake, near the community of Killarney, offers good fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is part of the Pembina River system and has good access and amenities. Rock Lake is a popular summer destination with a beach, campground, and nearby golf course.

8. Pembina River (Brook Trout & Brown Trout)

2 hours southwest of Winnipeg

The Pembina River, flowing through the Pembina Valley, offers trout fishing in a scenic valley setting. The river's cold, spring-fed waters hold brook trout and brown trout. The Pembina Valley is a beautiful region with deep river valleys and unique ecosystems. The river is accessible from several points along Highway 3. The Pembina Valley Provincial Park offers camping and hiking.

9. Whitewater Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye & Perch)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg

Whitewater Lake, near Boissevain, offers good fishing for northern pike, walleye, and yellow perch. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold healthy fish populations. Whitewater Lake is also an important bird area, making it a great destination for anglers who also enjoy birdwatching.

10. Shell River & Area (Brook Trout, Rainbow Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

The Shell River and its tributaries, near the community of Roblin, offer excellent trout fishing. The cold, spring-fed streams hold brook trout and rainbow trout. The area is less developed than Duck Mountain, offering a more remote experience. The Shell River is a good option for anglers looking to explore new water.

11. Pelican Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

2.5 hours southwest of Winnipeg, near Killarney

Pelican Lake is a large, shallow prairie lake near Killarney that offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake's stained water and abundant weed beds provide ideal habitat. Walleye in the 2-4 pound range are common. Pelican Lake has several campgrounds and cottage developments, making it a popular summer destination. The lake also has a strong perch population that provides excellent family fishing.

12. Killarney Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike)

2.5 hours southwest of Winnipeg

Killarney Lake, located within the town of Killarney, offers convenient fishing for walleye and northern pike. The lake is smaller than nearby Pelican Lake but holds healthy fish populations. The town of Killarney offers excellent amenities and the lake is easily accessible from several points. Killarney Lake is a good option for anglers looking for a quick fishing trip combined with small-town charm.

13. Lake Winnipegosis (Walleye, Northern Pike, Lake Trout, Perch)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Lake Winnipegosis is the third-largest lake in Manitoba, yet it receives far less fishing pressure than Lake Winnipeg or Lake Manitoba. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold excellent populations of walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. Lake trout are present in the deeper northern basins. The lake's remote character — much of the shoreline is undeveloped — means quality fishing for those who make the trip. Access points include the communities of Winnipegosis, Camperville, and Dawson Bay. The lake's size demands respect for weather conditions.

14. Swan Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg, near Swan River

Swan Lake, located near the town of Swan River, offers good fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is part of a chain of lakes in the Swan River Valley, an agricultural region nestled between Duck Mountain and the Porcupine Hills. Swan Lake has a popular beach and campground. The nearby Swan River also offers fishing for walleye and goldeye.

15. Childs Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg, Duck Mountain area

Childs Lake, located in the western portion of Duck Mountain Provincial Park, offers excellent trout fishing in a quieter setting than the park's more popular lakes. The lake holds rainbow trout and brook trout, with fish averaging 1-3 pounds. Childs Lake has a campground and boat launch. The lake is a good option for anglers looking to escape the crowds at East Blue and Wellman lakes.

16. Singush Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg, Duck Mountain area

Singush Lake is another Duck Mountain trout lake that offers quality fishing in a beautiful setting. The lake is smaller and less developed than the park's main lakes, offering a more remote experience. The trout population is healthy, with rainbows and brookies averaging 1-2 pounds. Singush Lake is best fished from a small boat or canoe.

17. Lake Dauphin (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

3 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Lake Dauphin, located near the town of Dauphin, offers good fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is shallower and more productive than the nearby Riding Mountain lakes. Lake Dauphin has several access points and is a popular destination for local anglers. The town of Dauphin offers full amenities and is the gateway to Riding Mountain National Park and Duck Mountain.

18. Gilbert Plains & Valley River Areas (Brook Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

The streams and rivers flowing off the Duck Mountain escarpment, including the Valley River and its tributaries near Gilbert Plains, offer brook trout fishing in scenic valley settings. These small, cold streams are best fished with light tackle or fly gear. The area combines agricultural valley views with forested slopes, offering a unique fishing experience.

19. Nesbitt Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg, near Virden

Nesbitt Lake, located near Virden, offers fishing for walleye and northern pike in a quiet prairie lake setting. The lake is smaller than nearby Oak Lake but offers less fishing pressure. Nesbitt Lake is a good option for anglers looking for a quieter alternative to the more popular lakes in the region.

20. Maple Lake (Northern Pike, Perch)

2 hours west of Winnipeg, near Austin

Maple Lake, located in the Spruce Woods area, offers fishing for northern pike and yellow perch. The lake is part of the Spruce Woods Provincial Park region, which offers unique sand dune landscapes and hiking trails in addition to fishing. Maple Lake is smaller and less pressured than the region's larger lakes.

21. Glenora Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg, near Cartwright

Glenora Lake, located south of Cartwright, offers walleye and northern pike fishing in a quiet prairie setting. The lake is smaller and less known than nearby Rock Lake and Pelican Lake, offering a more peaceful fishing experience. Glenora Lake is a good option for anglers exploring the Pembina Valley region.

22. Lauder Sandhills Lakes (Northern Pike)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg, near Lauder

The Lauder Sandhills area contains several small lakes and ponds that offer northern pike fishing in a unique landscape. The sandhills are a Manitoba landmark and offer hiking and wildlife viewing in addition to fishing. These small lakes are best explored with a canoe or kayak.

23. Butternut Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike)

3 hours west of Winnipeg, near Oak Lake

Butternut Lake, located near Oak Lake, offers walleye and northern pike fishing. The lake is smaller than Oak Lake but offers less fishing pressure. Butternut Lake is a good option for anglers looking for a quieter alternative to Oak Lake, especially during summer weekends.

24. Lightning Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg, near Virden

Lightning Lake, located in the Virden area, offers walleye and northern pike fishing. The lake is smaller than Oak Lake and Nesbitt Lake but holds healthy fish populations. Lightning Lake is a good option for anglers based in the Virden area looking for a quick after-work fishing trip.

25. Alexander & Beresford Lakes (Walleye, Northern Pike)

3 hours northwest of Winnipeg, near Roblin

Alexander Lake and Beresford Lake, located near Roblin, offer walleye and northern pike fishing in the Parklands region. These lakes are less known than the Duck Mountain lakes but provide good fishing with fewer crowds. The area combines farmland with forested lake country.

26. Goodlands & Lyleton Area Lakes (Northern Pike)

3.5 hours southwest of Winnipeg

The area around Goodlands and Lyleton in southwestern Manitoba contains several small prairie pothole lakes that hold northern pike. These small lakes are best fished from small boats or canoes. The area is primarily agricultural but offers good fishing for anglers exploring the southwest corner of the province.

27. Minnedosa Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg

Minnedosa Lake, located near the town of Minnedosa, offers fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is a man-made reservoir on the Little Saskatchewan River. Minnedosa Lake has a popular beach, campground, and the Minnedosa Lake Golf Course nearby. The town of Minnedosa hosts the annual Minnedosa Trout Festival, celebrating the region's fishing heritage.

28. Rivers & Wheatland Area Lakes (Northern Pike)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg

The area around the town of Rivers and Wheatland contains several small prairie lakes that hold northern pike. These lakes are shallow, productive, and provide good fishing for anglers in the region. The area is primarily agricultural but the lakes offer welcome fishing opportunities.

29. Souris River Bend Lakes (Northern Pike, Perch)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg

The Souris River Bend Wildlife Management Area contains several oxbow lakes and backwaters that offer northern pike and yellow perch fishing. These small water bodies are excellent for canoe and kayak fishing. The area is also a prime birdwatching destination, with numerous waterfowl and shorebird species.

30. Criddle & Vane Lakes (Northern Pike)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg

Criddle Lake and Vane Lake, located in the Virden area, offer northern pike fishing in small prairie lake settings. These lakes are less pressured than the larger lakes in the region and can provide good fishing for anglers willing to explore.

31. Clear Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Northern Pike)

3 hours northwest of Winnipeg, Riding Mountain National Park

Clear Lake, located in Riding Mountain National Park, offers fishing for rainbow trout, brook trout, and northern pike. The lake is deep, clear, and supports a healthy fish population. A national park permit is required in addition to a Manitoba fishing licence.

32. Sandy Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg

Sandy Lake, located near the community of Sandy Lake, offers walleye and northern pike fishing. The lake's shallow waters and weed beds provide good habitat for both species.

33. Waterhen Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Waterhen Lake, located on the western side of the Lake Winnipegosis area, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake is shallower and more productive than Lake Winnipegosis.

34. Foxwarren Lake (Northern Pike, Perch)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg, near Foxwarren

Foxwarren Lake, located near the community of Foxwarren, offers northern pike and yellow perch fishing in a quiet prairie lake setting. The lake is smaller and less pressured than the region's more popular waters.

35. Deloraine Reservoir (Northern Pike, Walleye, Perch)

3.5 hours southwest of Winnipeg, near Deloraine

Deloraine Reservoir, located near the town of Deloraine in southwestern Manitoba, offers fishing for northern pike, walleye, and yellow perch. The reservoir is a man-made impoundment that holds healthy fish populations. The Deloraine area is in the Turtle Mountain region and offers a scenic prairie setting.

36. Swan River (Walleye, Northern Pike, Goldeye)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg, near Swan River

The Swan River flows through the Swan River Valley into the Lake Winnipegosis basin. The river offers walleye, northern pike, and goldeye fishing. The Swan River Valley is a scenic agricultural region nestled between Duck Mountain and the Porcupine Hills.

37. Turtle River (Walleye, Northern Pike)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg, near Bowsman

The Turtle River flows through the Swan River Valley and offers walleye and northern pike fishing. The river is smaller than the Swan River and offers a more intimate fishing experience.

38. Vermilion River (Walleye, Northern Pike)

3 hours northwest of Winnipeg, near Dauphin

The Vermilion River flows through the Dauphin area into Lake Winnipegosis. The river offers walleye and northern pike fishing in a scenic prairie valley setting.

39. Qu'Appelle River (Walleye, Northern Pike)

3 hours west of Winnipeg, southeastern Saskatchewan border

The Qu'Appelle River flows from Saskatchewan into Manitoba near St. Lazare. The Manitoba section of the river offers walleye and northern pike fishing. The Qu'Appelle Valley is a scenic region with unique geology.

40. Lake of the Prairies / Shellmouth Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

3 hours west of Winnipeg, near St. Lazare

Lake of the Prairies, also known as Shellmouth Reservoir, is a large man-made reservoir on the Assiniboine River near the Manitoba-Saskatchewan border. The reservoir spans the border, with the majority lying in Manitoba. The lake offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. Walleye in the 2-4 pound range are common. The reservoir's flooded timber and structure provide excellent fish habitat. Lake of the Prairies has several access points, campgrounds, and cottage developments. The reservoir is a popular destination for anglers from both Manitoba and Saskatchewan.

Fishing Tips for Western Manitoba

  • Focus on trout in Duck Mountain — Duck Mountain is Manitoba's premier trout destination. The park's lakes are stocked regularly and produce excellent fish. Spring and fall are the best seasons.
  • Try the Souris River for bass — The Souris River offers the best smallmouth bass fishing in western Manitoba. Focus on rocky sections and current breaks.
  • Book park accommodations early — Riding Mountain and Duck Mountain are popular summer destinations. Campgrounds and resort rooms book well in advance.
  • Explore Turtle Mountain — Lake Metigoshe and Turtle Mountain Provincial Park offer excellent fishing in a unique cross-border setting.
  • Watch for ticks — The Parklands region has significant tick populations in spring and early summer. Check yourself after fishing, especially in grassy or brushy areas.
