Fishing in Northern Arkansas & Ozarks

Bull Shoals Lake, Norfork Lake, White River trout, Beaver Lake, Buffalo River, and the Ozark Mountain fishing paradise.

1. White River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

White River, Bull Shoals to Batesville

The White River is one of the premier trout fisheries in the United States. The cold tailwaters below Bull Shoals Dam produce world-class rainbow and brown trout fishing with fish exceeding 10 pounds.

2. Bull Shoals Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye, Crappie, Catfish)

Bull Shoals Lake, Marion County

Bull Shoals Lake is a 45,000-acre reservoir on the White River offering excellent largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, walleye, and crappie fishing in clear Ozark waters.

3. Norfork Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Striped Bass, Walleye, Crappie)

Norfork Lake, Baxter County

Norfork Lake is a 22,000-acre reservoir offering excellent striped bass, walleye, and smallmouth bass fishing in clear, deep Ozark waters.

4. Norfork River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Norfork River, below Norfork Dam

The Norfork River offers excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing in its cold tailwaters below Norfork Dam.

5. Beaver Lake (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Walleye, Crappie, Catfish)

Beaver Lake, Benton County

Beaver Lake is a 28,000-acre reservoir in northwest Arkansas offering excellent striped bass, walleye, and largemouth bass fishing.

6. Table Rock Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Table Rock Lake, Carroll County

Table Rock Lake extends into Arkansas from Missouri and offers excellent bass and crappie fishing in the Ozark Mountains.

7. Buffalo National River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Catfish)

Buffalo National River, Newton County

The Buffalo River is a National Wild and Scenic River offering excellent smallmouth bass fishing in a stunning Ozark river canyon.

8. Spring River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Spring River, Fulton County

The Spring River offers excellent rainbow trout fishing in its spring-fed cold waters plus smallmouth bass fishing.

9. Eleven Point River (Rainbow Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Eleven Point River, Oregon County

The Eleven Point River is a National Scenic River offering good trout and smallmouth bass fishing.

10. Kings River (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Kings River, Madison County

The Kings River offers excellent smallmouth bass fishing in a scenic Ozark river canyon.

11. War Eagle Creek (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass)

War Eagle Creek, Benton County

War Eagle Creek offers good smallmouth bass fishing in northwest Arkansas.

12. Crooked Creek (Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Crooked Creek, Marion County

Crooked Creek offers excellent smallmouth bass fishing in the Ozarks.

13. Dry Run Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Dry Run Creek, below Norfork Dam

Dry Run Creek is a catch-and-release, youth-only trout stream offering trophy rainbow and brown trout fishing.

14. Lake Sequoyah (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Lake Sequoyah, Fayetteville area

Lake Sequoyah offers good largemouth bass and crappie fishing near Fayetteville.

15. Prairie Creek Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Prairie Creek Lake, Rogers area

Prairie Creek Lake offers good bass and crappie fishing in northwest Arkansas.

16. Lincoln Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Lincoln Lake, Washington County

Lincoln Lake offers good bass and crappie fishing near Fayetteville.

17. Elmdale Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass)

Elmdale Lake, near Fayetteville

Elmdale Lake offers rainbow trout stocking in winter and bass fishing year-round.

18. Lake Fayetteville (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Lake Fayetteville, Fayetteville

Lake Fayetteville offers urban bass and crappie fishing within the city of Fayetteville.

19. Lake Atalanta (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Lake Atalanta, Rogers

Lake Atalanta offers good bass fishing in Rogers, Arkansas.

20. Upper White River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Upper White River, below Beaver Dam

The upper White River below Beaver Dam offers excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing in northwest Arkansas.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at agfc.com. Non-resident options available. A trout permit is required for trout fishing.
  • Fish the White River -- The White River below Bull Shoals Dam is one of the best trout fisheries in the US. Guided drift boat trips are popular.
  • Try Bull Shoals for bass -- Bull Shoals Lake offers excellent bass fishing in clear Ozark waters. Spring and fall are best.
  • Check generation schedules -- Trout fishing on tailwaters depends on dam generation. Low water is best for wading.
  • Spring and fall are prime -- April-May and September-October offer the best fishing in the Ozarks with comfortable temperatures.
