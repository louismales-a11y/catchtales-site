Fishing in Southwestern Montana

Madison River, Gallatin River, Big Hole River, Hebgen Lake, and the rainbow trout, brown trout, cutthroat, and brook trout fishing of southwestern Montana.

1. Madison River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Mountain Whitefish, Arctic Grayling)

Madison River, Madison/Gallatin counties

The Madison River is one of the most famous trout streams in the world, flowing from Yellowstone National Park through the spectacular Madison Valley with excellent wade and float fishing.

2. Hebgen Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Lake Trout, Arctic Grayling, Kokanee)

Hebgen Lake, Gallatin County

Hebgen Lake is a 15,000-acre reservoir just outside Yellowstone offering excellent rainbow trout and brown trout fishing with good public access.

3. Gallatin River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Brook Trout)

Gallatin River, Gallatin County

The Gallatin River flows from Yellowstone Park through the Gallatin Valley offering excellent dry fly fishing for rainbow and brown trout.

4. Big Hole River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Arctic Grayling, Mountain Whitefish)

Big Hole River, Beaverhead/Silver Bow counties

The Big Hole River is a legendary blue-ribbon trout stream offering exceptional dry fly fishing and one of the last remaining Arctic grayling populations in the lower 48.

5. Jefferson River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Mountain Whitefish, Walleye, Sauger)

Jefferson River, Madison/Jefferson counties

The Jefferson River forms the Missouri River headwaters offering excellent trout fishing with good wade access.

6. Ruby River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout, Mountain Whitefish)

Ruby River, Madison/Beaverhead counties

The Ruby River offers excellent brown trout fishing in a scenic valley setting.

7. Beaverhead River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Mountain Whitefish)

Beaverhead River, Beaverhead County

The Beaverhead River below Clark Canyon Dam offers exceptional brown trout and rainbow trout fishing.

8. Clark Canyon Reservoir (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Walleye, Yellow Perch, Northern Pike)

Clark Canyon Reservoir, Beaverhead County

Clark Canyon Reservoir offers good fishing for rainbow trout, walleye, and northern pike near Dillon.

9. Wise River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Wise River, Beaverhead County

The Wise River is a popular tributary of the Big Hole offering excellent trout fishing.

10. Grasshopper Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Grasshopper Creek, Beaverhead County

Grasshopper Creek offers good trout fishing near Bannack State Park.

11. Red Rock River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Red Rock River, Beaverhead County

The Red Rock River offers good trout fishing in southwest Montana.

12. Lima Reservoir (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Lake Trout, Kokanee)

Lima Reservoir, Beaverhead County

Lima Reservoir offers good fishing near the Montana-Idaho border.

13. Earthquake Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Lake Trout)

Earthquake Lake, Gallatin County

Earthquake Lake on the Madison River offers excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing.

14. Ennis Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch)

Ennis Lake, Madison County

Ennis Lake on the Madison River offers good pike and perch fishing in addition to trout.

15. Wade Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Wade Lake, Madison County

Wade Lake offers excellent trophy trout fishing in a spring-fed setting.

16. Cliff Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Cliff Lake, Madison County

Cliff Lake offers excellent fishing near Wade Lake in the Madison Range.

17. Hidden Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Hidden Lake, Madison County

Hidden Lake offers good fishing in the Madison Range.

18. South Willow Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

South Willow Creek, Beaverhead County

South Willow Creek offers good trout fishing.

19. Big Hole River (Upper) (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Arctic Grayling)

Upper Big Hole River, Beaverhead County

The upper reaches of the Big Hole offer excellent brook trout and Arctic grayling fishing.

20. Poindexter Slough (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Northern Pike)

Poindexter Slough, Madison County

Poindexter Slough near Ennis offers good fishing for brown trout and pike.

21. Varney Bridge (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Varney Bridge, Madison County

Varney Bridge area on the Madison River offers excellent wade fishing access.

22. Raynolds Pass (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Raynolds Pass, Madison County

Raynolds Pass area on the Madison offers excellent fishing near the Yellowstone border.

23. Lyon Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Lyon Creek, Madison County

Lyon Creek offers good trout fishing near Ennis.

24. Divide Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Divide Creek, Beaverhead County

Divide Creek offers good trout fishing.

25. Warm Springs Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Warm Springs Creek, Beaverhead County

Warm Springs Creek offers good trout fishing.

26. Big Hole Battlefield (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Big Hole Battlefield area, Beaverhead County

The Big Hole River near the battlefield offers excellent trout fishing.

27. Madison River - Bear Trap Canyon (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Bear Trap Canyon, Madison County

Bear Trap Canyon on the Madison River offers spectacular scenery and excellent trout fishing.

28. West Fork Madison River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

West Fork Madison, Madison County

The West Fork of the Madison offers good trout fishing.

29. South Fork Madison River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

South Fork Madison, Madison County

The South Fork of the Madison offers good trout fishing.

30. Silver Bow Creek (Brown Trout, Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Silver Bow Creek, Silver Bow County

Silver Bow Creek offers good fishing and is a restored urban fishery near Butte.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fwp.mt.gov. Conservation license required.
  • Fish the Madison River -- The Madison is one of the world's most famous trout streams with excellent hatches throughout the summer.
  • Try the Big Hole -- The Big Hole River offers exceptional dry fly fishing and is home to the rare Arctic grayling.
  • Explore Hebgen Lake -- Hebgen Lake offers excellent fishing just outside Yellowstone National Park.
  • Summer is peak season -- June through September offer the best fishing with major hatches of salmonflies and caddis.
