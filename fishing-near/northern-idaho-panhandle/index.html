Fishing in Northern Idaho & Panhandle

Lake Pend Oreille, Coeur d'Alene Lake, Priest Lake, Hayden Lake, and the big-water fishing of the Idaho Panhandle.

1. Lake Pend Oreille (Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Kokanee Salmon, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye)

Lake Pend Oreille, Bonner County

Lake Pend Oreille is Idaho's largest lake at 43,000 acres and one of the deepest in the US. It offers world-class lake trout (mackinaw) fishing, with fish exceeding 40 pounds.

2. Coeur d'Alene Lake (Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Kokanee, Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Coeur d'Alene Lake, Kootenai County

Coeur d'Alene Lake is a 25,000-acre lake offering excellent trout, kokanee, and bass fishing in a stunning mountain setting.

3. Priest Lake (Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Priest Lake, Bonner County

Priest Lake offers excellent lake trout and kokanee fishing in a pristine wilderness setting in the Selkirk Mountains.

4. Hayden Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Smallmouth Bass, Largemouth Bass, Crappie)

Hayden Lake, Kootenai County

Hayden Lake offers good trout, kokanee, and bass fishing near Coeur d'Alene.

5. Pend Oreille River (Rainbow Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye, Northern Pike)

Pend Oreille River, Bonner County

The Pend Oreille River offers excellent fishing for walleye, smallmouth bass, and northern pike flowing from the lake.

6. Spokane River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Spokane River, Kootenai County

The Spokane River offers good trout and smallmouth bass fishing below Coeur d'Alene Lake.

7. Kootenai River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, White Sturgeon, Northern Pike)

Kootenai River, Boundary County

The Kootenai River offers excellent fishing in the far northern Panhandle, including white sturgeon.

8. Moyie River (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Brook Trout)

Moyie River, Boundary County

The Moyie River offers good trout fishing in the northern Panhandle.

9. Pack River (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout)

Pack River, Bonner County

The Pack River offers good trout fishing flowing into Lake Pend Oreille.

10. Clark Fork River (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Brown Trout)

Clark Fork River, Bonner County

The Clark Fork River offers good trout fishing flowing into Lake Pend Oreille.

11. Upper Priest Lake (Lake Trout, Brook Trout, Rainbow Trout)

Upper Priest Lake, Bonner County

Upper Priest Lake offers remote lake trout and brook trout fishing accessible only by trail or a short boat ride.

12. Twin Lakes (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Smallmouth Bass)

Twin Lakes, Kootenai County

Twin Lakes offer good trout and kokanee fishing near Rathdrum.

13. Hauser Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill, Catfish)

Hauser Lake, Kootenai County

Hauser Lake offers good warm-water fishing near the Washington border.

14. Kelso Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Kelso Lake, Bonner County

Kelso Lake offers good trout fishing.

15. Spirit Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee, Smallmouth Bass)

Spirit Lake, Kootenai County

Spirit Lake offers good trout and kokanee fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at idfg.idaho.gov. Non-resident options available.
  • Fish Pend Oreille for lake trout -- Lake Pend Oreille is one of the best lake trout fisheries in the lower 48. Focus on deep water in summer.
  • Try Coeur d'Alene for kokanee -- Coeur d'Alene Lake offers excellent kokanee salmon fishing. Trolling with dodgers works well.
  • Spring and fall are best -- May-June and September-October offer the best fishing in northern Idaho.
  • Watch for lake levels -- Pend Oreille and Coeur d'Alene lake levels fluctuate with dam operations. Check conditions.
