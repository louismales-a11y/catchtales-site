Fishing in Southeast Alaska

Juneau, Ketchikan, Sitka, Inside Passage, and the world-class salmon and halibut fishing of the Alaskan Panhandle.

1. Juneau Area (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut, Dolly Varden)

Juneau, state capital

Juneau offers exceptional saltwater fishing in the Inside Passage. Charter boats target king salmon, silver salmon, and halibut. The nearby rivers also offer Dolly Varden and cutthroat trout fishing.

2. Ketchikan Area (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut, Rainbow Trout)

Ketchikan, southern panhandle

Ketchikan is known as the "Salmon Capital of the World." The area offers world-class salmon fishing in the Tongass Narrows and surrounding waters, plus excellent halibut fishing.

3. Sitka Area (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut, Rockfish)

Sitka, Baranof Island

Sitka offers some of the best saltwater fishing in Alaska. The protected waters of Sitka Sound provide excellent king salmon, silver salmon, and halibut fishing with stunning scenery.

4. Skagway Area (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Skagway, northern panhandle

Skagway at the northern end of the Inside Passage offers good salmon fishing in the Taiya River and nearby saltwater. The historic Gold Rush town adds a unique backdrop.

5. Haines Area (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut)

Haines, Lynn Canal

Haines offers excellent salmon and halibut fishing in the Chilkat River and Lynn Canal. The area is also known for its eagle populations in winter.

6. Petersburg Area (Chinook Salmon, Halibut, Rockfish)

Petersburg, Mitkof Island

Petersburg is a fishing community known as "Little Norway." The surrounding waters offer excellent halibut and king salmon fishing.

7. Wrangell Area (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut, Steelhead)

Wrangell, Wrangell Island

Wrangell offers productive fishing for salmon, halibut, and steelhead in the Stikine River and surrounding Inside Passage waters.

8. Gustavus / Glacier Bay (Chinook Salmon, Halibut, Dolly Varden)

Gustavus, Glacier Bay National Park

Glacier Bay offers unique fishing in the shadow of tidewater glaciers. Halibut and salmon fishing is excellent, with spectacular scenery throughout the park.

9. Craig / Prince of Wales Island (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Steelhead)

Craig, Prince of Wales Island

Prince of Wales Island offers excellent salmon and steelhead fishing in its numerous streams and protected saltwater coves.

10. Yakutat (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut, Steelhead)

Yakutat, Gulf of Alaska coast

Yakutat sits at the northern edge of the panhandle and offers excellent fishing in the Situk River, one of Alaska's premier steelhead streams, plus good saltwater fishing.

11. Inside Passage (Multiple Species)

Inside Passage, entire southeast region

The Inside Passage offers world-class saltwater fishing from Ketchikan to Skagway. The protected waters provide excellent opportunities for salmon, halibut, and rockfish.

12. Tongass National Forest Streams (Rainbow Trout, Cutthroat Trout, Dolly Varden)

Tongass National Forest, throughout southeast

The countless streams of Tongass National Forest offer excellent trout and Dolly Varden fishing in pristine wilderness settings.

13. Elfin Cove (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish)

Elfin Cove, Chichagof Island

Elfin Cove is a remote fishing village offering world-class halibut and salmon fishing in the Icy Strait area.

14. Tenakee Springs (Coho Salmon, Halibut, Dolly Varden)

Tenakee Springs, Chichagof Island

Tenakee Springs offers productive fishing in a remote community accessible only by boat or float plane.

15. Kake Area (Chinook Salmon, Halibut, Rockfish)

Kake, Kupreanof Island

Kake offers good salmon and halibut fishing in Frederick Sound, an area known for whale watching as well as fishing.

16. Hoonah / Icy Strait (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut)

Hoonah, Chichagof Island

Hoonah offers excellent saltwater fishing in Icy Strait, one of the most productive fishing areas in Southeast Alaska. The community has a strong Tlingit heritage.

17. Pelican (Chinook Salmon, Halibut, Rockfish)

Pelican, Lisianski Inlet

Pelican is a remote fishing community offering excellent halibut and salmon fishing in the Lisianski Inlet area of Chichagof Island.

18. Angoon (Coho Salmon, Halibut, Dolly Varden)

Angoon, Admiralty Island

Angoon on Admiralty Island offers good fishing in the Kootznahoo Inlet area, known for its Admiralty Island bear viewing.

19. Port Alexander (Chinook Salmon, Halibut, Rockfish)

Port Alexander, Baranof Island

Port Alexander at the southern tip of Baranof Island offers excellent saltwater fishing in the outside waters of the Pacific Ocean.

20. Klawock River (Coho Salmon, Chinook Salmon, Steelhead)

Klawock, Prince of Wales Island

The Klawock River offers excellent silver salmon and steelhead fishing on Prince of Wales Island.

21. Situk River (Steelhead, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon)

Situk River, Yakutat area

The Situk River is one of Alaska's premier steelhead streams, offering excellent fall and spring steelhead runs plus silver and pink salmon.

22. Stikine River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Steelhead, Dolly Varden)

Stikine River, Wrangell area

The Stikine River is one of the largest rivers in Southeast Alaska, offering excellent salmon and steelhead fishing in a remote wilderness setting.

23. Taku River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Steelhead, Dolly Varden)

Taku River, Juneau area

The Taku River flows from British Columbia into the Inside Passage near Juneau, offering excellent king salmon and steelhead fishing.

24. Chilkat River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Chilkat River, Haines area

The Chilkat River offers excellent salmon and Dolly Varden fishing near Haines. The river is also famous for its winter bald eagle congregation.

25. Chilkoot River (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Chilkoot River, Haines area

The Chilkoot River offers good sockeye and silver salmon fishing near Haines, with excellent bear viewing opportunities.

26. Auke Lake (Rainbow Trout, Dolly Varden, Coho Salmon)

Auke Lake, Juneau

Auke Lake near Juneau offers good trout and Dolly Varden fishing in a scenic lake setting with the Mendenhall Glacier in view.

27. Mendenhall River (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Mendenhall River, Juneau

The Mendenhall River offers good salmon and Dolly Varden fishing near the Mendenhall Glacier, easily accessible from Juneau.

28. Anan Creek (Pink Salmon, Coho Salmon)

Anan Creek, Wrangell area

Anan Creek is famous for its pink salmon runs and bear viewing platform, one of the best places to watch bears fishing for salmon.

29. Steep Creek (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon)

Steep Creek, Juneau area

Steep Creek offers a unique roadside sockeye salmon viewing and fishing opportunity near the Mendenhall Glacier visitor center.

30. Tolstoi Bay (Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Tolstoi Bay, Prince of Wales Island

Tolstoi Bay offers good salmon and trout fishing on the remote western side of Prince of Wales Island.

31. Naha River (Steelhead, Coho Salmon, Dolly Varden)

Naha River, Ketchikan area

The Naha River offers excellent steelhead and silver salmon fishing on Revillagigedo Island near Ketchikan.

32. Salmon River (Southeast) (Steelhead, Coho Salmon, Chinook Salmon)

Salmon River, Ketchikan area

The Salmon River near Ketchikan offers excellent steelhead and salmon fishing on the southern tip of Revillagigedo Island.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at adfg.alaska.gov. Non-resident licences are available. A king salmon stamp is required for Chinook.
  • Book a charter -- Southeast Alaska has hundreds of licensed charter operators. Book well in advance for peak summer months.
  • Watch for whales -- The Inside Passage is prime whale watching territory. Keep an eye out while fishing.
  • Bring rain gear -- Southeast Alaska is a temperate rainforest. Expect rain and pack quality waterproof gear.
  • May through September -- The fishing season runs from May through September, with peak salmon runs in June-August.
