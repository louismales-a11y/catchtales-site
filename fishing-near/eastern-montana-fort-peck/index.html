Fishing in Eastern Montana & Fort Peck Area

Fort Peck Reservoir, Missouri Breaks, Milk River, and the walleye, northern pike, sauger, smallmouth bass, and crappie fishing of eastern Montana.

1. Fort Peck Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Sauger, Smallmouth Bass, Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Chinook Salmon)

Fort Peck Reservoir, Valley/Garfield counties

Fort Peck Reservoir is one of the largest reservoirs in the US at 245,000 acres, offering world-class walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass fishing.

2. Missouri River - Fort Peck Tailwaters (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Walleye, Sauger, Channel Catfish)

Missouri River below Fort Peck Dam, Valley County

The Missouri River tailwaters below Fort Peck Dam offer excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing with consistent cold water releases.

3. Missouri Breaks (Walleye, Northern Pike, Sauger, Channel Catfish, Smallmouth Bass)

Missouri Breaks, Phillips/Blaine counties

The Missouri Breaks offer exceptional remote fishing for walleye, pike, and catfish in the rugged badlands canyon of the Upper Missouri National Wild and Scenic River.

4. Milk River (Walleye, Northern Pike, Sauger, Channel Catfish, Smallmouth Bass)

Milk River, Phillips/Valley/Blaine counties

The Milk River offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing in eastern Montana with good access.

5. Nelson Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch, Rainbow Trout)

Nelson Reservoir, Phillips County

Nelson Reservoir is a 3,500-acre irrigation reservoir offering excellent walleye and pike fishing.

6. Fresno Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch, Sauger)

Fresno Reservoir, Hill County

Fresno Reservoir near Havre offers excellent walleye and pike fishing.

7. Beaver Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Beaver Creek, Hill County

Beaver Creek offers good trout fishing in the Bear Paw Mountains.

8. Bear Paw Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Kokanee)

Bear Paw Lake, Hill County

Bear Paw Lake offers good fishing in the Bear Paw Mountains.

9. Slippery Ann Creek (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass)

Slippery Ann Creek, Valley County

Slippery Ann Creek near Fort Peck offers good warmwater fishing.

10. Redwater River (Walleye, Northern Pike, Sauger)

Redwater River, McCone/Dawson counties

The Redwater River offers good walleye and pike fishing.

11. Big Dry Creek (Walleye, Northern Pike, Channel Catfish)

Big Dry Creek, McCone/Garfield counties

Big Dry Creek offers good warmwater fishing.

12. Dry Arm Reservoir (Walleye, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch)

Dry Arm Reservoir, Garfield County

Dry Arm Reservoir on the Fort Peck complex offers good fishing.

13. Hell Creek Arm (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie)

Hell Creek Arm, Garfield County

Hell Creek Arm of Fort Peck Reservoir offers excellent fishing with good access.

14. Fourchette Creek (Walleye, Northern Pike, Smallmouth Bass)

Fourchette Creek, Garfield County

Fourchette Creek offers good fishing in the Fort Peck area.

15. Rock Creek (MT) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Rock Creek, Valley County

Rock Creek offers good trout fishing northeast of Fort Peck.

16. Little Beaver Creek (Walleye, Northern Pike)

Little Beaver Creek offers good fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fwp.mt.gov. Conservation license required.
  • Fish Fort Peck Reservoir -- Fort Peck is one of the premier walleye and northern pike fisheries in North America.
  • Explore the Missouri Breaks -- The Missouri Breaks offer spectacular remote fishing in a wild and scenic river corridor.
  • Try the Milk River -- The Milk River offers excellent walleye and catfish fishing with good access.
  • Spring and fall are best -- May-June and September-October offer the best walleye and pike fishing.
