Fishing in Central Saskatchewan

The Saskatoon area offers the South Saskatchewan River, prairie reservoirs, and boreal forest lakes within easy reach of the city.

Central Saskatchewan, anchored by Saskatoon, offers a mix of prairie river fishing, parkland lakes, and the transition into the boreal forest. The South Saskatchewan River through Saskatoon provides excellent urban fishing, while a short drive in any direction leads to quality walleye, pike, and perch lakes.

1. South Saskatchewan River (Walleye, Northern Pike, Goldeye, Lake Sturgeon)

Through Saskatoon

The South Saskatchewan River through Saskatoon offers excellent urban fishing for walleye, northern pike, goldeye, and lake sturgeon. The walleye population has rebounded strongly in recent years. The river's current, deep pools, and structure hold fish throughout the summer. Key access points include the Meewasin Valley Trail, riverbank parks, and the weir area near the University of Saskatchewan. Bottom bouncing and jigging are productive techniques.

2. Pike Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

30 min south of Saskatoon

Pike Lake, located in Pike Lake Provincial Park, offers good fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold healthy fish populations. The park has a beach, campground, and golf course, making it a popular family destination.

3. Blackstrap Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

30 min south of Saskatoon

Blackstrap Lake, a man-made reservoir in Blackstrap Provincial Park, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake is known for its consistent walleye population. Blackstrap Provincial Park offers camping, hiking, and a beach. The lake's proximity to Saskatoon makes it a popular after-work destination.

4. Jackfish Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

45 min north of Saskatoon

Jackfish Lake, near the town of Blaine Lake, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake's stained water and weed beds provide good habitat. Jackfish Lake has a popular beach and several cottage developments. The lake is a favourite among Saskatoon anglers.

5. Murray Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

45 min north of Saskatoon

Murray Lake, located near Hepburn, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake is smaller than Jackfish Lake but offers good fishing with fewer crowds. Murray Lake has a boat launch and is a good option for anglers looking for a quieter experience.

6. Redberry Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

1 hour north of Saskatoon

Redberry Lake, located in Redberry Lake Biosphere Reserve, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake is a unique saline lake that supports healthy fish populations. The biosphere reserve designation recognizes the area's ecological significance. Redberry Lake has a campground and boat launch.

7. Wakaw Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

1 hour northeast of Saskatoon

Wakaw Lake, located near the town of Wakaw, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold healthy fish populations. Wakaw Lake has a popular recreation area with camping and a beach.

8. Lenore Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

1.5 hours northeast of Saskatoon

Lenore Lake, part of the Lenore Lake Basin, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake is shallower and more saline than the region's freshwater lakes. Lenore Lake offers a unique fishing experience in Saskatchewan's parkland region.

9. Fishing Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike, Perch)

1.5 hours east of Saskatoon

Fishing Lake, located near the town of Wadena, offers walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch fishing. The lake lives up to its name, providing consistent fishing in a parkland setting. Fishing Lake has a campground and boat launch.

10. Quill Lakes (Northern Pike, Walleye, Perch)

2 hours east of Saskatoon

The Quill Lakes — Big Quill, Little Quill, and Quill — are a unique chain of saline lakes that offer northern pike, walleye, and yellow perch fishing. Big Quill Lake is the largest saline lake in Canada. The lakes are an important bird area, hosting significant waterfowl populations. Fishing is best in spring and fall.

11. Manitou Lake (Northern Pike, Perch)

1.5 hours southeast of Saskatoon

Manitou Lake, near the town of Manitou Beach, offers northern pike and yellow perch fishing. The lake is a saline lake known for its mineral-rich waters and the Manitou Springs resort. Fishing is a secondary activity to the area's spa and wellness tourism.

12. Brightwater Creek & Gardiner Dam Area (Walleye, Northern Pike)

1 hour south of Saskatoon

The Brightwater Creek and Gardiner Dam area on the South Saskatchewan River offers walleye and northern pike fishing. The dam creates a popular fishing hole that holds fish year-round. The area is accessible from Saskatoon and offers good shore and boat fishing.

13. North Saskatchewan River (Walleye, Northern Pike, Goldeye, Sturgeon)

Flows through North Battleford, 2 hours northwest of Saskatoon

The North Saskatchewan River flows through North Battleford and offers walleye, northern pike, goldeye, and lake sturgeon fishing. The river's current and deep pools provide diverse habitat. The river is accessible from several parks in North Battleford and the surrounding area.

14. Jackpine Lake (Northern Pike, Perch)

1 hour north of Saskatoon

Jackpine Lake, located north of Saskatoon near the boreal forest transition, offers northern pike and yellow perch fishing. The lake is smaller and more remote than the region's more popular waters, offering a quieter experience.

15. Emma Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye, Perch)

1.5 hours north of Saskatoon

Emma Lake, located in the Prince Albert area, offers northern pike, walleye, and yellow perch fishing. The lake is part of a chain of lakes in the boreal forest transition zone. Emma Lake has a popular resort and campground area.

Fishing Tips for Central Saskatchewan

  • Fish the South Saskatchewan River — The river through Saskatoon offers excellent urban walleye fishing. Spring and fall are prime time.
  • Explore the parkland lakes — The area between Saskatoon and Prince Albert has hundreds of small lakes that offer excellent fishing with less pressure.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 16+ need a Saskatchewan fishing licence. Buy online at nirb.saskatchewan.ca.
  • Check water levels — River fishing conditions on the North and South Saskatchewan rivers can change rapidly with dam releases. Check water levels before heading out.
  • Ice fishing — Pike Lake, Blackstrap, and Jackfish Lake are popular ice fishing destinations near Saskatoon.
