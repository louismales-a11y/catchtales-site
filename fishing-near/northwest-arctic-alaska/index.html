Fishing in Northwest & Arctic Alaska

Nome, Kotzebue, Brooks Range, Arctic char, sheefish, and the remote waters of Arctic Alaska.

1. Nome Area (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling, Pink Salmon)

Nome, Seward Peninsula

Nome offers excellent fishing for Arctic char, Dolly Varden, and Arctic grayling in the rivers of the Seward Peninsula. The area also has good pink salmon runs in even-numbered years.

2. Kotzebue Sound (Arctic Char, Sheefish, Arctic Grayling)

Kotzebue, northwest Alaska

Kotzebue offers unique Arctic fishing for sheefish, Arctic char, and Arctic grayling in the Kotzebue Sound region. Sheefish are a prized Arctic species that can exceed 30 pounds.

3. Brooks Range Rivers (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char, Dolly Varden)

Brooks Range, northern Alaska

The rivers of the Brooks Range, including the Noatak and Kobuk, offer pristine Arctic grayling and char fishing in one of America's last wilderness areas.

4. Kobuk River (Sheefish, Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Kobuk River, northwest Alaska

The Kobuk River offers exceptional sheefish and Arctic char fishing in the northwestern Arctic. The river flows through Kobuk Valley National Park.

5. Noatak River (Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling, Dolly Varden)

Noatak River, Noatak National Preserve

The Noatak River flows through the largest undisturbed watershed in North America, offering world-class Arctic char and grayling fishing.

6. Selawik River (Sheefish, Arctic Char, Northern Pike)

Selawik River, northwest Alaska

The Selawik River offers excellent sheefish and northern pike fishing in the Selawik National Wildlife Refuge.

7. Buckland River (Arctic Char, Sheefish, Arctic Grayling)

Buckland River, Seward Peninsula

The Buckland River offers good Arctic char and sheefish fishing in the Seward Peninsula region.

8. Wulik River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden)

Wulik River, near Kivalina

The Wulik River offers excellent Arctic char and Dolly Varden fishing in northwest Alaska near the Red Dog Mine.

9. Utqiaġvik / Barrow (Arctic Char, Arctic Cod)

Utqiaġvik (Barrow), northernmost Alaska

Utqiaġvik offers unique Arctic fishing opportunities in the northernmost community in the US. Arctic char and Arctic cod are the primary targets.

10. Point Hope (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden)

Point Hope, northwest coast

Point Hope offers Arctic char fishing in one of the oldest continuously inhabited communities in North America.

11. Kivalina River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling)

Kivalina River, northwest Alaska

The Kivalina River offers good Arctic char and Dolly Varden fishing near the community of Kivalina on the Chukchi Sea.

12. Kelly River (Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling)

Kelly River, Noatak Preserve

The Kelly River is a tributary of the Noatak offering excellent Arctic char and grayling fishing in the Noatak National Preserve.

13. Eli River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden)

Eli River, Seward Peninsula

The Eli River offers good Arctic char and Dolly Varden fishing in the remote Seward Peninsula region.

14. Kuzitrin River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling)

Kuzitrin River, Seward Peninsula

The Kuzitrin River offers excellent Arctic char and grayling fishing in the Nome region of the Seward Peninsula.

15. Chukchi Sea Coast (Arctic Char, Arctic Cod)

Chukchi Sea coast, northwest Alaska

The Chukchi Sea coast offers unique Arctic saltwater fishing for Arctic char and Arctic cod in the remote northwest.

16. Fish River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling, Pink Salmon)

Fish River, Seward Peninsula

The Fish River offers excellent Arctic char and Dolly Varden fishing in the Seward Peninsula.

17. Niukluk River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling)

Niukluk River, Seward Peninsula

The Niukluk River offers good Arctic char and Dolly Varden fishing in the Nome area.

18. Koyuk River (Arctic Char, Sheefish, Northern Pike)

Koyuk River, Seward Peninsula

The Koyuk River offers good Arctic char and sheefish fishing in the Seward Peninsula.

19. Inglutalik River (Arctic Char, Dolly Varden, Arctic Grayling)

Inglutalik River, Seward Peninsula

The Inglutalik River offers good Arctic char and Dolly Varden fishing in the eastern Seward Peninsula.

20. Shaktoolik River (Sheefish, Arctic Char, Northern Pike)

Shaktoolik River, Norton Sound

The Shaktoolik River offers excellent sheefish and Arctic char fishing in the Norton Sound region.

21. Unalakleet River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Arctic Char, Sheefish)

Unalakleet River, Norton Sound

The Unalakleet River offers excellent king salmon and sheefish fishing in the Norton Sound region.

22. Shishmaref Area (Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling)

Shishmaref, Chukchi Sea

The Shishmaref area offers Arctic char and Arctic grayling fishing on the Chukchi Sea coast.

23. Kongakut River (Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling, Dolly Varden)

Kongakut River, Brooks Range

The Kongakut River in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge offers premier Arctic char fishing in a remote wilderness setting.

24. Hulahula River (Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling, Dolly Varden)

Hulahula River, Arctic Refuge

The Hulahula River flows through the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge offering excellent Arctic char and grayling fishing.

25. Canning River (Arctic Char, Arctic Grayling)

Canning River, Arctic Refuge

The Canning River offers good Arctic char and Arctic grayling fishing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.

26. Sagavanirktok River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char, Dolly Varden)

Sagavanirktok River, Prudhoe Bay

The Sagavanirktok River offers Arctic grayling and char fishing near the Prudhoe Bay oil fields.

27. Colville River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char, Northern Pike)

Colville River, North Slope

The Colville River is the largest river on Alaska's North Slope, offering Arctic grayling and char fishing.

28. Ikpikpuk River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char)

Ikpikpuk River, North Slope

The Ikpikpuk River offers good Arctic grayling and char fishing on the North Slope.

29. Meade River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char)

Meade River, North Slope

The Meade River offers good Arctic grayling and Arctic char fishing near Utqiaġvik.

30. Kuparuk River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char)

Kuparuk River, North Slope

The Kuparuk River offers Arctic grayling and char fishing on the North Slope near Deadhorse.

31. Anaktuvuk River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char)

Anaktuvuk River, Brooks Range

The Anaktuvuk River offers good Arctic char and grayling fishing in the central Brooks Range.

32. John River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char)

John River, Brooks Range

The John River offers excellent Arctic grayling and char fishing in the southern Brooks Range.

33. Koyukuk River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike, Sheefish)

Koyukuk River, interior/Arctic

The Koyukuk River offers excellent Arctic grayling, northern pike, and sheefish fishing in the Koyukuk National Wildlife Refuge.

34. Alatna River (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char)

Alatna River, Brooks Range

The Alatna River offers excellent Arctic grayling and char fishing in the Gates of the Arctic National Park.

35. Noatak River Upper (Arctic Grayling, Arctic Char, Dolly Varden)

Noatak River, Gates of the Arctic

The upper Noatak River flows through Gates of the Arctic National Park offering pristine Arctic fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at adfg.alaska.gov.
  • Target sheefish -- The Kobuk and Selawik rivers offer the best sheefish fishing in Alaska. These powerful fish are unique to the Arctic.
  • Book through Nome lodges -- Nome has several lodges offering guided trips to the Seward Peninsula's excellent Arctic char rivers.
  • Short season -- The Arctic fishing season runs from late June through August. Plan carefully and book well ahead.
  • Bring serious insect repellent -- The Arctic tundra has intense mosquito populations. A bug jacket and high-DEET repellent are essential.
