Fishing in North Georgia Mountains

Lake Burton, Lake Rabun, Lake Chatuge, Blue Ridge Lake, Carters Lake, and the trout and bass waters of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

1. Lake Burton (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Rainbow Trout, Walleye)

Lake Burton, Rabun County

Lake Burton is a 2,775-acre reservoir in the Blue Ridge Mountains offering excellent bass, trout, and walleye fishing in a scenic mountain setting.

2. Lake Rabun (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Rainbow Trout, Bream)

Lake Rabun, Rabun County

Lake Rabun is an 835-acre reservoir offering good bass and trout fishing in the north Georgia mountains.

3. Lake Chatuge (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Walleye, Crappie)

Lake Chatuge, Towns County

Lake Chatuge is a 7,050-acre TVA reservoir on the Georgia-North Carolina border offering excellent bass and walleye fishing.

4. Nottely Lake (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Crappie, Catfish)

Nottely Lake, Union County

Nottely Lake is a 4,180-acre TVA reservoir offering good bass and crappie fishing near Blairsville.

5. Blue Ridge Lake (Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass, Rainbow Trout, Walleye)

Blue Ridge Lake, Fannin County

Blue Ridge Lake is a 3,290-acre TVA reservoir offering excellent bass and trout fishing in the Blue Ridge Mountains.

6. Carters Lake (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Walleye, Rainbow Trout)

Carters Lake, Murray County

Carters Lake is a 3,200-acre deep-water reservoir offering excellent spotted bass, walleye, and trout fishing.

7. Lake Blue Ridge (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Walleye, Spotted Bass)

Lake Blue Ridge, Fannin County

Lake Blue Ridge offers good trout and walleye fishing near Morganton.

8. Toccoa River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Toccoa River, Fannin County

The Toccoa River offers excellent trout fishing in the Blue Ridge Mountains.

9. Chattahoochee River (Upper) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Upper Chattahoochee River, White/Habersham counties

The upper Chattahoochee River offers excellent trout fishing in the north Georgia mountains above Lake Lanier.

10. Smith Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Smith Creek, White County

Smith Creek offers excellent trout fishing at Unicoi State Park near Helen.

11. Dukes Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Dukes Creek, White County

Dukes Creek is a catch-and-release, artificial-lures-only trout stream near Helen.

12. Noontootla Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Noontootla Creek, Fannin County

Noontootla Creek offers excellent wild trout fishing in the Blue Ridge Mountains.

13. Coosawattee River (Spotted Bass, Largemouth Bass, Catfish)

Coosawattee River, Murray/Gilmer counties

The Coosawattee River offers good warm-water fishing below Carters Dam.

14. Conasauga River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Brook Trout)

Conasauga River, Murray County

The Conasauga River offers good trout fishing in the Cohutta Wilderness.

15. Vogel State Park Lake (Rainbow Trout, Bream, Catfish)

Vogel State Park, Union County

Vogel State Park's lake offers good family fishing in a beautiful mountain park.

16. Lake Winfield Scott (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Lake Winfield Scott, Union County

Lake Winfield Scott offers good trout fishing.

17. Fish Trap Lake (Rainbow Trout, Largemouth Bass)

Fish Trap Lake, Lumpkin County

Fish Trap Lake offers good fishing.

18. Dockery Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Dockery Lake, Lumpkin County

Dockery Lake offers good trout fishing.

19. Amicalola Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Amicalola Creek, Dawson County

Amicalola Creek offers good trout fishing.

20. Cooper Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout)

Cooper Creek, Fannin County

Cooper Creek offers good trout fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at georgiawildlife.com. A trout licence is required for trout fishing.
  • Fish the mountain reservoirs -- North Georgia's TVA reservoirs offer excellent fishing with no natural lakes in the state.
  • Try the Toccoa River -- The Toccoa River offers excellent trout fishing below Lake Blue Ridge Dam.
  • Spring trout stocking -- Georgia stocks thousands of trout each spring in north Georgia streams.
  • Fall fishing -- September through November offers the best fishing in the mountain lakes with comfortable temperatures.
