Fishing in the Mackenzie River Valley

Mackenzie River inconnu, Arctic grayling, Nahanni River walleye, Liard River pike, and the great river valley of NWT.

1. Mackenzie River (Arctic Grayling, Inconnu, Northern Pike)

Fort Providence area

The Mackenzie River at Fort Providence offers inconnu (coney), Arctic grayling, and northern pike fishing. Inconnu can exceed 30 pounds and are powerful fighters. This is one of the best locations in Canada to target this unique species.

2. South Nahanni River (Arctic Grayling, Walleye)

Nahanni National Park

The South Nahanni River in Nahanni National Park offers Arctic grayling and walleye fishing in a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Grayling in the 14-18 inch range are common. The river's spectacular scenery includes Virginia Falls, twice the height of Niagara.

3. Liard River (NWT Reach)

Liard River area, near Fort Simpson

The Liard River offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the NWT reach near its confluence with the Mackenzie River. The river's slow-moving sections and backwaters provide excellent pike habitat.

4. Mackenzie River (Arctic Grayling, Inconnu, Northern Pike)

Fort Simpson area

The Mackenzie River at the confluence with the Liard River at Fort Simpson offers excellent inconnu, Arctic grayling, and northern pike fishing. The meeting of these two great rivers creates productive fishing conditions.

5. Willow Lake (NWT)

Willow Lake area, near Fort Providence

Willow Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing near Fort Providence in the Mackenzie River valley. A productive lake with good fish populations and excellent bird watching.

6. Spence River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Spence River area, near Fort Providence

The Spence River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Mackenzie River valley near Fort Providence.

7. Horn Plateau (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout)

Horn Plateau area, west of Mackenzie River

The Horn Plateau offers remote lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in the Mackenzie Mountains west of the Mackenzie River. Accessible by float plane.

8. Rabbitskin River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Rabbitskin River area, Mackenzie valley

The Rabbitskin River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Mackenzie River valley near Fort Simpson.

9. North Nahanni River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

North Nahanni River area, near Fort Simpson

The North Nahanni River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Mackenzie River watershed. A productive river with good fish populations.

10. Birch River (NWT)

Birch River area, near Fort Simpson

The Birch River offers Arctic grayling fishing in the Liard River watershed near Fort Simpson in the Mackenzie Valley.

11. Poplar River (NWT)

Poplar River area, near Fort Providence

The Poplar River offers northern pike and Arctic grayling fishing in the Mackenzie River valley near Fort Providence.

12. Fort Providence Channel (Inconnu, Northern Pike)

Fort Providence area, Mackenzie River

The channels and backwaters of the Mackenzie River near Fort Providence offer excellent inconnu and northern pike fishing with good shore and boat access.

13. Mills Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Mills Lake area, Mackenzie River valley

Mills Lake offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the Mackenzie River floodplain near Fort Providence. A productive shallow lake with excellent bird life.

14. Jean Marie River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Jean Marie River area, Mackenzie valley

The Jean Marie River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Mackenzie River valley near the community of Jean Marie River.

15. Trout Lake (NWT)

Trout Lake area, Mackenzie valley

Trout Lake offers lake trout and northern pike fishing in the Mackenzie River valley. Despite its name, the lake holds good populations of both species.

16. Fort Liard Area (Northern Pike, Walleye)

Fort Liard area, Liard River

The Fort Liard area on the Liard River offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the southern NWT. The community provides access to the Liard River system.

17. Petitot River (Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Petitot River area, southern NWT

The Petitot River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Liard River watershed of the southern NWT near the BC border.

18. Blackstone River (Liard)

Blackstone River area, Liard watershed

The Blackstone River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Liard River watershed of the southern NWT.

19. Kotaneelee River (Arctic Grayling, Eel, Northern Pike)

Kotaneelee River area, Liard watershed

The Kotaneelee River offers Arctic grayling and northern pike fishing in the Liard River system of the southern NWT near the Yukon border.

20. Tlogotsho Range Lakes (Arctic Grayling, Lake Trout)

Tlogotsho Range area, Mackenzie Mountains

The lakes of the Tlogotsho Range in the Mackenzie Mountains offer remote lake trout and Arctic grayling fishing in a spectacular alpine setting.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at nwtfishing.ca. A separate NWT fishing licence is required for non-residents.
  • Target inconnu -- The Mackenzie River at Fort Providence offers the best inconnu fishing in Canada. Target them in June and July near the river mouth.
  • Explore Nahanni Park -- Nahanni National Park offers world-class wilderness canoeing and fishing. Multi-day trips require permits and experience.
  • Book a river guide -- Several Fort Simpson and Fort Providence guides offer Mackenzie River fishing trips targeting inconnu and pike.
  • Watch for the ferry -- The Mackenzie River ferry at Fort Providence is a unique experience. Watch for free-ranging bison near the road.
