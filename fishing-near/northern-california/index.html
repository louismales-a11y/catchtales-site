Fishing in Northern California

Shasta Lake, Trinity Lake, Sacramento River, Klamath River, and the diverse waters of far Northern California.

1. Shasta Lake (Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Rainbow Trout, Chinook Salmon, Crappie)

Shasta Lake, Shasta County

Shasta Lake is California's largest reservoir at 30,000 acres, offering excellent bass, trout, and salmon fishing in the shadow of Mount Shasta.

2. Trinity Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass)

Trinity Lake, Trinity County

Trinity Lake is a 17,000-acre reservoir offering excellent rainbow and brown trout fishing plus good bass fishing.

3. Sacramento River (Upper) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Upper Sacramento River, Shasta to Redding

The upper Sacramento River offers world-class rainbow and brown trout fishing in the tailwaters below Shasta Dam.

4. Klamath River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Steelhead, Rainbow Trout)

Klamath River, northern California

The Klamath River offers excellent salmon and steelhead fishing, one of the premier salmon rivers on the West Coast.

5. McCloud River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

McCloud River, Shasta County

The McCloud River offers excellent wild rainbow trout fishing in a scenic river below McCloud Reservoir.

6. Pit River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Pit River, Shasta/Modoc counties

The Pit River offers excellent trout and smallmouth bass fishing in northeastern California.

7. Fall River (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Fall River, Shasta County

The Fall River is a legendary spring-fed trout stream offering trophy rainbow trout in crystal-clear waters.

8. Hat Creek (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Hat Creek, Shasta County

Hat Creek is a famous spring-fed trout stream offering excellent fly fishing for rainbow and brown trout.

9. Lake Almanor (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass)

Lake Almanor, Plumas County

Lake Almanor is a 28,000-acre reservoir offering excellent trout and bass fishing in the northern Sierra foothills.

10. Whiskeytown Lake (Rainbow Trout, Kokanee Salmon, Largemouth Bass)

Whiskeytown Lake, Shasta County

Whiskeytown Lake offers good trout, kokanee, and bass fishing in a scenic national recreation area.

11. Lewiston Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Lewiston Lake, Trinity County

Lewiston Lake offers excellent trout fishing in the Trinity River system.

12. Trinity River (Steelhead, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon)

Trinity River, Trinity County

The Trinity River offers excellent salmon and steelhead fishing, a major tributary of the Klamath River.

13. Eel River (Steelhead, Chinook Salmon)

Eel River, Humboldt/Mendocino counties

The Eel River offers good steelhead and salmon fishing in a scenic coastal river.

14. Smith River (Steelhead, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon)

Smith River, Del Norte County

The Smith River is California's last free-flowing major river, offering excellent steelhead and salmon fishing.

15. Clear Creek (Rainbow Trout, Steelhead)

Clear Creek, Shasta County

Clear Creek offers good trout and steelhead fishing below Whiskeytown Dam.

16. Ruth Lake (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Largemouth Bass)

Ruth Lake, Trinity County

Ruth Lake offers trout and bass fishing in a remote Trinity County setting.

17. Big Bear Lake (Northern CA) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout)

Big Bear Lake, Plumas County

Big Bear Lake in Plumas County offers good trout fishing in the northern Sierra Nevada.

18. Lake Oroville (Rainbow Trout, Chinook Salmon, Largemouth Bass, Spotted Bass)

Lake Oroville, Butte County

Lake Oroville is a 15,800-acre reservoir offering excellent trout, salmon, and bass fishing in the Sierra foothills.

19. Feather River (Steelhead, Chinook Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Feather River, Butte/Yuba counties

The Feather River offers excellent salmon and steelhead fishing below Oroville Dam.

20. Yuba River (Steelhead, Rainbow Trout, Smallmouth Bass)

Yuba River, Nevada/Yuba counties

The Yuba River offers good steelhead and smallmouth bass fishing in the Sierra foothills.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at wildlife.ca.gov. Non-resident licences available.
  • Fish the Sacramento River -- The Sacramento River below Shasta Dam offers world-class trout fishing year-round.
  • Try the Fall River -- The Fall River is a world-famous spring creek offering trophy trout in challenging conditions.
  • Watch for salmon seasons -- Salmon seasons on the Klamath and Sacramento rivers are regulated and may be restricted. Check current regulations.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-June and September-November offer the best fishing in Northern California.
