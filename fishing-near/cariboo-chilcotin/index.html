Fishing in the Cariboo & Chilcotin

Thousands of rainbow trout lakes, Fraser River headwaters, and remote wilderness fishing in BC's central interior.

1. Horsefly River

Horsefly area

The Horsefly River is famous for its large sockeye salmon runs. The river also offers rainbow trout and steelhead fishing in the Cariboo region.

2. Quesnel Lake

Quesnel area

Quesnel Lake is the deepest fjord lake in the world and offers trophy rainbow trout, lake trout, and kokanee fishing.

3. Quesnel River

Quesnel

The Quesnel River offers rainbow trout and salmon fishing flowing from Quesnel Lake to the Fraser River.

4. Chilko Lake

Chilcotin region

Chilko Lake offers large rainbow trout and lake trout in a remote, stunning setting. The lake is one of BC's premier fly-in trout destinations.

5. Chilcotin River

Chilcotin region

The Chilcotin River offers rainbow trout, steelhead, and salmon fishing in the remote Chilcotin Plateau.

6. Fraser River (Upper)

Prince George area

The upper Fraser River offers rainbow trout, bull trout, and salmon fishing in the central interior.

7. Nechako River

Vanderhoof area

The Nechako River offers excellent rainbow trout and sturgeon fishing. The river is known for large rainbow trout.

8. Stuart Lake

Fort St. James area

Stuart Lake offers rainbow trout, lake trout, and kokanee fishing in the northern Cariboo.

9. Trembleur Lake

Fort St. James area

Trembleur Lake offers excellent rainbow trout and lake trout fishing in the Stuart River system.

10. Takla Lake

North of Fort St. James

Takla Lake offers rainbow trout, lake trout, and Dolly Varden fishing in a remote northern setting.

11. Dease Lake

Northern BC

Dease Lake offers lake trout, Arctic grayling, and rainbow trout fishing in far northern BC.

12. Tatlayoko Lake

Chilcotin region

Tatlayoko Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in a stunning setting at the head of the Homathko River.

13. Nazko Lakes

West of Quesnel

The Nazko Lakes offer excellent rainbow trout fishing in the Cariboo region. These small lakes are known for producing quality fish.

14. Bowron Lakes

Bowron Lake Park

The Bowron Lake canoe circuit offers rainbow trout and Dolly Varden fishing in a world-famous canoeing destination.

15. Barkerville Area Streams

Barkerville

The streams near historic Barkerville offer rainbow trout fishing in the Cariboo gold rush region.

16. Williston Lake

Near Mackenzie

Williston Lake is one of the largest reservoirs in North America, created by the Bennett Dam on the Peace River. The lake offers excellent rainbow trout, lake trout, and Arctic grayling fishing in the northern Rocky Mountain Trench.

17. Peace River (Upper)

Near Hudson's Hope

The upper Peace River offers excellent rainbow trout, Arctic grayling, and bull trout fishing below the Bennett Dam. The river's cold, clear waters provide excellent habitat.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fishing.gov.bc.ca.
  • Try the Horsefly River -- The Horsefly River sockeye run is one of the largest in North America. August is peak season.
  • Explore Chilko Lake -- Chilko Lake offers some of the best fly-in trout fishing in BC.
  • Bring insect repellent -- The Cariboo and Chilcotin have significant mosquito populations in early summer.
  • Plan for remote travel -- Many Cariboo and Chilcotin fishing spots require travel on gravel roads. Check road conditions before heading out.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
