Fishing in the Skeena & North Central BC

The legendary Skeena River steelhead, Babine Lake trout, Bulkley River salmon -- world-class fishing in BC's northwest.

1. Skeena River (Steelhead)

Terrace to Prince Rupert

The Skeena River is one of the world's premier steelhead rivers. The river's massive size and powerful current produce steelhead averaging 10-15 pounds, with fish over 20 pounds common.

2. Skeena River (Salmon)

Skeena watershed

The Skeena River hosts runs of all five Pacific salmon species. Chinook and coho are the primary targets for anglers.

3. Babine Lake

Near Smithers

Babine Lake is the largest natural lake in BC and offers excellent rainbow trout, lake trout, and kokanee fishing. The lake is famous for its large rainbow trout.

4. Bulkley River

Smithers area

The Bulkley River is a major tributary of the Skeena, offering world-class steelhead and salmon fishing. The river's clear water and classic runs make it a fly fishing paradise.

5. Kitimat River

Kitimat

The Kitimat River offers excellent salmon and steelhead fishing near the coast. The river's short, powerful run produces bright, aggressive fish.

6. Kispiox River

Kispiox area

The Kispiox River is a famous Skeena tributary known for producing large steelhead. The river's clear, cold water and classic runs are ideal for fly fishing.

7. Nass River

Nass Valley

The Nass River offers steelhead and salmon fishing in a remote wilderness setting. The river is known for large, powerful steelhead.

8. Morice River

Near Houston

The Morice River is a major steelhead stream in the Skeena system. The river also offers excellent salmon fishing.

9. Lakelse Lake

Near Terrace

Lakelse Lake offers rainbow trout, kokanee, and Dolly Varden fishing near Terrace. The lake is a popular family destination.

10. Sustut River

Remote northern BC

The Sustut River is a remote Skeena tributary offering wilderness steelhead fishing. Access is by float plane or helicopter.

11. Zymoetz River (Copper)

Terrace area

The Zymoetz River, also known as the Copper River, offers steelhead and salmon fishing near Terrace.

12. Kitsumkalum Lake

Near Terrace

Kitsumkalum Lake offers rainbow trout and Dolly Varden fishing near Terrace. The lake is a popular recreation area.

13. Bear River

Stewart area

The Bear River offers salmon and steelhead fishing near Stewart in the remote north coast region.

14. Stikine River

Northwestern BC

The Stikine River is a major northern river offering steelhead, salmon, and rainbow trout fishing in a remote wilderness setting. The river flows from the Spatsizi Plateau to the Pacific near Wrangell, Alaska.

15. Liard River

Northern BC

The Liard River flows through far northern BC, offering Arctic grayling, northern pike, and rainbow trout fishing. The Liard Hot Springs provide a unique post-fishing soak.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fishing.gov.bc.ca. A salmon conservation stamp is required.
  • Book steelhead trips early -- Skeena steelhead lodges book far in advance. Plan your trip months or years ahead.
  • Fish in fall -- September and October offer the best steelhead fishing on the Skeena system.
  • Bring quality rain gear -- The Skeena region is coastal rainforest with frequent rain.
  • Hire a guide -- The Skeena's large size makes guided trips the most productive option for visitors.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
