Fishing in South Florida & Everglades

Lake Okeechobee, Everglades National Park, Miami, Biscayne Bay, and the subtropical fishing paradise.

1. Lake Okeechobee (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Speckled Perch, Bluegill, Catfish)

Lake Okeechobee, multiple counties

Lake Okeechobee is the second-largest freshwater lake entirely within the US at 467,000 acres. It is one of the best bass fisheries in the world, producing trophy largemouth bass year after year.

2. Everglades National Park (Tarpon, Snook, Redfish, Snapper, Largemouth Bass)

Everglades National Park, Miami-Dade/Monroe counties

The Everglades offers world-class fishing for tarpon, snook, redfish, and snapper in a unique wilderness ecosystem.

3. Biscayne Bay (Bonefish, Permit, Tarpon, Snook, Snapper)

Biscayne Bay, Miami-Dade County

Biscayne Bay offers world-class flats fishing for bonefish, permit, and tarpon in the shadow of Miami.

4. Miami Beach (Kingfish, Mackerel, Snapper, Grouper)

Miami Beach, Miami-Dade County

Miami Beach offers good offshore and pier fishing for kingfish and mackerel.

5. Government Cut (Tarpon, Snook, Jack Crevalle, Snapper)

Government Cut, Miami

Government Cut offers excellent fishing at the Miami harbor entrance.

6. Key Biscayne (Bonefish, Permit, Tarpon, Snook)

Key Biscayne, Miami-Dade County

Key Biscayne offers excellent flats fishing around Cape Florida.

7. Flamingo (Tarpon, Snook, Redfish, Trout)

Flamingo, Everglades National Park

Flamingo at the southern tip of the Everglades offers excellent fishing in Florida Bay.

8. Shark River (Tarpon, Snook, Redfish)

Shark River, Everglades National Park

Shark River offers excellent backcountry fishing in the Everglades.

9. Whitewater Bay (Redfish, Snook, Tarpon, Trout)

Whitewater Bay, Everglades National Park

Whitewater Bay offers excellent fishing in Florida Bay.

10. Florida Bay (Bonefish, Permit, Tarpon, Redfish, Snapper)

Florida Bay, Monroe County

Florida Bay offers world-class flats fishing between the Everglades and the Keys.

11. Ichetucknee River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish)

Ichetucknee River, Columbia County

The Ichetucknee River offers good spring-fed river fishing.

12. Alligator Alley Canals (Peacock Bass, Largemouth Bass, Mayan Cichlid)

Alligator Alley (I-75), Broward/Collier counties

The canals along Alligator Alley offer unique fishing for peacock bass and Mayan cichlids.

13. Snake Creek Canal (Peacock Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Snake Creek Canal, Miami-Dade County

Snake Creek Canal offers good peacock bass fishing in urban Miami.

14. Blue Cypress Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Blue Cypress Lake, Indian River County

Blue Cypress Lake offers good bass fishing in the upper St. Johns River basin.

15. Loxahatchee National Wildlife Refuge (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bluegill)

Loxahatchee NWR, Palm Beach County

Loxahatchee offers good fishing in the northern Everglades.

16. Tamiami Canal (Peacock Bass, Largemouth Bass, Mayan Cichlid)

Tamiami Canal, Miami-Dade County

The Tamiami Canal offers excellent peacock bass fishing.

17. C-100 Canal (Peacock Bass, Largemouth Bass)

C-100 Canal, Miami-Dade County

South Florida canals offer excellent peacock bass fishing.

18. C-4 Canal (Peacock Bass, Largemouth Bass)

C-4 Canal, Miami-Dade County

Canals offer good peacock bass fishing.

19. Coral Reef Park Lake (Peacock Bass, Mayan Cichlid)

Coral Reef Park, Miami-Dade County

Urban lakes offer peacock bass fishing.

20. Kendall Lakes (Peacock Bass, Largemouth Bass)

Kendall, Miami-Dade County

Kendall area lakes offer good fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at myfwc.com.
  • Fish Lake Okeechobee -- Lake Okeechobee is one of the best bass lakes in the world. Winter and early spring are peak.
  • Explore the Everglades -- Everglades National Park offers world-class backcountry fishing for tarpon and snook.
  • Try peacock bass -- South Florida's canals offer the only peacock bass fishing in the US.
  • Hire a flats guide -- Biscayne Bay and Florida Bay have excellent guides for bonefish and permit.
