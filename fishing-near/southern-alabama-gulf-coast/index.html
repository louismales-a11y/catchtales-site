Fishing in Southern Alabama & Gulf Coast

Mobile Bay, Gulf Shores, Dauphin Island, Orange Beach, and the best saltwater fishing on Alabama's Gulf Coast.

1. Mobile Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder, Sheepshead)

Mobile / Daphne / Fairhope area

Mobile Bay is one of the most productive estuaries on the Gulf Coast, offering excellent speckled trout, redfish, flounder, and sheepshead fishing. The bay's extensive marsh grass and oyster reefs provide ideal habitat for inshore species.

2. Gulf Shores (Spanish Mackerel, Redfish, Speckled Trout, Pompano)

Gulf Shores area

Gulf Shores offers excellent surf fishing from the beach for Spanish mackerel, pompano, and redfish. The Gulf State Park Pier is one of the most popular fishing piers on the Gulf Coast, providing access to a variety of species.

3. Dauphin Island (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder, King Mackerel)

Dauphin Island, south of Mobile

Dauphin Island offers exceptional fishing from its beaches, piers, and the Dauphin Island Pier. The island's east end and west end beaches provide excellent surf fishing for speckled trout, redfish, and flounder.

4. Orange Beach (Red Snapper, Amberjack, Grouper, Mackerel)

Orange Beach area

Orange Beach is the premier offshore fishing destination in Alabama. Charter boats target red snapper, amberjack, grouper, and mackerel on the extensive artificial reef system and natural bottom areas.

5. Bon Secour Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

Bon Secour area, south of Foley

Bon Secour Bay offers excellent inshore fishing for speckled trout and redfish in a quieter, less-pressured setting than Mobile Bay. The Bon Secour National Wildlife Refuge protects the surrounding habitat.

6. Perdido Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

Perdido Beach / Orange Beach area

Perdido Bay straddles the Alabama-Florida line and offers excellent inshore fishing for speckled trout, redfish, and flounder. The bay's grassy flats and tidal creeks provide prime habitat.

7. Alabama River (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Catfish)

Alabama River, central to southern Alabama

The Alabama River flows from the confluence of the Coosa and Tallapoosa rivers to Mobile Bay, offering largemouth bass, striped bass, and catfish fishing in a large river system.

8. Mobile River (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Catfish)

Mobile area

The Mobile River offers freshwater fishing for largemouth bass and striped bass in its upper reaches, transitioning to saltwater species as it approaches Mobile Bay.

9. Tensaw River (Largemouth Bass, Redfish, Speckled Trout)

Tensaw Delta, north of Mobile Bay

The Tensaw River flows through the Mobile-Tensaw Delta, one of the most diverse ecosystems in North America. The river offers a mix of freshwater and saltwater species.

10. Gulf State Park Pier (Spanish Mackerel, Pompano, Redfish, Flounder)

Gulf Shores

The Gulf State Park Pier extends 825 feet into the Gulf of Mexico and is one of the most popular fishing piers on the Gulf Coast. It provides excellent access for Spanish mackerel, pompano, and redfish.

11. Fort Morgan (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Pompano)

Fort Morgan, west of Gulf Shores

Fort Morgan offers excellent surf fishing along the beaches near the historic fort. The area is known for productive speckled trout and redfish fishing.

12. Fairhope Pier (Speckled Trout, Flounder, Sheepshead)

Fairhope, Mobile Bay

The Fairhope Municipal Pier extends into Mobile Bay and offers excellent fishing for speckled trout, flounder, and sheepshead. A popular family fishing destination with bait and tackle nearby.

13. Weeks Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

Weeks Bay area, near Fairhope

Weeks Bay is a nutrient-rich estuary offering excellent speckled trout and redfish fishing. The Weeks Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve protects this productive ecosystem.

14. Mississippi Sound (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

Mississippi Sound, west of Mobile Bay

The Mississippi Sound along Alabama's Gulf Coast offers excellent inshore fishing for speckled trout and redfish in the shallow waters between the barrier islands and the mainland.

15. Artificial Reefs (Red Snapper, Amberjack, Grouper)

Offshore Gulf of Mexico

Alabama has one of the largest artificial reef programs in the US, with thousands of reef sites offshore. These reefs attract red snapper, amberjack, grouper, and other reef fish for charter boat anglers.

16. Gulf Shores Beach (Spanish Mackerel, Pompano, Redfish)

Gulf Shores, Baldwin County

Gulf Shores beaches offer excellent surf fishing.

17. Orange Beach Pier (Spanish Mackerel, Redfish, Flounder)

Orange Beach, Baldwin County

The Orange Beach Pier offers excellent fishing.

18. Wolf Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

Wolf Bay, Baldwin County

Wolf Bay offers good inshore fishing.

19. Oyster Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish)

Oyster Bay, Baldwin County

Oyster Bay offers good inshore fishing.

20. Terry Cove (Speckled Trout, Flounder)

Terry Cove, Baldwin County

Terry Cove offers good fishing near Orange Beach.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at outdooralabama.com. Saltwater fishing requires an additional saltwater endorsement.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-May and September-November offer the best inshore fishing for speckled trout and redfish.
  • Book a Gulf Coast charter -- Orange Beach and Dauphin Island have numerous charter operators targeting red snapper and other offshore species.
  • Fish the pier at Gulf State Park -- The Gulf State Park Pier is open 24/7 and provides excellent family fishing without a boat.
  • Watch the tides -- Inshore fishing in Mobile Bay is strongly influenced by tides. Incoming tides generally produce the best action.
