9. Armstrong Area Lakes (Lake Trout)

Armstrong

Lake Nipigon is the largest lake entirely within Ontario, covering 4,848 square kilometres of deep, clear water north of Lake Superior. The lake is famous for its world-class lake trout fishery, with fish in the 5-15 pound range common and trophies exceeding 30 pounds caught every year.

The lake also holds exceptional brook trout, northern pike, and walleye populations. The brook trout in Lake Nipigon are particularly notable -- the lake produced the former world record brook trout of 14.5 pounds in 1915. The Nipigon River, flowing from the lake to Lake Superior, also offers world-class brook trout and walleye fishing.

Key access points include Greenstone, Beardmore, and Lake Nipigon Provincial Park. The lake's remote character and large size demand careful trip planning. Anglers should be prepared for rapidly changing weather conditions on this large body of water.

35. Big Trout Lake (Lake Trout, Northern Pike)

Far northern Ontario, fly-in only

Big Trout Lake is a large, remote lake in the far north of Ontario, located in the Kitchenuhmaykoosib Inninuwug First Nation territory. The lake offers excellent northern pike and lake trout fishing in a pristine wilderness setting. The lake is fly-in only, accessible through the community of Kitchenuhmaykoosib (Big Trout Lake). The lake's remote location and minimal fishing pressure produce quality fish. Lake trout in the 5-15 pound range are common.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Plan extensively -- Far northern Ontario fly-in lodges book years in advance.
  • Bring warm clothing -- Even in July, northern Ontario can be cold and windy.
  • Respect the resource -- Northern fisheries are fragile. Practise careful catch-and-release.
  • Book a fly-in trip -- Fly-in fishing is the best way to access Ontario's remote northern lakes.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
