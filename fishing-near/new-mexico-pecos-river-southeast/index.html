Fishing in the Pecos River & Southeast

Pecos River, Santa Rosa Lake, Brantley Lake, Carlsbad, and the largemouth bass, walleye, channel catfish, and crappie fishing of southeastern New Mexico.

1. Pecos River (Middle) (Rainbow Trout, Brown Trout, Largemouth Bass, Channel Catfish, Crappie, Walleye)

Pecos River, San Miguel/Guadalupe counties

The middle Pecos River flows through the rolling plains of eastern New Mexico offering good catfish and bass fishing below the Santa Rosa Lake dam.

2. Santa Rosa Lake (Walleye, Largemouth Bass, Smallmouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, White Bass, Wiper)

Santa Rosa Lake, Guadalupe County

Santa Rosa Lake is a 4,100-acre reservoir on the Pecos River offering excellent walleye and wiper fishing with a state park.

3. Brantley Lake (Largemouth Bass, Walleye, Crappie, Channel Catfish, White Bass, Bluegill, Redear Sunfish)

Brantley Lake, Eddy County

Brantley Lake is a 4,600-acre reservoir on the Pecos River near Carlsbad offering excellent bass and crappie fishing.

4. Lake Carlsbad (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Bluegill, Walleye, Redear Sunfish)

Lake Carlsbad, Eddy County

Lake Carlsbad is a 230-acre lake in Carlsbad offering good warmwater fishing with a city park.

5. Avalon Lake (Largemouth Bass, Walleye, Channel Catfish, Crappie, Bluegill, White Bass)

Avalon Lake, Eddy County

Avalon Lake is a 500-acre reservoir on the Pecos River offering good bass and walleye fishing.

6. McMillan Lake (Largemouth Bass, Walleye, Crappie, Channel Catfish, Bluegill)

Fishing Tips for the Pecos River & Southeast

  • Check local regulations before fishing — each water body may have specific rules on limits, seasons, and bait.
  • Spring and fall typically offer the best fishing in New Mexico for most species.
  • Consider hiring a local guide for the best experience on unfamiliar waters.
  • Always carry a valid New Mexico fishing license — available online through the state wildlife agency.
  • Practice catch and release for sensitive species to help maintain healthy fish populations.
