Fishing in the Kootenays

Trout, char, sturgeon, and bass in southeastern BC. Kootenay Lake, Elk River, Columbia River, and the Rocky Mountain trenches.

1. Kootenay Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Nelson/Crawford Bay

Kootenay Lake is one of BC's premier fishing lakes, offering rainbow trout, bull trout, kokanee, and the famous Gerrard rainbow trout. Gerrards can exceed 20 pounds.

2. Elk River (Cutthroat Trout)

Fernie area

The Elk River offers excellent bull trout and cutthroat trout fishing in the Rocky Mountain foothills. The river's clear, cold waters are ideal for fly fishing.

3. Columbia River (Cutthroat Trout, Rainbow Trout)

Invermere area

The upper Columbia River offers rainbow trout, bull trout, and kokanee fishing. The river flows through the Columbia Valley wetlands.

4. Columbia River (Rainbow Trout, Walleye)

Castlegar/Trail area

The lower Columbia River offers walleye, sturgeon, and rainbow trout fishing in the broader sections near Trail and Castlegar.

5. Kootenay River (Rainbow Trout, Walleye)

Creston area

The Kootenay River offers rainbow trout, bull trout, and walleye fishing. The river flows through Kootenay National Park and the Rocky Mountain Trench.

6. Moyle Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Near Cranbrook

Moyle Lake offers rainbow trout and kokanee fishing in a scenic Kootenay lake. The lake is popular for family camping.

7. Wasa Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Wasa

Wasa Lake offers rainbow trout fishing and is known for its warm waters, making it one of the few swimming lakes in the Kootenays.

8. Cranbrook Area Lakes (Rainbow Trout)

Cranbrook

The Cranbrook area has numerous small lakes offering rainbow trout fishing, including Jimsmith Lake and Norbury Lake.

9. Windermere Lake (Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout)

Invermere

Windermere Lake offers rainbow trout and lake trout fishing near Invermere in the Columbia Valley.

10. Arrow Lakes (Rainbow Trout)

Revelstoke/Nakusp area

The Arrow Lakes are a massive reservoir system offering rainbow trout, kokanee, and Dolly Varden fishing.

11. Revelstoke Reach (Rainbow Trout)

Revelstoke

The Revelstoke Reach of the Arrow Lakes offers excellent rainbow trout and kokanee fishing near Revelstoke.

12. Kinbasket Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Valemount area

Kinbasket Lake, created by the Mica Dam, offers rainbow trout and bull trout fishing in a massive reservoir setting.

13. Slocan Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Slocan Valley

Slocan Lake offers rainbow trout, kokanee, and Dolly Varden fishing in the beautiful Slocan Valley.

14. Flathead River (Cutthroat Trout)

Southeastern BC

The Flathead River offers excellent cutthroat trout and bull trout fishing in a remote wilderness area near the US border.

15. Duncan Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Duncan area

Duncan Lake offers rainbow trout and Dolly Varden fishing in the Kootenay Lake region.

16. Moyie Lake (Cutthroat Trout, Lake Trout, Rainbow Trout, Walleye)

Moyie

Moyie Lake offers rainbow trout and kokanee fishing in a scenic Kootenay lake.

17. Tie Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Jaffray area

Tie Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Elk Valley.

18. Lillian Lake (Rainbow Trout)

South Country area

Lillian Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the southern Kootenays.

19. New Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Fermie area

New Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Elk Valley.

20. Lake Koocanusa (Rainbow Trout, Walleye)

Fermie area

Lake Koocanusa is a large reservoir offering rainbow trout, kokanee, and walleye fishing.

21. Doris Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Cranbrook area

Doris Lake offers rainbow trout fishing near Cranbrook.

22. Kikomun Creek Lakes (Rainbow Trout)

Kikomun Park

Kikomun Creek Provincial Park has several lakes offering rainbow trout and bass fishing.

23. Rosell Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Kikomun area

Rosell Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Kikomun area.

24. Mack Creek (Rainbow Trout)

Near Revelstoke

Mack Creek offers rainbow trout fishing in the Revelstoke area.

25. Mount Revelstoke Lakes (Rainbow Trout)

Revelstoke

The lakes in Mount Revelstoke National Park offer rainbow trout fishing in an alpine setting.

26. Trout Lake (Kootenay)

Trout Lake area

Trout Lake in the Kootenays offers rainbow trout and Dolly Varden fishing.

27. Beaton Arm (Rainbow Trout)

Beaton area

The Beaton Arm of the Arrow Lakes offers rainbow trout and kokanee fishing.

28. Ferguson Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Lardeau area

Ferguson Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Duncan Lake area.

29. Tikitiki Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Near Nakusp

Tikitiki Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Arrow Lakes region.

30. Merritt Creek (Cutthroat Trout)

Near Crawford Bay

Merritt Creek offers cutthroat trout fishing in the Kootenay Lake area.

31. Leach Lake (Rainbow Trout)

Aberfeldie area

Leach Lake offers rainbow trout fishing in the Fernie area.

32. Island Lake (Cutthroat Trout, Rainbow Trout)

Near Fernie

Island Lake offers rainbow trout and cutthroat trout fishing in the Elk Valley.

33. Lake of the Hanging Glacier (Cutthroat Trout)

Near Invermere

The Lake of the Hanging Glacier is an alpine lake offering cutthroat trout fishing in a stunning setting.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at fishing.gov.bc.ca.
  • Try Kootenay Lake -- Kootenay Lake offers the famous Gerrard rainbow trout, which can exceed 20 pounds.
  • Fish the Elk River -- The Elk River near Fernie offers excellent fly fishing for bull trout and cutthroat trout.
  • Watch for bears -- The Kootenays have significant bear populations. Carry bear spray and be bear aware.
  • Explore the backcountry -- The Kootenays have hundreds of remote lakes accessible by hiking or canoeing.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
