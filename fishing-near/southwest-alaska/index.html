Fishing in Southwest Alaska

Kodiak Island, Bristol Bay, Aleutian Islands, Katmai, and the world's greatest sockeye salmon runs.

1. Bristol Bay (Sockeye Salmon, Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Bristol Bay, southwest Alaska

Bristol Bay is home to the world's largest sockeye salmon runs, with tens of millions of fish returning each year. The region also offers excellent king salmon, silver salmon, and rainbow trout fishing in its many rivers.

2. Kodiak Island (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Sockeye Salmon, Halibut)

Kodiak Island, Gulf of Alaska

Kodiak Island offers world-class salmon fishing and halibut fishing in a stunning coastal setting. The island's rivers host all five species of Pacific salmon, plus trophy rainbow trout.

3. Naknek River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Naknek River, Katmai area

The Naknek River offers incredible sockeye salmon runs and trophy rainbow trout fishing near Katmai National Park. The river is famous for its large rainbow trout that follow the salmon runs.

4. Katmai National Park Rivers (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Katmai National Park

Katmai National Park's rivers, including the Brooks River, offer spectacular sockeye salmon viewing and fishing. The park is also famous for brown bear viewing as bears gather to fish for salmon.

5. Alagnak River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char, Northern Pike)

Alagnak River, Katmai region

The Alagnak River is a designated wild river offering exceptional salmon and rainbow trout fishing in the Katmai region.

6. Nushagak River (Chinook Salmon, Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Nushagak River, Bristol Bay

The Nushagak River offers excellent king salmon and sockeye salmon fishing in the Bristol Bay watershed.

7. Kvichak River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Kvichak River, Bristol Bay

The Kvichak River is one of the most productive sockeye salmon rivers in the world, with runs exceeding 10 million fish in peak years.

8. Wood River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Wood River, Bristol Bay

The Wood River system offers excellent sockeye salmon and rainbow trout fishing in the Bristol Bay watershed.

9. Aleutian Islands (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish, Lingcod)

Aleutian Islands, southwest Alaska

The Aleutian Islands offer some of the most remote fishing in Alaska. The cold, productive waters provide world-class halibut, salmon, and rockfish fishing.

10. Unalaska / Dutch Harbor (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish)

Unalaska, Aleutian Islands

Dutch Harbor is one of the largest fishing ports in the world. The surrounding waters offer excellent halibut and salmon fishing, plus unique Aleutian cultural experiences.

11. Kuskokwim River (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Arctic Grayling, Northern Pike)

Kuskokwim River, southwest Alaska

The Kuskokwim River is one of Alaska's largest rivers, offering excellent salmon and freshwater fishing in the southwest region.

12. Togiak River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Togiak River, Bristol Bay

The Togiak River offers excellent sockeye salmon and rainbow trout fishing in the Bristol Bay region, with stunning tundra scenery.

13. Ugashik Lakes (Sockeye Salmon, Arctic Char, Rainbow Trout)

Ugashik Lakes, Alaska Peninsula

The Ugashik Lakes on the Alaska Peninsula offer excellent sockeye salmon, Arctic char, and rainbow trout fishing in a remote setting.

14. Iliamna Lake (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Lake Trout, Arctic Char)

Iliamna Lake, Bristol Bay

Iliamna Lake is the largest lake in Alaska, offering excellent sockeye salmon, lake trout, and rainbow trout fishing.

15. Chignik River (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Chignik River, Alaska Peninsula

The Chignik River offers good salmon and rainbow trout fishing on the Alaska Peninsula, accessible by air from Anchorage.

16. Brooks River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Brooks River, Katmai National Park

The Brooks River in Katmai National Park is world-famous for its sockeye salmon runs and brown bear viewing. Fishing is strictly regulated to protect the bears.

17. Kulik River (Rainbow Trout, Sockeye Salmon, Arctic Char)

Kulik River, Katmai region

The Kulik River offers trophy rainbow trout fishing in the Katmai region, with fish exceeding 30 inches.

18. Moraine Creek (Rainbow Trout, Sockeye Salmon)

Moraine Creek, Katmai region

Moraine Creek offers excellent rainbow trout and sockeye salmon fishing in the Katmai National Preserve.

19. American Creek (Rainbow Trout, Sockeye Salmon, Arctic Char)

American Creek, Katmai region

American Creek offers trophy rainbow trout fishing in the Katmai region.

20. Battle River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Battle River, Alaska Peninsula

The Battle River offers good sockeye salmon and rainbow trout fishing on the Alaska Peninsula.

21. King Salmon River (Chinook Salmon, Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

King Salmon River, Naknek area

The King Salmon River offers excellent king salmon and rainbow trout fishing near Naknek.

22. Egegik River (Sockeye Salmon, Chinook Salmon, Arctic Char)

Egegik River, Bristol Bay

The Egegik River offers excellent sockeye and king salmon fishing in the Bristol Bay region.

23. Igushik River (Sockeye Salmon, Arctic Char, Rainbow Trout)

Igushik River, Bristol Bay

The Igushik River offers good sockeye salmon and Arctic char fishing in the Bristol Bay watershed.

24. Koggiling River (Sockeye Salmon, Arctic Char)

Koggiling River, Bristol Bay

The Koggiling River offers good sockeye salmon fishing in the Bristol Bay region.

25. Meshik River (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Meshik River, Alaska Peninsula

The Meshik River offers good salmon and rainbow trout fishing on the Alaska Peninsula near Port Heiden.

26. Kukak Bay (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish)

Kukak Bay, Katmai coast

Kukak Bay offers excellent saltwater fishing in the Katmai National Park coastal area, combining halibut fishing with bear viewing.

27. Geographic Harbor (Halibut, Salmon, Rockfish)

Geographic Harbor, Katmai coast

Geographic Harbor offers protected saltwater fishing in a stunning volcanic caldera setting within Katmai National Park.

28. Karluk River (Sockeye Salmon, Rainbow Trout, Arctic Char)

Karluk River, Kodiak Island

The Karluk River is one of Kodiak Island's most famous salmon rivers, offering excellent sockeye and rainbow trout fishing.

29. Ayakulik River (Sockeye Salmon, Chinook Salmon, Rainbow Trout)

Ayakulik River, Kodiak Island

The Ayakulik River offers excellent king and sockeye salmon fishing on Kodiak Island.

30. Buskin River (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Pink Salmon)

Buskin River, Kodiak Island

The Buskin River offers good salmon fishing near the city of Kodiak, with easy access from the road system.

31. Old Harbor (Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut)

Old Harbor, Kodiak Island

Old Harbor offers good saltwater and salmon fishing on the southeastern side of Kodiak Island.

32. Larsen Bay (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut)

Larsen Bay, Kodiak Island

Larsen Bay offers excellent sockeye salmon and halibut fishing on the western side of Kodiak Island.

33. Port Heiden (Sockeye Salmon, Coho Salmon, Arctic Char)

Port Heiden, Alaska Peninsula

Port Heiden offers good salmon and Arctic char fishing on the north side of the Alaska Peninsula.

34. Sand Point (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish)

Sand Point, Shumagin Islands

Sand Point offers excellent halibut and salmon fishing in the Shumagin Islands of the Aleutian chain.

35. King Cove (Halibut, Chinook Salmon, Rockfish)

King Cove, Alaska Peninsula

King Cove offers excellent saltwater fishing on the Pacific side of the Alaska Peninsula.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at adfg.alaska.gov.
  • Book a Bristol Bay lodge -- Bristol Bay has numerous world-class lodges offering all-inclusive fishing packages. Book well in advance.
  • Watch for bears -- Southwest Alaska has the highest concentration of brown bears in the world. Always carry bear spray and travel with experienced guides.
  • July is peak sockeye -- July offers the peak of the massive Bristol Bay sockeye salmon runs.
  • Fly-in required -- Most southwest Alaska fishing locations require float plane access. Plan accordingly and budget for air charters.
