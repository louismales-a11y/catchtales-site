Fishing in Panhandle & Northwest Florida

Destin, Panama City, Apalachicola River, Choctawhatchee Bay, and the incredible fishing of Florida's Panhandle.

1. Destin Harbor (Red Snapper, Grouper, Amberjack, Mackerel)

Destin, Okaloosa County

Destin is known as the "World's Luckiest Fishing Village," offering world-class offshore fishing for red snapper, grouper, and amberjack in the Gulf of Mexico.

2. Choctawhatchee Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder, Bluefish)

Choctawhatchee Bay, Okaloosa/Walton counties

Choctawhatchee Bay offers excellent inshore fishing for speckled trout and redfish in a large, productive estuary.

3. Panama City Beach (King Mackerel, Spanish Mackerel, Red Snapper, Grouper)

Panama City Beach, Bay County

Panama City Beach offers excellent offshore and pier fishing in the Gulf of Mexico.

4. Apalachicola River (Largemouth Bass, Striped Bass, Catfish, Sunfish)

Apalachicola River, Franklin County

The Apalachicola River offers excellent bass and catfish fishing in one of Florida's largest river systems.

5. Apalachicola Bay (Oysters, Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

Apalachicola Bay, Franklin County

Apalachicola Bay is famous for its oysters and offers excellent inshore fishing.

6. Lake Seminole (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Catfish, Bream)

Lake Seminole, Jackson County

Lake Seminole is a 37,500-acre reservoir on the Florida-Georgia border offering excellent bass and crappie fishing.

7. Escambia River (Largemouth Bass, Catfish, Bream, Crappie)

Escambia River, Escambia County

The Escambia River offers good blackwater river fishing near Pensacola.

8. Pensacola Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder, Spanish Mackerel)

Pensacola Bay, Escambia County

Pensacola Bay offers excellent inshore fishing in the westernmost part of the Panhandle.

9. Blackwater River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish)

Blackwater River, Santa Rosa County

The Blackwater River offers good freshwater fishing in a scenic blackwater stream.

10. Yellow River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish)

Yellow River, Okaloosa/Santa Rosa counties

The Yellow River offers good bass and bream fishing.

11. St. Andrew Bay (Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder, Sheepshead)

St. Andrew Bay, Bay County

St. Andrew Bay offers excellent inshore fishing near Panama City.

12. Deer Point Lake (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bream, Catfish)

Deer Point Lake, Bay County

Deer Point Lake offers good freshwater fishing near Panama City.

13. Ochlockonee River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish, Crappie)

Ochlockonee River, Wakulla/Leon counties

The Ochlockonee River offers good fishing in the eastern Panhandle.

14. Lake Talquin (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bream, Catfish)

Lake Talquin, Leon/Gadsden counties

Lake Talquin is a 10,000-acre reservoir offering excellent bass and crappie fishing near Tallahassee.

15. St. George Island (Redfish, Speckled Trout, Flounder, Spanish Mackerel)

St. George Island, Franklin County

St. George Island offers excellent surf fishing on the Gulf of Mexico.

16. St. Joseph Bay (Scallops, Speckled Trout, Redfish, Flounder)

St. Joseph Bay, Gulf County

St. Joseph Bay offers excellent inshore fishing and scalloping.

17. Chipola River (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Catfish)

Chipola River, Calhoun County

The Chipola River offers good spring-fed river fishing.

18. Dead Lakes (Largemouth Bass, Crappie, Bream, Catfish)

Dead Lakes, Gulf County

Dead Lakes offers unique fishing in a flooded cypress swamp.

19. Econfina Creek (Largemouth Bass, Bream)

Econfina Creek, Bay County

Econfina Creek offers good spring-fed creek fishing.

20. Appalachicola National Forest Lakes (Largemouth Bass, Bream, Crappie)

Apalachicola National Forest

The numerous lakes in the national forest offer good freshwater fishing.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at myfwc.com. Saltwater fishing requires a separate licence.
  • Book a Destin charter -- Destin has one of the largest charter fleets in the US, targeting red snapper and grouper.
  • Fish the flats -- Choctawhatchee Bay's grass flats offer excellent speckled trout fishing.
  • Red snapper season -- Red snapper season is limited and varies annually. Check current regulations.
  • Spring and fall are best -- April-May and September-October offer the best fishing in the Panhandle.
