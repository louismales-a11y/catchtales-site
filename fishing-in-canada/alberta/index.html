Fishing in Alberta

Click a region to explore top fishing spots, species, and local tips.

Alberta has over 600 lakes and thousands of kilometres of rivers spanning from the Rocky Mountain foothills to the northern boreal forest. The Bow River through Calgary is one of the world's most famous urban trout fisheries, producing trophy brown and rainbow trout. Lake Athabasca in the north offers massive lake trout and northern pike, while the Cold Lake and Lac la Biche regions provide excellent walleye and perch fishing. The foothills rivers - the Crowsnest, Oldman, and Highwood - offer exceptional fly fishing for wild trout in spectacular mountain settings.
