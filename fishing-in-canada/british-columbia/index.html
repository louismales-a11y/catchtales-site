Fishing in British Columbia

Click a region to explore top fishing spots, species, and local tips.

British Columbia has over 20,000 lakes and thousands of kilometres of spectacular Pacific coastline. The province offers world-class salmon fishing — chinook, coho, sockeye, pink, and chum — in coastal rivers like the Skeena, Dean, and Fraser Rivers. Interior lakes including Kootenay, Okanagan, and Shuswap provide excellent rainbow trout, kokanee, and burbot fishing, while the remote lakes of northern BC offer trophy lake trout and Arctic grayling in pristine wilderness settings.
