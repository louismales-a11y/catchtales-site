Top 10 Walleye Fishing Spots in Ontario

Discover the top walleye fishing locations in Ontario, from Lake Erie to Lake of the Woods. Tips on seasons, techniques, and what makes each lake special.

Ontario holds more walleye than any other province or state. With thousands of lakes and rivers, finding good walleye water is easy — finding great walleye water takes a guide. Here are ten of the best walleye fisheries in Ontario.

1. Lake Erie (Western Basin)

Best time: April through October

The western basin of Lake Erie is one of the most productive walleye fisheries in the world. The shallow, nutrient-rich waters support an enormous population of walleye that feeds heavily on emerald shiners and other baitfish. Spring spawning runs in the Maumee and Detroit rivers are legendary. Summer trolling in the open lake produces limits of 3-8 pound fish consistently. The Ontario shoreline near Leamington and Wheatley offers excellent shore and boat fishing.

2. Lake of the Woods

Best time: May through September

Lake of the Woods straddles the Ontario-Manitoba border and offers over 14,000 islands and 65,000 miles of shoreline. The walleye fishing here is world-class, with healthy populations of fish in the 2-6 pound range and trophy fish exceeding 10 pounds. The lake's diverse structure — rocky points, weed beds, sandy flats, and deep basins — provides year-round opportunities. The Winnipeg River system and Whitefish Bay are particularly productive.

3. Rainy Lake

Best time: June through August

Rainy Lake on the Ontario-Minnesota border offers excellent walleye fishing in a pristine, remote setting. The clear, cold water produces walleye with firm, white flesh. The countless islands, rocky reefs, and deep channels provide perfect habitat. Night fishing during the summer can be spectacular, with big walleye moving into shallow reefs to feed.

4. Lake Nipissing

Best time: May through September

Lake Nipissing near North Bay is a massive, shallow lake that's incredibly productive for walleye. The lake has hundreds of islands, rocky shoals, and weedy bays. Walleye are abundant throughout, with fish in the 2-5 pound range common. The lake's stained water means walleye relate closely to structure — target rocky points, weed edges, and current areas near the French River outlet.

5. Bay of Quinte (Lake Ontario)

Best time: May and September-October

The Bay of Quinte is famous for its trophy walleye fishery. The bay's stained water and abundant baitfish produce fast-growing walleye that regularly exceed 10 pounds. Spring and fall are the best times, with fish concentrating in the upper and middle bay. Trolling with crankbaits and worm harnesses is the most productive technique. The fall bite, when walleye are feeding heavily before winter, can be nothing short of spectacular.

6. Lac Seul

Best time: June through August

Lac Seul, located northwest of Dryden, is widely considered one of the best walleye lakes in Ontario — if not all of Canada. The lake's clear water, abundant structure, and healthy walleye population make it a premier destination. Fish in the 3-6 pound range are common, and 10-pound trophies are caught every year. The lake is large and can be challenging to fish without a guide, but the rewards are exceptional.

7. Lake Simcoe

Best time: May through October, and winter ice fishing

Lake Simcoe offers excellent year-round walleye fishing. The lake's shallow, weedy flats and rocky shoals hold good numbers of walleye. Spring and fall are the best open-water seasons, with fish relating to structure in 10-25 feet of water. In winter, Lake Simcoe is one of Ontario's most popular ice fishing destinations for walleye, with productive areas around Georgina Island and Cook's Bay.

8. French River

Best time: June through September

The French River connects Lake Nipissing to Georgian Bay and offers excellent walleye fishing in a beautiful, historic setting. The river's current, deep pools, and rocky structure create ideal walleye habitat. Drift fishing with jigs and minnows along the current edges is very productive. The river also offers protection from wind, making it a great choice on blustery days.

9. Eagle Lake

Best time: June through August

Eagle Lake, located near Dryden in northwestern Ontario, is a clear-water lake known for its excellent walleye and smallmouth bass fishing. The lake's rocky structure, deep basins, and abundant baitfish produce healthy walleye populations. Fish in the 2-5 pound range are common, with occasional trophies over 8 pounds. The lake's clarity means fishing deeper water during the day and shallow water during low-light periods.

10. Ottawa River

Best time: May through October

The Ottawa River offers excellent walleye fishing along its entire length. The river's current, deep channels, and rocky structure provide ideal habitat. Spring and fall are the best times, with fish concentrating in deeper pools and current breaks. The stretch near Pembroke and the area around the Lac Deschenes are particularly productive. The river's size can be intimidating, but focus on structure near current and you'll find fish.

Walleye Fishing Tips

  • Low light is prime time — Walleye are most active during dawn, dusk, and overcast conditions. Plan your fishing around these windows.
  • Use jigs and minnows — A 1/4 to 3/8 oz jig tipped with a minnow is the most consistent walleye presentation across Ontario.
  • Troll crankbaits — For covering water, trolling with deep-diving crankbaits like Reef Runners and Rapala Shad Raps is highly effective on larger lakes.
  • Check the solunar forecast — Walleye feeding activity is strongly influenced by moon phases. Use the CatchTales app's built-in solunar predictions to plan your trips.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Purchase online at ontario.ca/fishing.
