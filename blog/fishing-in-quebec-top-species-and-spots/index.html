Fishing in Quebec: Top Species and Best Spots

Quebec is a vast fishing province — from the Atlantic salmon rivers of the Gaspe to the brook trout and walleye of the north, and the bass and pike of the St. Lawrence.

Quebec is Canada's largest province by area, and its fishing opportunities are equally expansive. The province spans from the warm waters of the St. Lawrence River Valley to the Arctic tundra of Nunavik. Quebec is home to world-renowned Atlantic salmon rivers, exceptional brook trout fishing, and productive walleye, pike, and bass waters. Here are the top species and where to find them.

1. Gaspe Peninsula (Atlantic Salmon)

Best time: June through September

The Gaspe Peninsula is one of the last great strongholds of wild Atlantic salmon in North America. Rivers like the Matapedia, Cascapedia, Bonaventure, and Restigouche are world-famous for their salmon fishing. These rivers offer spectacular fly fishing in pristine settings, with salmon averaging 8-15 pounds and trophies over 30 pounds. Most salmon fishing on Gaspe rivers is catch-and-release, operated through exclusive lodges and outfitters. The season runs from June through September, with the best fishing typically in July and August.

2. St. Lawrence River (Smallmouth Bass, Walleye & Muskie)

Best time: May through September

The St. Lawrence River offers exceptional fishing for smallmouth bass, walleye, and muskie. The river's clear, deep water and abundant structure create ideal habitat. The section from Montreal to Lake St. Francis is particularly productive for bass, with fish in the 2-4 pound range common. Walleye fishing is excellent near the mouths of tributaries and around structure. The river also supports a trophy muskie fishery, with fish over 50 inches possible. Access points include the Montreal area, Sorel, and the Lac St-Francis region.

3. Lac Saint-Jean (Walleye, Northern Pike & Ouananiche)

Best time: May through September

Lac Saint-Jean, north of Quebec City, is one of Quebec's premier freshwater fisheries. The lake is famous for its walleye (doré) population, with fish in the 2-5 pound range common. Northern pike are abundant in the weedy bays and river mouths. The lake is also home to ouananiche, a landlocked Atlantic salmon that provides exceptional sport on light tackle. The Peribonka and Mistassini rivers, which flow into the lake, offer excellent salmon and brook trout fishing.

4. Outaouais Region (Walleye, Bass & Pike)

Best time: May through September

The Outaouais (Ottawa River) region offers excellent fishing for walleye, smallmouth bass, and northern pike. The Ottawa River, which forms the boundary between Quebec and Ontario, holds healthy populations of all three species. The Gatineau River and Lac Poisson Blanc are also productive. The region is popular with anglers from both Ottawa and Montreal, offering easy access and good infrastructure. The river's rapids, deep pools, and rocky structure provide diverse fishing opportunities.

5. Lac Memphremagog (Smallmouth Bass & Lake Trout)

Best time: May through September

Lac Memphremagog, in the Eastern Townships, offers excellent fishing for smallmouth bass and lake trout. The lake is deep, clear, and has excellent water quality. Smallmouth bass fishing is outstanding, with fish in the 2-4 pound range common around the rocky shoals and islands. Lake trout are found in the deeper basins, with fish in the 5-12 pound range. The lake is easily accessible from Montreal and is a popular destination for weekend anglers. Ice fishing for lake trout is also popular in winter.

6. Cote-Nord (Atlantic Salmon & Brook Trout)

Best time: June through August

The Cote-Nord (North Shore) region of the St. Lawrence River offers exceptional fishing for Atlantic salmon and brook trout. Rivers like the Moisie, Magpie, and Saint-Jean are world-famous for their salmon runs, offering spectacular fly fishing in remote settings. The region's countless cold streams and lakes hold native brook trout that can reach impressive sizes. The Cote-Nord is accessible via Route 138, with many rivers accessible from the highway. Lodges and outfitters operate on most major rivers.

7. Lac Mistassini (Lake Trout & Walleye)

Best time: June through August

Lac Mistassini, the largest natural lake in Quebec, is a remote wilderness destination offering exceptional lake trout and walleye fishing. The lake is deep, clear, and incredibly productive, with lake trout regularly exceeding 20 pounds. Walleye are abundant in the shallower basins. The lake is fly-in only, accessed through lodges that operate on its shores. The fishing season is short — typically June through August — but the quality is outstanding. This is a true bucket-list destination for serious anglers.

8. Abitibi-Temiscamingue (Walleye & Northern Pike)

Best time: May through September

The Abitibi-Temiscamingue region in western Quebec offers excellent fishing for walleye and northern pike. The region's many lakes and rivers, including Lac Abitibi, Lac Simard, and the Harricana River, hold healthy fish populations. Walleye in the 2-4 pound range are common, and pike fishing is excellent in the weedy bays. The region is accessible by road and has numerous outfitters, lodges, and campgrounds. The fishing is less pressured than in southern Quebec, offering a true wilderness experience.

9. Nunavik & Ungava Bay (Arctic Char & Brook Trout)

Best time: July through August

Nunavik, the northernmost region of Quebec, offers some of the most remote and pristine fishing in North America. The rivers flowing into Ungava Bay and Hudson Bay hold exceptional populations of Arctic char and brook trout. Arctic char in the region average 5-10 pounds, with trophies over 15 pounds possible. The brook trout fishing is world-class, with fish exceeding 5 pounds in many rivers. Access is by fly-in only, with lodges operating on the major rivers. This is the ultimate wilderness fishing destination.

10. Laurentians & Lanaudiere (Brook Trout & Lake Trout)

Best time: May through September

The Laurentians and Lanaudiere regions, just north of Montreal, offer excellent trout fishing within easy reach of the city. The region's countless lakes and streams hold brook trout, lake trout, and rainbow trout. Many lakes are stocked and managed for quality fishing. Popular destinations include Lac des Sables, Lac Archambault, and the Rouge River. The region is well-equipped with resorts, outfitters, and public access points, making it a favourite for family fishing trips.

Fishing Tips for Quebec

  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need a Quebec fishing licence. Buy online at peche.mffp.gouv.qc.ca. Non-residents require a separate licence.
  • Know the salmon rivers — Quebec's Atlantic salmon rivers are strictly managed. Most are catch-and-release only. You need a separate salmon licence and must book through a registered outfitter on many rivers.
  • Learn the seasons — Fishing seasons vary by zone and species in Quebec. The general open-water season runs from May through September, but many waters open earlier or close later. Always check current regulations.
  • Bring insect repellent — Northern Quebec has intense mosquito and black fly populations in early summer. A head net and high-DEET repellent are essential gear.
  • Respect the outfitter system — Much of Quebec's prime fishing territory is managed by pourvoiries (outfitters) who control access to vast territories. Booking through an outfitter is often the only way to access remote waters.
