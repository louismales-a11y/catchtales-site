Best Lures for Bass Fishing in Canada and the United States

A complete guide to the best lures for bass fishing in North America, including topwater, spinnerbaits, crankbaits, jigs, and soft plastics for every situation.

Choosing the right lure can make the difference between a great day on the water and a frustrating one. Here's a guide to the most effective bass lures for waters across Canada and the United States.

Topwater Lures

Topwater fishing produces some of the most exciting strikes in bass fishing.

Whopper Plopper

The Whopper Plopper has become a modern classic. Its rear propeller creates a surface disturbance that drives bass crazy. Best used in calm water around weed lines, docks, and points. Excellent for both largemouth and smallmouth.

Buzzbait

A classic topwater that creates a buzzing sound as it moves across the surface. Works well in all conditions but particularly in stained or murky water. Fish it fast across weed beds and along shorelines.

Frog (Hollow Body)

Perfect for fishing heavy cover — lily pads, matted vegetation, and thick weeds. The hollow body frog walks across the thickest cover without snagging. When a bass explodes through the weeds to hit it, hold on.

Spinnerbaits

Spinnerbaits are versatile and effective in a wide range of conditions.

Standard Willow Leaf Spinnerbait

Great for covering water. The flashing blades attract bass from a distance. Use a double willow setup in clear water for smallmouth, or a Colorado blade in stained water for largemouth.

Crankbaits

Crankbaits are excellent for searching water and covering ground quickly.

Square Bill Crankbait

Perfect for fishing around wood, rocks, and shallow cover. The square bill deflects off structure and triggers reaction strikes. Best in spring and fall in 2-6 feet of water.

Deep Diving Crankbait

For reaching bass in deeper water (8-20 feet). Use around points, humps, and deep weed lines. Crankbaits that run 10-15 feet are essential for summer fishing on larger lakes.

Jigs

Jigs are the ultimate big bass bait. They work year-round in almost any condition.

Football Jig

Designed for fishing rocky bottoms and deep structure. The football-shaped head doesn't get stuck in rocks. Pair with a crawfish trailer for an incredibly effective presentation.

Flipping Jig

Built for heavy cover — thick weeds, laydowns, docks. The streamlined head and heavy gauge hook punch through cover. Use with a chunk-style trailer.

Soft Plastics

Soft plastics account for more big bass than any other lure category.

Plastic Worm (Texas Rig)

The most versatile bass lure ever created. Fish it weightless in shallow cover, or with a bullet weight for deeper water. Green pumpkin, watermelon, and black/blue are reliable colours across North America.

Tube Jig

The number one smallmouth bass lure. Fish it on a tube jig head around rocks, gravel, and sand. Drag it slowly across the bottom or hop it through deeper water.

Drop-Shot Rig

Deadly for finicky bass in clear, deep water. The weight is on the bottom with the hook tied above it. The bait stays suspended and moves naturally in the current.

Lure Selection by Season

  • Spring (pre-spawn/spawn) — Jerkbaits, spinnerbaits, jigs, soft plastics around shallow cover
  • Summer — Deep crankbaits, drop-shot, football jigs, frogs in early morning/evening
  • Fall — Square bill crankbaits, spinnerbaits, Whopper Ploppers, jerkbaits
  • Winter — Slow presentations: jigs, drop-shot, blade baits in deeper water
