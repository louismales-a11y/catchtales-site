Best Fishing Apps for Canada and the United States in 2026

A review of the best fishing apps for anglers in Canada and the United States, including catch logging, fish identification, weather, maps, and more.

There are dozens of fishing apps on the market, but only a few are truly designed for Canadian and US waters. Here's a comparison of the best fishing apps available in 2026.

1. CatchTales

Platform: Android | Price: Free (with Pro upgrade)

CatchTales is the most comprehensive fishing companion app designed specifically for Canada and the United States. It combines catch logging, fish identification (305 species), interactive maps with nautical charts and weather radar, solunar predictions, voice controls, tackle tracking, and community features — all in one beautifully designed app.

Key Features:

  • 305-species fish ID guide — Every freshwater species found in Canada and the US
  • Interactive maps — Nautical charts, depth contours, weather radar overlays
  • Voice controls — Hands-free catch logging
  • Solunar predictions — Best fishing times for your exact location
  • Weather forecasts — Real-time conditions and 5-day forecast
  • Tackle box management — Track your lures and gear
  • Cloud sync — Back up your data and sync across devices
  • Brag Board — Share your catches with the community
  • Dual skin system — Dream underwater mode or Classic theme
  • Multi-language — English, French, Spanish, German, Ukrainian

Best for: Anglers who want an all-in-one solution for logging, planning, and identifying fish across Canada and the US.

2. Fishbrain

Platform: iOS, Android | Price: Free with premium subscription

Fishbrain is the largest social fishing app with a focus on catch sharing and social features. It has a large user base, which means plenty of catch data and photos to browse. Premium features include advanced weather forecasts and bait recommendations.

Key Features:

  • Social feed of catches from other anglers
  • Species identification
  • Weather and solunar forecasts
  • Fishing spots map
  • Premium subscription for advanced features ($6-10/month)

Limitations:

  • Subscription model can be expensive over time
  • Less focused on Canadian waters than US
  • No voice controls
  • No offline maps
  • No nautical chart overlays

3. Fishidy

Platform: Web, iOS, Android | Price: Free with premium subscription

Fishidy offers detailed lake maps and fishing reports. It's strong on lake information but weaker on catch logging features.

Key Features:

  • Detailed lake maps with depth contours
  • Fishing reports and hot spot markers
  • Weather and solunar data
  • Premium subscription for full access ($4-8/month)

Limitations:

  • Subscription model
  • Limited Canadian lake data compared to US
  • Basic catch logging with limited fields
  • No fish identification guide
  • No voice controls

4. Anglers Logbook

Platform: iOS, Android | Price: Free with premium upgrade

Anglers Logbook focuses on catch logging with detailed data fields. It's a solid option for serious loggers but lacks the maps, weather, and fish ID features of more comprehensive apps.

Key Features:

  • Detailed catch logging with many data fields
  • Photo storage
  • Basic statistics
  • Premium upgrade for unlimited catches ($4 one-time)

Limitations:

  • No fish identification guide
  • No interactive maps or weather overlays
  • No voice controls
  • Basic interface design
  • No community features

5. iFish (Canada)

Platform: iOS, Android | Price: Free

iFish is a Canadian-focused fishing app that provides fishing forecasts and solunar data for Canadian waters.

Key Features:

  • Canadian fishing forecasts
  • Solunar predictions
  • Basic catch logging

Limitations:

  • No fish identification guide
  • No interactive maps
  • No voice controls
  • Limited to Canada only (no US coverage)
  • Basic interface

Comparison Summary

For anglers who want a comprehensive, all-in-one fishing app that works across both Canada and the United States, CatchTales offers the best combination of features, including fish identification, interactive maps, weather, solunar predictions, voice controls, and community features — all with a one-time Pro purchase and no ongoing subscription fees.
